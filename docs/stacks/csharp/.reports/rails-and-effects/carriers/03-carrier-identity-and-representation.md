# Carrier Identity And Representation

[PHYSICAL_SHAPE_DIVERGENCE]:
- The carriers do not share a representation kind, and the kind dictates copy, allocation, and identity semantics. The absence carrier is a `readonly struct` with two fields (`A? Value`, `bool isSome`) — value-copied, never null as a reference, zero-allocation, and `default` is a valid `None`. The result carrier is an `abstract class` with two sealed nested subclasses (`Succ(A)`, `Fail(Error)`) declared with primary constructors — a reference type, heap-allocated per case, not a record. The accumulating and general two-sided carriers are `abstract record` with sealed nested record cases (`Success`/`Fail`, `Right`/`Left`) — reference types whose case payload participates in the compiler-synthesized record equality. The effect carriers are `record`s wrapping a delegate-bearing inner effect; their synthesized equality compares the wrapped function, which is identity-meaningless.
- The struct/class split is load-bearing for `default(T)`: `default` of the absence carrier is a usable `None`; `default` of the result/accumulating/general carriers is a null reference, and any operation on it throws `NullReferenceException` before the rail ever sees it. A field, array slot, or uninitialized local typed as a class-shaped carrier is a latent null; the struct-shaped carrier is total over `default`.
- One case family carries a span projection of arity zero-or-one: `SuccSpan()`/`FailSpan()` return a `ReadOnlySpan<A>`/`ReadOnlySpan<Error>` of length one on the matching case and empty on the other. The carrier is therefore a zero-or-one foldable directly over a stack span, and the absence carrier projects to four collection carriers (`Arr`, `Lst`, `Seq`, `IEnumerable`) — every result/absence value is a bounded foldable whose `Fold`/`Traverse`/LINQ-comprehension behavior follows from a one-element-or-empty sequence, not from a special case.

[DEFAULT_EQUALITY_IS_REFLECTION_BACKED]:
- Default structural equality on a payload-typed carrier routes through a per-type strategy struct that falls through to a reflective trait probe, not through `EqualityComparer<A>.Default`. The strategy struct's `Equals` delegates to a resolver whose static constructor runs once per closed payload type: it searches the loaded assemblies for a convention-named trait implementation (`Eq` + the type's name) by string, and on a hit binds that trait's `static bool Equals(A,A)` / `static int GetHashCode(A)` as a cached `MethodInfo` invocation; on a miss it records a resolution error and falls back to `EqualityComparer<A>.Default`.

```csharp
// per-type static-ctor probe, run once on first equality touch of A
Type? trait = Resolver.Find(typeof(A), "Eq");          // string-convention search across assemblies
MethodInfo? eq = Resolver.Method(trait, "Equals", typeof(A), typeof(A));
EqualsFunc = (x, y) => ((bool?)eq.Invoke(null, [x, y])) // reflective invoke per comparison
                       ?? throw new InvalidOperationException();
```

- Two consequences follow directly from the `MethodInfo.Invoke` round-trip. First, equality of a carrier over a rich payload is a reflective call per comparison, not a devirtualized one — the cost is invisible at the call site (`a == b`) and material in hot equality paths, sets, and dictionary probes. Second, the bound delegate throws when the resolved trait method returns null, so a payload whose discovered trait `Equals` can return a null boxed result surfaces an `InvalidOperationException` from inside the carrier's `==`, far from the comparison's apparent source. The throw is a property of the discovered trait, not of the carrier, and it is reachable only once the static ctor has bound a real trait method rather than the fallback comparer.
- The escape is to never rely on default carrier equality over a payload with reflective trait ambiguity: compare on a projected scalar discriminant, or pass an explicit equality witness to the comparison surface. The carriers expose a typeclass-keyed overload alongside the default one — `Equals<EqA>(other) where EqA : Eq<A>` and `CompareTo<OrdA>(other) where OrdA : Ord<A>` on the absence and result carriers, two-witness `Equals<EqF,EqA>` / `CompareTo<OrdF,OrdA>` on the two-sided carriers — which resolves the strategy at the type level with zero reflection and zero ambiguity. The witness form is the deterministic equality; the zero-argument form is the convenience that pays the resolver.

[FAILURE_CASE_IDENTITY_ASYMMETRY]:
- The result carrier and the accumulating/general carriers disagree on whether the failure payload participates in identity, and the disagreement is exact. On the result carrier, the failure case's `Equals` returns true for any other failure regardless of error content, and its `GetHashCode` returns a constant — every failure is one equivalence class, the error is identity-transparent. On the accumulating and general carriers, the failure/left case hashes and compares its payload through the payload's own trait, so two failures are equal only when their carried values are. The same logical "this failed" therefore collapses to one bucket under the result carrier and fans out under the accumulating carrier.
- This decides container behavior. A set or dictionary keyed on the result carrier coalesces all failures into a single key — distinct errors overwrite — which is correct for "did it fail" deduplication and wrong for "which failures occurred". The accumulating carrier preserves each distinct failure as a distinct key. Choosing the carrier for a keyed collection is choosing whether failure detail survives the hash, and the choice is irreversible inside the container.

[TOTAL_ORDER_PLACES_FAILURE_FIRST]:
- Every two-case carrier defines a total order in which the failure side sorts strictly before the success side, uniformly: the failure/left/absence case returns a negative comparison against any success/right/present value, and the success side returns positive against any failure. Within a side, ordering delegates to the payload's order witness. Sorting a sequence of result/either/validation/option values therefore groups all failures ahead of all successes with no predicate, and a min over the sequence is "the first failure if any, else the least success" — the order is a built-in partition, not an accident of declaration.
- The absence carrier's order is the degenerate case of the same law: `None` sorts before any `Some`, `None` compares equal to `None`, and `None` hashes to zero. A sorted structure of optionals front-loads every absence; a min-priority queue of optionals dequeues absences first.

[MIXED_OPERAND_COMPARISON_ELIDES_LIFT]:
- The two-case carriers carry comparison and equality operators against the bare payload and against the lift literals, not only against another carrier. The result carrier compares directly against `A` (`carrier < value`, `value >= carrier`), the two-sided carriers compare and equate against the success-side payload, the success lift literal, and the failure lift literal. A predicate over a rail value against a constant needs no `Pure`/`Succ` wrap on the constant and no unwrap on the carrier — the operator absorbs the lift on the literal side and the discriminant on the carrier side. A success compares to its payload by the payload's order; a failure compares to any payload as strictly-less, so the mixed-operand comparison silently inherits the failure-first total order.

[EFFECT_CARRIERS_ARE_OPAQUE]:
- The effect carriers expose no meaningful identity. They are records over a wrapped delegate (a reader-over-IO inner effect), so the synthesized `Equals`/`GetHashCode` compare the captured function reference, never the effect's eventual result — two effects that compute the same value are unequal, and the same effect value is equal only to itself. There is no order, no structural projection, and no serialization. An effect is a description, and a description has reference identity only; using one as a dictionary key, in a set, or in a `Distinct` keys on closure identity, never on behavior. Equality of effects is a category error the type system permits because the record machinery generates it unconditionally; the correct comparison is over the run result inside the rail, never over the effect value.

[SERIALIZATION_SURFACE_IS_NON_UNIFORM]:
- Only the absence carrier carries a hand-written binary serialization contract: it implements the serialization interface, writes a presence flag plus the bound value, and reconstructs from the flag — round-tripping `None` as a flag with no payload and `Some` as flag-plus-value. The result, accumulating, and general carriers implement no such contract; the effect carriers cannot be serialized at all. A wire or persistence layer that assumes a uniform carrier serialization is wrong for three of the families — the discriminated reference carriers must be projected to a wire-shaped union or to the absence carrier before crossing a boundary, and the effect carriers must be run to a value first. The absence carrier is the only one that travels as-is, and only through the legacy binary contract, not through a modern codec it does not advertise.

[LIFT_LITERALS_ARE_CARRIER_AGNOSTIC_HUBS]:
- The success and failure lift literals are `readonly record struct` single-field values whose entire purpose is deferred carrier selection. The success literal exposes a `Bind` overloaded across roughly fifteen destination carriers (result, either, the effect families, reader, state, and the kind-generic `K<M,B> where M : Monad<M>`), plus direct projections to the absence, result, and either carriers; the failure literal projects to either and maps its payload. A value or error written once as a lift literal commits to no carrier until the surrounding bind or the destination type fixes one — the literal is the polymorphic ingress point that lets a single `from x in Pure(v)` clause flow into whichever carrier the comprehension's later clauses demand, with the operator overloads and implicit conversions on each carrier accepting the literal without an explicit constructor. The lift literal is therefore the dispatch hub that replaces per-carrier construction at the head of a pipeline.
