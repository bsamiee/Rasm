Binding: the leader composes identity and lifecycle — advancement edits the status token alone, and supersession names the successor id in the superseded record's own leader, so the old record always points forward. `[PROPOSED]` is a question, never a resting state: it advances to `[ACCEPTED]` or `[REJECTED]`, or the record drops. `Rejected` repeats per invalidated branch that stays useful, carrying the losing argument and the condition that reopens it; a field deciding nothing for a record is omitted, never filled. `Premises` marks draw from the `[MARK]` vocabulary: `[FACT]` anchors the observed forcing condition that selected the ruling, `[ASSUMPTION]` carries its invalidating condition, `[INFERENCE]` names the premises it rests on; a record with no unproven premise omits the field.
