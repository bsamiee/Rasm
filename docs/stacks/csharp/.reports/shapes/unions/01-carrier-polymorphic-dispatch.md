# Carrier-Polymorphic Union Dispatch Over Higher-Kinded Rails

[CARRIER_ABSTRACTION_LAW]:
- A total dispatch whose arms return a concrete rail (`Fin<A>`, `Validation<E,A>`, `Eff<A>`) hard-binds the fold to one failure semantics; lifting the arm result to `K<F, A>` and constraining `F` to a trait makes the dispatch carrier-polymorphic — one union, one arm set, and the failure discipline becomes a type-argument choice at the fold's call boundary, not a rewrite of the arms.
- The trait the fold demands selects exactly the semantics it is allowed to use: `Applicative<F>` permits combining independent child results but forbids inspecting one to decide the next; `Monad<F>` adds the sequential dependency at the cost of losing parallel accumulation; `Choice<F>`/`Alternative<F>` add per-arm fallback; `Fallible<E,F>` adds carrier-agnostic rejection and recovery. The weakest sufficient trait is the correct constraint — demand `Monad` and the same fold can no longer be specialized to an accumulating carrier.
- One dispatch surface therefore spans both axes that normally fork a result family into parallel types: fail-fast versus accumulate is `traverseM` versus `traverse`, and recoverable versus terminal is `Choice`-armed versus plain — none of which touches the union, its cases, or its `Switch` arms.

```csharp
public static K<F, B> Fold<F, B>(this Node source, Func<Leaf, K<F, B>> onLeaf, Func<B, B, B> combine)
    where F : Applicative<F> =>
    source.Switch<Func<Node, K<F, B>>, K<F, B>>(
        onLeaf,
        leaf:   static (f, l)  => f(l),
        branch: static (f, br) => Applicative.apply((br.Left.Fold(f, combine), br.Right.Fold(f, combine)), combine));
```

[ARM_AS_KLEISLI_POINT]:
- Each non-abstract case is the natural Kleisli point `A -> K<F, B>`: the case payload is the `A`, the arm body is the lift into `F`, and the generated total `Switch` is the only place the family's continuation is selected — every arm produces a rail value, the dispatcher never sees a bare value, and the carrier's `Bind`/`Apply` sequences the children.
- A recursive case's arm is itself a traversal, not a hand-written sequencer: the arm collects child folds as `K<F, B>` values and combines them through the carrier's applicative `apply` over a tuple, so the failure policy of the combine is inherited from `F`, never spelled in the arm. Switching the same fold from short-circuit to accumulate is purely a change of which trait `F` satisfies at the call site.
- The state-threaded `Switch<TState, TResult>` and the carrier are orthogonal channels: `TState` rides the environment (bindings, depth budget, configuration) and `TResult` is instantiated to `K<F, B>`; the environment never leaks into the failure rail and the rail never leaks into the environment, so a reader-shaped fold and a fallible fold compose without nesting two monads when the environment is plain data.

[APPLICATIVE_VS_MONADIC_TRAVERSAL]:
- The library exposes two structurally distinct sequencers over a traversable interior: `traverse` requires `F : Applicative<F>` and visits every position, producing all effects before combining; `traverseM` requires `M : Monad<M>` and threads sequentially so the first failure aborts the remainder. A union whose cases carry child collections picks one by the trait it constrains its fold against — there is no flag, the constraint is the policy.
- `sequence`/`sequenceM` are the same split for an already-effectful interior `K<T, K<F, A>>`, flipping it to `K<F, K<T, A>>`; a union case holding `Seq<K<F, Child>>` sequences in one combinator rather than folding by hand, and the accumulate-versus-abort choice again rides the trait, not the body.
- Applicative traversal over a fallible carrier whose failure type is a monoid accumulates every error in one pass; over a carrier whose failure is not a monoid it still type-checks but degenerates to first-error semantics — the accumulation guarantee lives in the failure type's algebra, not in the traversal verb, so a fold that must accumulate has to constrain the failure type, not merely call the applicative form.

[ACCUMULATING_FAILURE_ALGEBRA]:
- The accumulating carrier resolves its failure-combining operation from the failure type's monoid instance at run time, not at compile time: an applicative `Apply` that fails on both sides looks up the failure type's `Monoid`/`Semigroup` instance reflectively and throws a type-load fault when the chosen failure type implements neither. A union fold specialized to the accumulating carrier with a non-monoidal failure type compiles clean and faults only when two arms actually fail in one traversal — the error surfaces at the first multi-failure input, never at the call site.
- This makes the failure type a load-bearing part of a carrier-polymorphic fold's contract: the same fold body is safe under a fail-fast carrier with any error type and unsafe under the accumulating carrier unless the error type carries a monoid; the fold's `where` clause cannot express "and the failure type accumulates," so the obligation is discharged at the carrier-selection site and is invisible to the arms.
- Per-case validation that must report every offending case in one pass is therefore the accumulating carrier with a monoidal failure, applied through the applicative `apply` tuple over the children; per-case validation that must stop at the first bad case is the same arms under a monadic carrier — one union, one fold, two boundary specializations.

[CHOICE_RECOVERY_ARMS]:
- A carrier satisfying `Choice<F>` admits `choose(fa, fb)` as left-biased fallback and `Alternative<F>` admits `choice(params ReadOnlySpan<K<F, A>>)` as ordered first-success over a span of candidates; a union arm that has several admissible interpretations of one case expresses them as an ordered candidate span and lets the carrier pick the first that succeeds, with the empty carrier supplying the terminal failure — no arm enumerates the fallback ladder imperatively.
- Carrier-agnostic rejection is `Fallible.fail<E, F, A>(error)` / `Fallible.error<F, A>(error)`, which mint a failed `K<F, A>` without naming the concrete carrier; a union arm that rejects its own payload returns the carrier-agnostic failure and stays portable across every `F` the fold is later specialized to, so the rejecting arm is written once and never re-specialized when the carrier changes.
- Two recovery scopes exist and must not be conflated: per-case alternation (`choose`/`choice` inside one arm) recovers a single case's interpretation, while whole-fold recovery wraps the dispatch result — one `K<F, B>` — in a `Fallible` catch to downgrade or default the entire subtree; the arm owns local ambiguity, the fold boundary owns terminal policy, and neither leaks the concrete carrier into the union.

[REF_STRUCT_RESULT_CHANNEL]:
- The func-form generated `Switch<TState, TResult>` annotates both `TState` and `TResult` with the ref-struct anti-constraint, so the result slot accepts a stack-only carrier; a span-shaped or stack-allocated accumulator can be the `TResult` and flow through dispatch without boxing, which a `K<F, A>` heap carrier cannot — the two result disciplines are mutually exclusive on one fold and chosen by whether the accumulation must escape the stack.
- The eager `Map` is disqualified as a carrier producer for any non-pure rail: every arm value is constructed before the discriminant selects one, so task-shaped or effect-executing arms run all branches and abandon the losers; only cold carriers — effect descriptions not yet run, already-settled rail values, and constant projections — are safe `Map` arms, and any arm that allocates or executes belongs in the func `Switch` where exactly one arm runs.
- The partial-map arm carrier is a readonly ref struct that arm expressions convert to at evaluation, which forbids an `await` to the right of an already-evaluated arm in one `MapPartially` call; a carrier-polymorphic fold that must await hoists the awaited carrier into a local before the dispatch, and the func `Switch` forms — whose arguments are heap delegates — carry no such restriction.

[DISPATCH_GRANULARITY_AS_TRAVERSAL_SHAPE]:
- A stop-at overload folds a case subtree behind one abstract handler, and that coarse handler is the natural seam to re-enter a different carrier: the outer fold can run under a monadic carrier for short-circuit while the stopped subtree re-dispatches on its own nested surface under an accumulating carrier, so blast-radius granularity and failure-accumulation granularity are configured at the same boundary and a case added inside the subtree breaks exactly the inner fold's totality.
- Because the case list is part of every dispatch method's signature, a carrier-polymorphic fold's arm set is re-signatured on case addition and every specialization at every carrier breaks at compile time in lockstep — the carrier abstraction widens reuse without widening the silent-failure surface, since totality is still proven per dispatch site regardless of which `F` it was specialized to.
- Partial overloads route a new case to `@default` silently and therefore poison the carrier-polymorphic guarantee precisely where it is most valuable; reserve partial dispatch for genuinely total defaults and keep the fold on the total forms so that adding a case forces every carrier specialization through the compile-time sweep rather than letting one carrier silently absorb the new case.

[SEEDING_AND_PURE_LIFT]:
- The carrier-agnostic injection of a plain value is the carrier's `Pure` through the trait, so a fold lifts a constant or never-failing arm without naming `F`; the arm writes the pure lift rather than a concrete success constructor, which both keeps it portable across every specialization and keeps the arm set uniform `A -> K<F, B>` so the dispatcher's result type is single and the carrier's combine is well-typed — a bare-value arm beside rail-valued arms would force a `TResult` the carrier cannot combine.
- The applicative tuple combinator `apply((K<F,A>, K<F,B>), Func<A,B,R>)` is the canonical two-child combine inside a recursive arm: it evaluates both child folds, combines successes with the pure function, and merges failures by the carrier's own policy — the arm names the pure combine and nothing about success-versus-failure, so one arm body is correct under fail-fast and accumulate alike, and the only edit that flips the whole fold between them is the trait its `where` clause demands.
