[ORDERED_INDEX_COLLAPSE]: collapse every linear-array re-sort and linear-scan in the folder onto the admitted `effect` sorted-collection owners — the read-model tier's indices are ordered structures, never re-sorted arrays.
- The `evidence/evidence-correlation` `byHlc` fold re-sorts a whole `ReadonlyArray` on every arrived row (`[...rows, e].sort(byHlc)`, O(n log n) per row — the C# floor's named anti-pattern), the `crdt-semilattice` RGA arm linear-scans its `cells` array per insert and delete, and the `frontier-gc` antichain plus the version-vector stability meet have no ordered index. - Unlocks a bounded-cost evidence timeline a long session never degrades, an RGA whose insert and tombstone are logarithmic rather than linear, and a frontier whose advance reads the `headOption`/`lastOption` extremes directly — the ordered-structure capability the read-model tier is defined by, not a convenience.
- Draws on `effect` `RedBlackTree` (the `greaterThanEqual`/`at`/`insert`/`removeFirst` range-cursor owner the timeline since-T window needs), `SortedMap` (the in-order keyed map for the RGA dense order), and `SortedSet` (the cursor meet) — the admitted persistent ordered owners the folder currently bypasses — and the `Order.combine` HLC comparator already composed in `evidence`; the gap is that the folder folds ordered domains into unordered arrays and re-derives the order per row, the flat-code surface sprawl the density floor forbids.
