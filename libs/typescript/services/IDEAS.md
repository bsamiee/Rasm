# [SERVICES_IDEAS]

The forward pool of higher-order concepts for the node tier and its hosting IaC. Each open card is a bracketed slug leader plus the capability, what it unlocks, and the gap or modern technique it draws on; an idea drives one or more `TASKLOG.md` tasks. ## [1]-[OPEN]

[BACKEND_RUNTIME_SLO]
- A net-new `observability/` sub-domain owning the closed `Objective` vocabulary (`latency`/`availability`/`saturation`/`freshness`), the multi-window multi-burn-rate fast/slow fold over the `@effect/sql-pg`-stored signal series, the `AlertRoute` `Data.TaggedEnum` over the `eventing/transactional-outbox` `DeliverySink` axis, and the `@effect/opentelemetry` `Metrics`/`Tracer` reader that drives them — the node analog of the browser `platform/web-vitals/performance-budget`, distinct from the deploy-time `provisioning/contract` `ObservabilityStack.emit` collector.
- Operational truth the durable tier structurally lacks: an objective breach is one fold over the recorded series rather than a per-service threshold check, the burn-rate alert fires through the existing outbox delivery sinks rather than a parallel notification path, and a new objective is one vocabulary row. The cluster signal source is the `@effect/cluster` `ClusterMetrics` five-gauge surface (`entities`/`singletons`/`runners`/`runnersHealthy`/`shards`), read off the runtime, never a re-derived shard scrape.
- Draws on the Google SRE multi-window multi-burn-rate alerting algebra and the `@effect/opentelemetry` reader surface; the node-side consumer of the cross-`libs/` `ONE_HEALTH_DEGRADATION_WIRE`/`ONE_DISTRIBUTED_TRACE` C#-minted health and trace frames, decoding the level and trace context off the wire rather than re-minting.

[RUNTIME_SECRET_GOVERNANCE]
- A net-new `secrets/` sub-domain owning the one `SecretStore` `Effect.Service` over the `ConfigProvider` boundary, the `SecretRef` `Data.TaggedEnum` over the provider axis (ESC env / Doppler / static config admitted today; an AWS Secrets Manager arm gated on `@aws-sdk/client-secrets-manager` admission, not yet in the workspace catalog), the TTL-leased cache with rotation-driven `SubscriptionRef` invalidation, and the audit receipt every resolution emits — lifting the deploy-time-only secrets concern to a runtime resolver every node owner reads.
- One runtime secrets owner the whole node tier reads: a rotation invalidates the lease and the dependent owners re-resolve through the `SubscriptionRef.changes` edge rather than a process restart, a new secret-backed capability is one `SecretRef` row, and the deploy-time and runtime resolution meet at the one `ConfigProvider` boundary.
- Draws on the Effect `ConfigProvider`/`Config.redacted` runtime-config law and the `@pulumi/esc-sdk` `EscApi.openAndReadEnvironment` already consumed deploy-time; the runtime owners (`authn/verifier` keys, the `durable-execution/ai-activity` provider keys, the `persistence/store-boundary` connection string, the object-store credentials) reach for `Config` ad hoc today with no shared lease/rotation owner.

[OBJECT_STORE_TIER]
- A net-new `object-store/` sub-domain owning the one `ObjectStore` `Effect.Service` over the `@effect-aws/client-s3` `S3Service` layer, the `ObjectKey` content-addressed brand (the `interchange` `XxHash128` content key), the `PresignGrant` `Data.TaggedEnum` over the get/put/multipart verbs, and the lifecycle/expiry policy rows — collapsing the S3 presign and the asset-export codec fan-out (`exceljs`/`papaparse`/`jspdf`/`jszip`/`sharp`) out of `persistence/work-and-signals` into one streaming storage tier.
- One blob tier every node owner composes: a content-keyed artifact stored by the durable tier is fetched by the key the browser worker digests, the asset-export codecs become one streaming `ObjectStore.put`, and a presign grant is one policy row gated by the `persistence/tenancy` GUC, never a raw SDK call per surface.
- Draws on the `@effect-aws/client-s3` `S3Service`-layer-as-service binding (beyond the raw `@aws-sdk/client-s3`), the cross-`libs/` `ONE_CONTENT_IDENTITY` seed the object key reuses, and the streaming multipart boundary; the presign, multipart, and codec fan-out reach `@aws-sdk/client-s3`/`@aws-sdk/s3-request-presigner` directly per call site today with no storage-tier owner.

[REACTIVE_QUERY_INVALIDATION]
- A read-side reactive-query owner the node tier lacks: the `@effect/sql` `SqlClient.reactive` query bound over the `@effect/experimental` `Reactivity` key-scoped invalidation substrate, so a mutation invalidates exactly the affected query keys and a dependent read re-runs as a `Stream`, rather than every node consumer polling or re-querying on a timer. The `interchange` `CommandGateway` mutation and the `eventing/transactional-outbox` relay publish the invalidation keys; the node read paths (the `messaging/internal-rpc` query procedures, the SLO signal-series read) subscribe through `SqlClient.reactive`.
- A node-tier read model that pushes on the keys a write actually touched: a new reactive read is one `reactive([keys], query)` row keyed by entity, not a parallel change-feed; the same `Reactivity` substrate the `@effect/sql-pg` `PgClient` already requires (the `Reactivity.Reactivity` requirement the layer discharges) is mined directly rather than left as plumbing. Re-opens the over-narrow `EXPERIMENTAL_RETIRE_OR_KEEP` disposition: `Reactivity` is a SECOND first-class `@effect/experimental` consumer beside the `Sse` decode, not a transitive substrate.
- Draws on the `@effect/experimental` `Reactivity` key-scoped mutation/query/stream invalidation and the `@effect/sql` `SqlClient.reactive` reactive-query surface — both admitted, neither yet exploited by any design page; distinct from the `projection` browser fold (this is the server-side query-invalidation edge, that is the decoded-stream client fold).

[NATIVE_DELAYED_DELIVERY]
- A native delayed-delivery and timer owner over the `@effect/cluster` `DeliverAt` protocol: an entity payload implementing `DeliverAt` schedules its own message for a future `DateTime` through the shard manager, so a per-key retry-after, a deferred saga step, an agent-turn cooldown, and a scheduled tenant action ride the cluster's OWN delayed-delivery rail rather than a parallel timer table or an external scheduler. `DeliverAt`-scheduled entity messages compose with the existing `messaging/entity` and `agent/session-actors` actors and the `runtime-backplane` cron, filling the per-message-delay gap between the immediate `Apply` and the cron-pinned sweep.
- Per-key scheduled delivery the durable units lack: a delayed action is one `DeliverAt`-implementing payload on an existing entity, exactly-once and shard-placed like every other cluster message, never a second scheduling substrate; the `runtime-backplane` cron stays the cluster-wide pinned sweep, `DeliverAt` becomes the per-key future-message rail beneath it.
- Draws on the `@effect/cluster` `DeliverAt` symbol protocol (`isDeliverAt`/`toMillis`) — admitted, exposed in the catalogue, exploited by no page; the timer concern is currently spread across the saga `Effect.timeoutTo`, the activity retry schedule, and the cron, with no per-key future-delivery owner.

[ENTITY_RESOURCE_LIFETIME]
- A restart-surviving per-key resource owner over the `@effect/cluster` `EntityResource`: a scoped resource acquired inside an entity (a connection pool to an external sink, a model-host session, a sub-process handle) that survives runner restarts until its idle-TTL elapses or `close` fires, with the `EntityResource.makeK8sPod` arm placing a managed pod resource per key and the `CloseScope` releasing it. The collaborative-session and agent-session actors gain a real resource lifetime — the persisted `Chat` handle, the external tool host — bound to the actor key rather than re-acquired per message.
- Per-key resource durability the actors lack: a stateful entity holds a live resource across restarts and idle-evicts it, so an actor's external session is one `EntityResource.make({ acquire, idleTimeToLive })` row, never a re-acquire-per-message cost or an unbounded leak; the k8s-pod arm gives a per-tenant or per-session managed compute resource the deploy tier provisions and the actor key owns.
- Draws on the `@effect/cluster` `EntityResource`/`CloseScope`/`makeK8sPod` surface — admitted, catalogued, exploited by no page; the actor pages persist STATE through the `PgClient` but hold no live RESOURCE bound to the key, the exact gap `EntityResource` closes.

[IDEMPOTENCY_LEDGER]
- A durable idempotency/replay-dedup ledger: one keyed ledger recording a request/command idempotency key and its terminal outcome so a re-delivered message short-circuits to its prior result, giving the outbox relay, the entity actors, and the agent turns one shared cross-surface exactly-once dedup owner.
- A single dedup surface across `eventing/transactional-outbox`, `messaging/entity`, and `agent/session-actors` instead of each re-deriving its own; the cross-surface exactly-once the per-surface primitives do not span.
- Deferred-frontier candidate: the cluster `MessageStorage` `SaveResult.Duplicate` and the workflow `idempotencyKey` already carry per-surface dedup, so the ledger admits only the cross-surface dedup those primitives do not cover — record the overlap before authoring.

[QUOTA_GOVERNOR]
- A per-tenant quota and backpressure governor: a durable rate/concurrency cap over the remote-lane callouts, the agent turns, and the outbox external sinks, distinct from the workflow `DurableRateLimiter` per-key cap, so a tenant cannot starve the cluster. The governor is a `messaging/entity` per-tenant actor placement over the `@effect/experimental` rate-limiting module, the aggregate-fairness layer above the per-key primitive.
- Per-tenant aggregate backpressure across the AI, outbox, and remote-lane surfaces, beyond the per-key primitive; a fairness contract the durable tier lacks.
- Deferred-frontier candidate: the workflow `DurableRateLimiter` is the per-key primitive and `@effect/experimental` carries a rate-limiting module, so the governor admits only the per-tenant aggregate backpressure neither covers — record the boundary before authoring.

[EMBEDDING_LIFECYCLE]
- An embedding-lifecycle owner: the re-embed scheduling, the embedding-profile migration, and the staleness-driven refresh the `hybrid-search/fused-rank` semantic arm depends on, beyond the single re-embed `WorkQueue` row the search references today. The batched embed rides the `@effect/ai` `EmbeddingModel.makeDataLoader` windowed data-loader (and the `EmbeddingModel.make` per-call cache), the exact admitted surface no page yet drives.
- Profile-migration and batched re-embed orchestration so a model upgrade re-embeds the corpus durably rather than ad hoc; the lifecycle the search arm consumes but does not own.
- Deferred-frontier candidate: the search page already owns the staleness column and the re-embed `WorkQueue` row, so the lifecycle owner admits only the profile-migration and batch-embed orchestration the search arm does not own — record the split before authoring.
