## [3]-[ATTACKS] — by mandated surface

### [3.1] Lifecycle state machine — stuck states

- A1 [VALID] "A state with no deadline is a place to die": as drawn in 10 §1.2 the ladder names happy-path transitions; nothing guaranteed every state an exit. ATTACK: host wedges during `Negotiating` (hello accepted, then dialog) — no scenario deadline is armed yet, heartbeat not started ⇒ stuck. FIX (applied 07 §5.3): stuck-state LAW — every non-terminal state has a `SessionPolicy` deadline row and a `DeadlineHit` transition to `Faulted`; `Faulted` always reaches `Terminal` (envelope emission is the fold of whatever exists, never skipped).
- A2 [VALID] crashed supervisor wedges the next run: the lease lives at the resource (charter §4), but a supervisor that dies holding it leaves a token with no holder. The old python flock self-released on process exit; an O_EXCL lease file does not. FIX (applied 07 §5.5): staleness admission — holder-PID liveness with start-time validation (the `EndpointRecord` pattern reused), reclaim emits `lease.reclaimed` fact.
- A3 [VALID] nothing stops a third party mid-session: supervisor lease gates SUPERVISORS; a hand-run client or router-spawned tool can still dial the pipe (the busy semaphore existed in the old tool for exactly this, Transport.cs:170-174). The model deleted it without replacement. FIX (applied 07 §3.1): shell busy admission — second connection's mutating calls fail fast with `BusyHeld(holderPid, age)`.
- A4 [VALID] `PhaseStatus.Worst` is order-dependent: model §2.1 gives failed/timeout/busy equal rank 3 over DISTINCT exit codes (1/5/5); `other.Rank > Rank ? other : this` then resolves ties by arrival order — the same batch can exit 1 or 5 depending on scenario order. FIX (applied 07 §2 [AMENDED-1]): strict total order ok=skipped(1) < unsupported(2) < failed(3) < timeout(4) < busy(5), preserving the old tool's exit precedence (BridgeWire.cs:13-38). Regression law lands in the C# spec suite (08 §6).
- A5 [REFUTED] "warm reuse needs a daemon and daemons wedge": warmth is detected per invocation (hello against the endpoint record), nothing persists in the supervisor between runs except the journal and lease files, both staleness-admitted. No resident process exists to wedge (features F8, cut 1).

### [3.2] Envelope — lossy evidence

- B1 [VALID] the envelope cannot reproduce the consumer's existing contract: `VerifySummary.first_fault_phase` (model.py:371-384, verified current) has no envelope source because model §2.4 excludes phase events from `Evidence`. The "field-for-field in one msgspec decode" claim (model §2.4) was ~70% true. FIX (applied 07 §2 [AMENDED-2], 08 §3): `SessionEnvelope.FirstFaultPhase` as a fold-result field; `first_fault_output` deleted python-side (no scrape, no field); `exceptions/facts/captures` as `$type` folds. Corpus amendment recorded with both citations.
- B2 [REFUTED] "unbounded `Evidence` blows up stdout": the envelope's `Evidence` fold admits fact+capture cases only — phase/progress spam stays in receipts + spool (model §2.4 comment, made explicit in 07 §5.4); facts are author-sized wire fields, and transcripts stay disk artifacts by cut 26. Dev-tool scale (tens of scenarios × tens of facts) is KBs. The 32 KiB truncation class died by NOT routing evidence through process output; no new cliff replaces it.
- B3 [VALID] `EventStamp.Sequence` had three writers and no owner: cargo, shell, and supervisor all emit events; unowned sequence = ordering ambiguity in the merged stream and a useless spool-reconcile key. FIX (applied 07 §2 pin): shell assigns the session-global sequence for all in-host events (it is already the single relay — cargo publishes through the shell-owned delegate); supervisor fold seeds above the relayed max; ordering law `(AtUnixMs, Sequence)`.
- B4 [VALID] stream and spool can silently disagree: StreamJsonRpc notifications are fire-and-forget; a dropped notification loses a fact from the envelope while the spool keeps it — and nothing would ever notice. FIX (applied 07 §5.4): terminal spool reconciliation — count/last-sequence comparison, mismatch emits `evidence.divergence` fact. Degraded-but-diagnosed, the tolerance-doc contract.

### [3.3] Handshake amortization claims

- C1 [VALID] the claim quietly assumed away invoke-time MSBuild: today's per-file cost is dominated by `dotnet run` + restore + `-getProperty/-getItem` children (3 even warm, Program.cs:399-431; N×45 s worst case Build.cs:99-109). The rebuild deletes the per-SCENARIO multiplier, but nothing in 06 said who enumerates the closure — re-importing MSBuild evaluation into the supervisor would claw back seconds per invocation. FIX (applied 07 §5.6): build-time `bridge-closure.json` target; the supervisor reads, never evaluates; `deps.json` fallback gated by Phase-1 proof 6 ([NEEDS_COMPILE_PROOF]: whether `Private=false` HintPath host refs surface in deps.json).
- C2 [NEEDS_LIVE_PROOF] the <10 s warm loop and ≤50% batch wall-time (cutover criteria 4) remain unproven until probe 0d: the 3-8 s figure is corroborated (corpus 03 §F3; skills bridge-runtime.md:40-44 — N files = N × handshake), but no controlled supervisor-spawn benchmark exists. The claim is plausible arithmetic, not evidence; 0d is the gate and stays mandatory.

### [3.4] Crash-recovery story

- D1 [VALID] `quit.force` was a rung without a definition — and the obvious reading is WRONG: "force" on macOS suggests SIGTERM, but SIGTERM self-SIGABRTs via RhMacSignalHandler (as does `RhinoApp.Exit(false)`; empirically corrected 2026-05-29 — restated, not just cited), which MANUFACTURES the `.ips`/autosave markers reconcile exists to clear. A SIGTERM rung would violate port obligation 1 while appearing to honor it. FIX (applied 07 §5.5): rung definitions — quit.ae = Apple Event terminate; quit.force = `NSRunningApplication.forceTerminate` (Cocoa-mediated SIGKILL, needs the live handle); quit.kill = `kill(2)` SIGKILL floor; SIGTERM banned at every rung.
- D2 [VALID] instance-scoped reconcile (mcp §4 rule 3) was underspecified to the point of being unimplementable: matching markers "by PID/start-time window" requires knowing the DEAD instance's window — the next session has a new PID, and our own SIGKILL markers from a prior `quit` would be skipped as foreign (recovery-dialog roulette returns) or matched globally (the cross-tool hazard returns). FIX (applied 07 §5.5): the quit journal — retired endpoint records + kill timestamps persisted in `~/.rasm/`; reconcile matches markers against SUPERVISED instances' [launch, kill] windows; foreign markers are skipped AND reported as evidence.
- D3 [REFUTED] "crashes between invocations are lost": no watcher lives between invocations (no daemon), so a host that dies idle leaves markers no kqueue saw — but reconcile already reports markers found/cleared as evidence events (port obligation 2 housing, charter §7 row 2), so the information surfaces in the next envelope as facts; it was never any session's fault to attribute. The `.ips` diff baseline correctly excludes pre-session reports from session attribution.
- D4 [REFUTED] "the pre-launch reconcile race re-opens on warm runs": warm sessions skip launch, but a crash mid-warm-session transitions through `Faulted` → next invocation's hello fails → cold path → reconcile runs before `open`. The only path to a cold open IS through `Reconciling` (07 §5.2 — a named state, not a step that can be skipped; the 04 FM2 placement law holds by construction).

### [3.5] GH2 isolated-loading interactions

- E1 [NEEDS_LIVE_PROOF] the cargo ALC unload contract is paper until probe 0a: the only adjacent evidence is RhinoCode's collectible context loading the FP stack hazardously-but-workably (CodeEngine.cs:32 + the F11 bootstrap) — there is ZERO unload/unloadConfirmed evidence anywhere in the tree. If Rasm statics pin the ALC un-fixably, F2 degrades to per-session host recycling (10 §4 fallback) with every other feature intact. The design needs no change; the gate stays absolute: no cargo code before 0a.
- E2 [VALID] internal contradiction: charter D-4 says scenario projects MAY name GH2 types; tolerance M2 says scenarios speak `Gh2Lane` vocabulary, "never raw GH2 types". Both cannot bind. RESOLUTION (applied 07 §4.3): D-4 lifts the HOST-LAW prohibition (naming GH2 types is no longer a crash — that was a RhinoCode-isolated-resolver artifact); M2's churn budget still routes all GH2 touches through `Gh2Lane` — a scenario naming a raw GH2 type is a LINT (churn-budget violation), not a loader error. One rule, both docs satisfied.
- E3 [VALID] probe 0b as written would reproduce the old deadlock and falsely confirm the ceiling: the prior live result (restated: bridge scenarios ran ON the UI thread; the GH2 solver is idle-loop-driven; `Start` never settled and `StartWait` deadlocked — reference_gh2_headless_solution_limits) and the decompile (06 §2: `Start` returns `Task<Solution>`, solve runs in `Task.Run` on a threadpool worker) are BOTH true — in different thread regimes. An unpinned probe is worthless. FIX (applied 07 §10 proof 5 and 10-distill): 0b definition now pins thread context — solve wait OFF the UI thread, idle pump alive.
- E4 [REFUTED] "preloading GH2 for every session wastes seconds and pollutes non-GH sessions": `CargoManifest.HostPlugins` derives from the closure manifest (07 §5.6) — a session whose scenario closure lacks Grasshopper2 carries no GUID and triggers no preload. One truth (the closure), no dual heuristic (01 F17 dies), no unconditional preload.
- E5 [REFUTED] "forwarded GH2 types split identity when the host updates mid-week": cargo compiles against bundle HintPaths and the M4 fingerprint gate rebuilds + restages + re-probes on any fingerprint delta before scenarios run; macOS cannot replace a running bundle's loaded images mid-session (tolerance §4 row M4). The split-singleton failure (04 FM11) required DUPLICATING a host assembly into the isolated context — `HostAssemblyTable` forwarding makes that structurally impossible (model §3.2).

### [3.6] MCP-complement boundary

- F1 [VALID] the suppression hook is an off-label 0.1.x internal on a weekly-moving target: `RHINO_MCP_AUTOSTART_PORT` short-circuits `Plugin.Register` as READ at plugin-v0.1.5 source (mcp §4 rule 1 admits the fragility); 0.1.5's release notes are stability-only but nothing pins their next release. A launch-time assumption with no runtime check is folklore-in-waiting (the tolerance doc's own regime-C law). FIX (applied 07 §9): `doctor`/hello row `mcp.listener` — positive runtime detection of a rhinomcp listener in the verification host, failing with a prescription. The env var becomes belt; the probe is truth.
- F2 [REFUTED] "EITHER-6 (standardize evidence wire on MCP structuredContent) pressures the Contract toward MCP's schema": the facade is a FOLD of `SessionEnvelope` living supervisor-side, phase-5+, demand-gated (mcp §3.2.2); `outputSchema` generates FROM `BridgeJsonContext`, not the reverse. Zero Contract coupling. The 2026-06-11 landscape re-check strengthens the complement verdict (rhinomcp unchanged architecturally at 0.1.5; Anthropic's nine-connector wave commoditizes the modeling lane; the macOS RhinoCode CLI runtimeconfig breakage Nov-Dec 2025 is fresh evidence AGAINST any CLI-shell-out lane and FOR the in-process bridge) — no amendment to the mcp verdict.
- F3 [REFUTED] "their kill-teardown makes our reconcile dangerous": it makes a GLOBAL reconcile dangerous, which is exactly why D2's quit journal scopes matching to instances WE supervised; router-killed instances' markers are skipped-as-foreign and reported. The hazard is real and the design now structurally cannot trip it.
