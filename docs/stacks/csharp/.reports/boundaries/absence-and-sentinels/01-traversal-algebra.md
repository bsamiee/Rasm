# Traversal Algebra of Boundary Absence

[CARRIER_POLYMORPHIC_SWEEP]:
- A boundary that probes N foreign sources produces `K<T, K<F, A>>` — a structure `T` of absence-carriers `F` — and collapses to `K<F, K<T, A>>` in one call, hoisting the carrier outward so the whole sweep becomes a single rail decision. `T.Sequence(ta)` and `traverse(f, ta)` own this collapse; the structure `T` (`Seq`, `Arr`, `Lst`, `Iterable`, even `Option`) and the carrier `F` are both abstract, so one written expression serves every probe-collection shape and every absence carrier without a per-shape overload.
- Two collapse families exist and are not interchangeable. `traverse`/`sequence` constrain the carrier to `Applicative<F>` and run every effect, combining results with the carrier's `Apply`. `traverseM`/`sequenceM` constrain it to `Monad<M>` and sequence with `Bind`, so a failing element short-circuits the remainder. The constraint is the policy: an applicative sweep is a complete census of absence, a monadic sweep is fail-fast.
- The element transform feeds the carrier directly — `traverse(probe, sources)` where `probe : A -> K<F, B>` — so the projection from raw foreign value to carrier happens once per element inside the fold, never as a pre-pass that materializes an intermediate collection of carriers. The pre-pass form (`sources.Map(probe)` then `sequence`) is byte-equivalent only for pure projections; when `probe` is effectful the fused `traverse` is the sole correct shape.

[ACCUMULATE_VERSUS_SHORTCIRCUIT]:
- The accumulate-versus-short-circuit decision is owned by the carrier's `Apply`, not by the sweep. `Option.Apply` and the monadic carriers discard on the first absence — the sweep yields `None`/first-error and the surviving evidence is lost. `Validation<FAIL>.Apply` routes both-fail through `FAIL`'s semigroup `Combine`, so an applicative sweep over `Validation` returns every failure reason in one value. Selecting the carrier therefore selects whether the boundary reports one absence or all of them.
- A subtle middle case: the carrier's applicative combines fail-with-fail through `Error +`, which builds a flattened `ManyErrors`, but a *success* function applied to a *failing* argument yields only the argument's error. Because the tupled and chained applicative form is a left-fold — `f.Map(a).Apply(b).Apply(c)` — the moment the first failing argument turns the accumulator into a failing function, every subsequent `.Apply` hits the fail-with-fail branch and folds its own error in. The net effect is full accumulation across all arguments evaluated after the first failure, with the pre-failure successes silently dropped. This is neither pure short-circuit nor pure accumulation; treat it as accumulate-from-first-failure and reach for the dedicated accumulating carrier when every reason must survive regardless of position.
- An N-source boundary combine is one tupled applicative call, not a nested match ladder. The carrier is inferred and the arity is absorbed by the lifted function:

```csharp
static K<F, Session> OpenSession<F>(
    K<F, Endpoint> host,
    K<F, Credential> cred,
    K<F, Capability> caps)
    where F : Applicative<F> =>
    Applicative.apply(
        (host, cred, caps),
        static (h, c, k) => new Session(h, c, k));
```

- The same body, instantiated at `F = Validation<Error>`, returns every missing-input reason in a single `ManyErrors`; instantiated at `F = Option`, it returns `None` on the first absence and costs nothing on the present path. One written combinator, two boundary policies, chosen at the call site by the carrier supplied — no second method, no flag.

[UNCONDITIONAL_EFFECT_HAZARD]:
- A traversal never guarantees the element function runs. The absent branch of every carrier returns `F.Pure(...)` over the empty structure without invoking the transform, so an audit, metric, or log written into the traverse function fires only on present elements. A boundary obligation that must fire for every probe — including the absent ones — cannot live inside `traverse`; it sequences as an independent effect, or splits the present and absent populations first and emits over both.
- The dual hazard sits on single-carrier biased combinators: routing both presence and absence through a bi-shaped transform keeps the pipeline on one rail, but the absent thunk and the null-returning present branch can each silently manufacture a structurally-empty result with no diagnostic. The accumulating-traversal discipline subsumes both: project each probe to the carrier at its own seam, sweep once, and let the carrier's algebra — not a hand-written branch — decide the combined outcome.

[POPULATION_PARTITION]:
- When the boundary must keep both the survivors and the casualties — not collapse to a single rail outcome — the fold is a partition, not a traverse. A single non-short-circuiting fold over `K<F, Fin<A>>` for any foldable `F` yields `(Seq<Error> Fails, Seq<A> Succs)` in one pass, so a capability census reports usable results and failure reasons simultaneously without two iterations. The effectful sibling sequences each inner monadic action inside the outer rail, catching per-element failure through the fallible trait so the outer rail stays live even as individual probes fail — the dense surface for a batch where partial success is the expected steady state.
- For absence-typed (rather than error-typed) probe results, the silent-drop collector discards `None` with no record; when an absent probe warrants a metric, the choosing projection with a labelled absent branch replaces it. The rule generalizes: a partition that throws away the casualty population is the wrong fold whenever the boundary's health surface is derived from why probes were absent.

[STRUCTURE_REWRITE_VERSUS_VALUE_MAP]:
- Hoisting a carrier out of a structure (traversal) is distinct from rewriting one carrier into another (natural transformation). A natural transformation rewrites the *container* while leaving the bound value untouched — projecting an absence carrier to an error carrier, an effect carrier, or a sequence carrier through a single declared structural arrow rather than a `Match`-then-rewrap. Each absence carrier publishes a fixed set of these arrows; the boundary picks the target rail by naming it, and the rewrite is total and allocation-minimal because it dispatches on the case, not on a thunk.
- The composition that matters at a boundary: `traverse` to gather N probes into one carrier of a structure, then a structural rewrite to move that single carrier onto the rail the interior speaks. A sweep that yields `Option<Seq<A>>` becomes `Fin<Seq<A>>` by one structural arrow, not by `Match(Some: ..., None: ...)` — the rewrite names the absence's error code once at the seam and the interior never sees the optional. Reaching for `Map` here is the error: `Map` transforms the values inside the carrier and cannot change the carrier, so it can never move absence onto the error rail.

[TRANSFORMER_STACK_THREADING]:
- Absence inside an effectful boundary is a carrier stacked on the effect monad, and the sweep threads through both layers at once. A monadic traversal over the stacked carrier sequences the effect per element and short-circuits the absence layer on the first miss; the applicative traversal runs every effect and combines the absence layer by its `Apply`. The choice of `traverse` versus `traverseM` therefore decides, in a single line, both whether later probes' side effects execute and whether their absences accumulate — the two concerns that a hand-written loop would tangle into separate flags.
- The stacked carrier exposes no direct absence-to-failure projection; the failure recovery surface it implements is keyed on the empty marker, not on a typed error, so a boundary that must discriminate *kinds* of absence after the sweep extracts the inner absence carrier from the effect, projects it onto the typed-error rail with a coded reason, and re-lifts. The discrimination lives at the extraction seam; the sweep itself stays carrier-agnostic.
