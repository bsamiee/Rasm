# Quasirandom Equidistribution as a Derived Design Surface

[ABSENT_SURFACE_FORCES_OWNED_DESIGN]:
- The admitted package universe ships only pseudorandom generators — linear-congruential, lagged-Fibonacci, multiple-recursive, two xorshift families, and a single 256-bit scrambled-rotate generator — and no low-discrepancy, digital-net, lattice, or scrambled-sequence type of any kind. Quasi-Monte Carlo field integration therefore has no surface to wrap and is an owned build: the design pressure is not "which constructor" but "what is the closed family, what is the policy vocabulary, and what evidence does a deterministic point set owe a caller who cannot read an error bar off a single number."
- A pseudorandom draw and a quasirandom draw are not two cases of one sampler: the first targets statistical independence and the second targets equidistribution, and conflating them under a shared `Sample()` returns a value whose variance law the caller cannot recover. The boundary that admits a sample source must carry the discriminant — independent versus equidistributed — as a type, because every downstream variance estimate, error bar, and convergence rate forks on it. The independent source owns a serializable continuation state; the equidistributed source owns an index and a randomization seed, and the two state shapes do not unify.
- The deterministic nature of a digital net is the whole problem a receipt must solve: a single quasirandom estimate is one number with no spread, so the worst-case bound is the only error statement available and it requires an unknown function-variation constant, making it useless as a practical bar. The design law is that an equidistributed estimate is never emitted alone — it is emitted as a randomized replicate family whose cross-replicate spread is the recoverable error, and the carrier that holds the replicate set is the only admissible return shape.

[DIGITAL_NET_AS_CARRIER_POLYMORPHIC_RECURRENCE]:
- A base-2 digital net is generated by exactly one recurrence over a fixed-width unsigned carrier, and the dimension count, the resolution bits, and the per-dimension direction vectors are its only data. The carrier is the integer width, not a floating point: each coordinate is a left-justified fixed-point integer accumulated by exclusive-or against direction words, and the division into the unit interval happens once at the boundary that hands a value to the integrand. Keeping the integer representation interior is not an optimization — it is the precondition for every randomization operator, because a digital shift and a linear matrix scramble are both exclusive-or and bit-matrix operations on that integer and are unrepresentable on the divided double.
- The fast advance is a derived recurrence, not an enumerated loop: consecutive points in reflected-binary order differ in exactly one direction word, so the successor coordinate is the predecessor exclusive-or'd with the single direction word indexed by the rightmost zero bit of the prior index. The whole inner step is one trailing-zero-count intrinsic and one exclusive-or per dimension, and the set of points over a full power-of-two block is identical to the non-reordered net — the reordering is a free permutation that preserves the equidistribution exactly.

```csharp
static (ulong[] state, double[] point) Next(
    ulong[] state, ReadOnlySpan<ulong[]> directions, int priorIndex, int bits)
{
    var c = BitOperations.TrailingZeroCount(~(uint)priorIndex);     // rightmost zero bit of n-1
    var advanced = state.Select((s, d) => s ^ directions[d][c]).ToArray();
    var scale = 1.0 / (1UL << bits);
    return (advanced, advanced.Select(s => s * scale).ToArray());    // divide once, at the boundary
}
```

- The recurrence is carrier-polymorphic in the same sense the integration stage recurrence is: the direction words live in the unsigned bit carrier and the emitted coordinate lives in the floating carrier, bridged at one division. A net over a wider word is the same recurrence with a wider carrier and is selected by the resolution-bits policy, not by a parallel type; the bits value is bounded above by the carrier width and bounded effectively by the floating mantissa, so beyond the mantissa width the low direction words fall off the representable grid and the extra bits buy resolution only for the exclusive-or randomizers, never for the divided value.
- The direction words are themselves derived, not stored as a flat constant: each dimension's words come from a primitive polynomial over the two-element field and a short list of odd initial numerators, expanded by a fixed exclusive-or-and-shift recurrence to fill the resolution. The constraint that every initial numerator is odd and bounded by its bit position is a definition-time admissibility law on the direction table — a violated numerator silently destroys the net property with no exception — so the table is admitted through a validating factory that returns the equidistributed generator or a typed structural fault, never a raw coefficient array handed to the recurrence.

[BALANCE_AS_AN_ARITY_LAW_NOT_A_KNOB]:
- The equidistribution guarantee holds only at counts that are powers of the base, and every operation that violates this — taking a non-power-of-two prefix, dropping the origin point, striding or thinning the sequence — degrades the discrepancy with no diagnostic. The count is therefore not a free integer parameter: the entrypoint that draws a block accepts the exponent, not the cardinality, so a balanced block is the only representable request and an unbalanced count cannot be spelled. The arity collapse is that "draw a sample set" discriminates on a block exponent whose value is the equidistribution contract, and a caller needing more points jumps to the next exponent rather than topping up an unbalanced remainder.
- The net is extensible across exponents only under the randomization operators that commute with block growth: a single digital shift preserves the ability to grow a block and reuse its points, while a linear matrix scramble and a nested permutation scramble generally do not. Extensibility is thus a property of the randomization policy, not of the net, and a design that promises progressive sample growth with error bars must select the shift randomizer specifically — the policy value carries both the variance behavior and the extensibility behavior as one row, so the choice cannot be made on one axis and silently lose the other.

[SCRAMBLE_AND_CONSTRUCTION_AS_POLICY_VALUES]:
- The scramble is a policy row that fixes a variance law, not a boolean: an unscrambled net is deterministic with no error estimate and error decaying near the inverse of the count times a logarithmic dimension penalty; a digital-shift randomization restores an unbiased estimator at the same leading rate; a linear matrix scramble with a shift, and a full nested permutation scramble, both lift smooth-integrand convergence to the inverse three-halves power of the count and guarantee the estimator is never asymptotically worse than independent sampling. The matrix scramble attains the smooth rate at the cost of one nonsingular lower-triangular bit-matrix per dimension, while the nested scramble matches it with a permutation tree whose node count grows geometrically in resolution — the tradeoff is constant factor and memory, and it belongs in the policy row as data the design selects by integrand regime, never re-derived at the call site.
- The path-construction policy is the lever that makes high nominal dimension tractable, and it is orthogonal to the scramble. The net's early coordinates are the best-equidistributed; a construction that routes the integrand's dominant variance into those early coordinates collapses the effective dimension. The incremental construction assigns one coordinate per increment and spreads variance evenly; the bridge construction assigns the early coordinates to the endpoint and recursive midpoints; the eigen construction factors the covariance and orders the factors by descending eigenvalue, routing the largest-variance direction to the first coordinate. The eigen ordering is variance-optimal for the truncation dimension among linear constructions — it is a derived ordering from one eigendecomposition, not a hand-tuned permutation — and the policy carries the construction as a row because the same net under two constructions integrates the same function at two different rates.
- The construction policy carries a documented non-monotonicity that forbids treating it as a pure win: routing variance into early coordinates reduces the effective dimension in the common case but is known to increase it on adversarial integrands, so the policy row is selected against the integrand's variance structure and the design records which construction produced an estimate as evidence, never as an invisible default. The selection is a policy decision the caller makes from the integrand's covariance, and the receipt that names the construction is what lets a downstream gate attribute a poor convergence to the construction rather than to the net.

[RANDOMIZED_REPLICATE_RECEIPT]:
- The only admissible error statement for an equidistributed estimate is the spread across independent randomizations of one net, so the return shape is a replicate family, not a scalar. A small number of independent randomizations — each an independent shift or scramble of the same block — yields one unbiased mean per replicate; the mean of the means is the estimate and the cross-replicate sample variance divided by the replicate count is the squared standard error, with a Student bound on the residual degrees of freedom giving an interval that holds its nominal coverage empirically despite the per-replicate means being non-normal. The replicate count and the per-replicate block exponent are one allocation policy: the variance reduction lives in the block, the error visibility lives in the replicates, and the total budget splits between them as a policy value the design selects, never two loose integers the loop multiplies.
- The replicate receipt is a distinct evidence family from an integration error receipt and must not collapse into it: where the integration receipt carries the adaptive error estimate, the cancellation ratio, and the termination discriminant of one deterministic quadrature, the replicate receipt carries the per-replicate means, the cross-replicate variance, the interval half-width at a confidence level, and the realized randomization seeds that make the estimate reproducible. A field integral that nests a quasirandom inner expectation inside an adaptive outer quadrature stacks the two receipts — the inner replicate spread becomes the outer integrand's evaluation uncertainty — and keeping them as separate carriers is what lets the outer gate see that the inner uncertainty, not the outer adaptivity, dominates the total.

```csharp
[Union]
public partial interface QuasirandomEstimate<T> where T : IFloatingPointIeee754<T>
{
    QuasirandomEstimate<T> Replicated(T mean, T stdError, T halfWidth, int replicates, int blockExp);
    QuasirandomEstimate<T> Deterministic(T value, int blockExp);   // no randomization: no error bar
    QuasirandomEstimate<T> EffectiveDimensionExceeded(T mean, T stdError, int truncationDim);
}

static QuasirandomEstimate<T> FromReplicates<T>(ReadOnlySpan<T> means, int blockExp, T tQuantile)
    where T : IFloatingPointIeee754<T>
{
    var r = T.CreateChecked(means.Length);
    var mean = TensorPrimitives.Sum(means) / r;
    var ss = means.ToArray().Fold(T.Zero, (a, m) => a + (m - mean) * (m - mean));
    var stdErr = T.Sqrt(ss / (r - T.One)) / T.Sqrt(r);
    return QuasirandomEstimate<T>.Replicated(mean, stdErr, tQuantile * stdErr, means.Length, blockExp);
}
```

[EQUIDISTRIBUTION_EVIDENCE_AS_A_DISTINCT_RECEIPT]:
- The quality of a digital net is a structural integer, not a runtime measurement: a net's deviation from perfect equidistribution is bounded by one quality parameter that directly multiplies the discrepancy and the integration-error constant, and that parameter is a definition-time fact of the direction table, computable at construction. The generator therefore carries its quality parameter as evidence on every block it produces, and a downstream gate that needs a discrepancy guarantee reads it off the value rather than re-deriving it; a net assembled from a poor direction table is admissible to construct but carries its degraded parameter honestly, so the gate rejects it on quality rather than discovering the degradation as a slow convergence.
- The two-dimensional projections of a high-dimensional net are not uniformly good even under optimal direction tables: specific coordinate pairs carry a poor projection quality parameter and exhibit visible diagonal structure at small counts, so any surface that lets a caller select an arbitrary pair of coordinates for a planar field probe owes a per-pair projection-quality figure as evidence. The projection quality is a property of the chosen coordinate subset, derivable from the direction table, and exposing it turns a silent striping artifact into a gate the caller can read before trusting a low-dimensional slice of the net.
- The effective dimension is itself evidence the construction owes its consumer: the fraction of variance captured by the leading coordinates under the chosen construction is the truncation figure, and a construction that fails to concentrate variance there surfaces as a high truncation dimension and a degraded rate. The receipt carries the truncation figure beside the estimate so that a poor convergence is attributable to a high effective dimension rather than to the net or the integrand, and the terminal case where the leading coordinates fail to capture the required variance fraction is a typed evidence case — the estimate is returned with the diagnosis that the construction did not reduce the dimension, not silently with a wide error bar the caller must reverse-engineer.

[LATTICE_RULE_AS_THE_SMOOTH_PERIODIC_SIBLING]:
- The digital net is one case of the equidistributed family; the rank-one lattice rule is a second case selected by integrand smoothness, and the two share the replicate-receipt return but differ entirely in generation. A lattice rule places its points as the fractional parts of integer multiples of a single generating vector scaled by the count, so its state is one generating vector and a count, and its randomization is a single uniform shift of the whole point set. The lattice rule rewards genuine smoothness: for a periodic integrand of smoothness order above one it achieves a rate equal to the count raised to the negative smoothness order, exceeding the net's near-linear ceiling, while a non-periodic integrand must be periodized by a tent or baker transform first or the smooth rate is lost.
- The generating vector is itself a derived value from one greedy construction that fixes the vector coordinate by coordinate, each new coordinate chosen to minimize the worst-case error of the rule built so far, with the candidate sweep evaluable by a transform in near-linear time per coordinate. The vector is therefore not a stored magic constant but a construction output validated against a smoothness-weighted error functional, and the policy that selects the lattice case over the net case is a single integrand-regime row — periodic-and-smooth versus bounded-variation — that the design reads from the integrand's analytic class, collapsing the choice between two generation algorithms into one vocabulary key rather than two parallel entrypoints.
