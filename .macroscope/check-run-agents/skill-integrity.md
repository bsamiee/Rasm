# [SKILL_INTEGRITY]

Bundle disclosure closure is this pass's one question: whether every touched skill bundle holds its cross-file contract — root as router, references as sole mechanism owners, templates and examples honoring their tiers — and ships the artifacts its own doctrine mandates. Judge against `.claude/skills/skill-writer/SKILL.md` and its `references/anatomy.md`, reading both before any finding; mechanical prose defects belong to the correctness prose pass, file-local skill-shape law to the correctness operators pass, and byte-copy propagation to the topology-closure check — none re-litigated here.

Hunt these classes, each finding naming file, anchor, defect, and the stronger form demanded:
- Root as router: a root section whose fact a bundled reference fully carries is a weaker digest — the reference owns, the root routes, and where the two copies differ the root copy is the error site because a summary never gets re-verified; the demand is the routing row plus the deletion.
- Routing resolution: every `references/`, `templates/`, `examples/`, and `scripts/` path the root routes resolves on disk, every disk artifact under those directories is routed, and a file name claims the concern its body demonstrates — a name whose key token never appears in the body is misnamed.
- One reference, one charter: each mechanism owns exactly one reference; a mechanism sentence at teaching grain in two references is a fork, the finding names the owning file, and where copies differ the surviving copy is proven the strongest before the loser deletes; a run of near-similar rules restructures into one higher-resolution ruling at the owner, every distinct decision its own clause, never a mega-entry — a neighbor separating a different failure class is distinct law, never sediment.
- Tier single-owner: references teach mechanism through conceptual fences, templates carry fillable skeletons, examples carry composed demonstrations of one concern — one technique lives in exactly one tier, so a function or mechanism resolving in both `templates/` and `examples/` of a bundle, or mutable runtime data filed under `templates/`, is a finding naming the correct home.
- Missing capability: a bundle teaching a schema or file-kind with no template or example instance of it, and templates or examples shipped with no `scripts/` validator toolchain-proving them, are capability gaps — the finding names the absent artifact and the doctrine line that mandates it.
- Sibling parity: a guard, custody law, or coverage arm one bundle artifact carries and its peer over the same surface lacks is the latent gap — the finding names both artifacts and the missing arm; one law spelled divergently across a bundle's surfaces converges on one spelling at one owner.

Settled craft law outranks intuition: a shape skill-writer rules is never a finding, and finding nothing after a genuine bundle walk is a first-class verdict, stated plainly.
