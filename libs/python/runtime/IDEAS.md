# [PY_RUNTIME_IDEAS]

The forward pool of higher-order concepts for `runtime`, grounded in the folder's domain and the monorepo purpose. Each open idea is a card — a bracketed slug, the capability, what it unlocks, and the gap or modern technique it draws on — and spawns one or more `TASKLOG.md` tasks. A finished or dropped idea moves to `[2]-[CLOSED]` with a one-line disposition. The design pool is realized at the C# parity bar: the metrics spine, the subinterpreter offload lane, the content-addressed lane cache, the structural drift guard, the proto-transcode codec, the credential-axis decode, the MessagePack op-log decode, the capability-SDK consume, the seed-reproduction parity binding, the cp315 catalogue capture, the composition-root OTLP install owner, the inbound W3C trace-context extract, the outbound gRPC client-span/hooks, the C#-minted causal/tenant inbound frame, the streaming-transport acquisition, the OS-keystore secret boundary, the `clock` HLC/`ElementId`/`Tenant` collapse, the `transport/wire` codec collapse, and the per-profile `FeatureGate` admission cell are all closed. The open cards are the higher-order owners that span the closed sub-domains or extend a local cell beyond what this pass realized — the lifecycle supervision spine over the lane/server/resource set, the broader multi-source structured-settings schema beyond the realized `FeatureGate` cell, the federated content-addressed reuse fabric over the wire codec, the distributed causal-merge/schedule beyond the local `clock` cell, the frozen content-keyed resource topology over roots and admission, and the compressed CRDT op-log leg genuinely [UPSTREAM-BLOCKED] on the cp315 `lz4` wheel.

OPEN contains `ACTIVE` work and `QUEUED` next-up work in logical sequence; `BLOCKED` keeps open but non-actionable work; `CLOSED` separates finished `COMPLETE` items from unimplemented `DROPPED` items. `Ripple` names the origin or counterpart card a cross-folder entry pairs with.

[LIFECYCLE_SUPERVISION_SPINE]-[QUEUED]: one supervision owner restarts the lane, server, and resource set under one structured-concurrency root.
- Capability: `runtime` owns one supervision/lifecycle spine over the `execution/lanes` task-group set, the `transport/serve` `ServerHost`, and the `transport/roots` provider sessions, so a companion process brings every long-lived owner up, drains it, and tears it down through one structured-concurrency root rather than each sub-domain owning an ad-hoc startup/shutdown path.
- Shape: a `Supervisor` owner over one parent `anyio.create_task_group` whose children are the registered long-lived owners discriminated by a `SupervisedKind` vocabulary (`SERVE`, `WATCH`, `SCHEDULE`, `LANE`), each child carrying a `RestartPolicy` row (one-for-one, transient, permanent) and draining through the existing `DrainReceipt`; a `LifecyclePhase` `StrEnum` (`STARTING`/`READY`/`DRAINING`/`STOPPED`) the `RuntimeContext` deadline and the `reliability/resilience` retry rows fold against, with `anyio` cancellation scopes the single shutdown mechanism so a parent cancel reaches every leaf in reverse-registration order through one `AsyncExitStack`.
- Unlocks: the companion sidecar profile runs the serve loop, the `watchfiles` feeder, the `apscheduler` `Trigger` fires, and the lane set under one restart-and-drain root, so a crashed child restarts under its policy and a SIGTERM drains the whole tree once rather than per-owner shutdown hooks scattered across the serve, lanes, and roots pages.
- Anchors: `execution/lanes` `LanePolicy`/`StagePlan`/`DrainReceipt`/`fired`/`watched`, `transport/serve` `ServerHost`, `transport/roots` provider `AsyncExitStack` sessions, `execution/admission#CONTEXT` `Deadline`, `reliability/resilience` `RetryClass`, and `anyio` task-group/`CancelScope`/`fail_after` cancellation.
- Tension: the spine supervises caller-registered owners only and never discovers the host or derives a product root — process lifecycle ownership versus the `execution` boundary that refuses lifecycle ownership is the line the spine must hold by supervising what the caller admits, never minting its own service set; the C# `AppHost` stays the product orchestrator across the wire.
