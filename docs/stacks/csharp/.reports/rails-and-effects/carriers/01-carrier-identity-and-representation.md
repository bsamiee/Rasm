# Carrier Identity And Representation

[PHYSICAL_SHAPE_DIVERGENCE]:
- The carriers do not share a representation kind, and the kind dictates copy, allocation, and identity semantics. The absence carrier is a two-field `readonly struct` — value-copied, zero-allocation, never null as a reference. The result carrier is a heap-allocated class hierarchy; the accumulating and general two-sided carriers are record hierarchies whose case payloads participate in compiler-synthesized record equality; the effect carriers are records wrapping a delegate-bearing inner effect, so their synthesized equality compares the captured function, which is identity-meaningless.
- The struct/class split is load-bearing for `default(T)`: `default` of the absence carrier is a usable `None`; `default` of every class-shaped carrier is a null reference, and any operation on it throws before the rail ever sees it. A field, array slot, or uninitialized local typed as a class-shaped carrier is a latent null; the struct-shaped carrier is total over `default`.
- The result carrier's cases project spans of arity zero-or-one: the success case returns a one-element `ReadOnlySpan` of its value and an empty failure span, the failure case the mirror, and the success-side collection projections build directly over that span. Every result value is therefore a bounded foldable over a stack span — fold, traverse, and comprehension behavior follows from a one-element-or-empty sequence, not from a special case.

[DEFAULT_EQUALITY_IS_REFLECTION_BACKED]:
- Default structural equality on a payload-typed carrier routes through a per-type strategy struct that falls through to a reflective trait probe, not through `EqualityComparer<A>.Default`. The strategy's static constructor runs once per closed payload type: it searches loaded assemblies for a convention-named equality trait implementation by string, and on a hit binds that trait's static `Equals`/`GetHashCode` as a cached reflective invocation; on a miss it records a resolution error and falls back to the framework default comparer.
- Two consequences follow from the reflective round-trip. First, equality of a carrier over a rich payload is a reflective call per comparison — invisible at the call site, material in hot equality paths, sets, and dictionary probes. Second, the bound delegate throws when the resolved trait method returns a null boxed result, surfacing an invalid-operation failure from inside the carrier's `==` far from the comparison's apparent source — a property of the discovered trait, reachable only once a real trait method was bound instead of the fallback.
- The escape is to never rely on default carrier equality over a payload with reflective trait ambiguity: compare on a projected scalar discriminant, or use the typeclass-keyed overloads — single-witness `Equals`/`CompareTo` on the absence and result carriers, two-witness forms on the two-sided carriers — which resolve the strategy at the type level with zero reflection. The witness form is the deterministic equality; the zero-argument form is the convenience that pays the resolver.

[FAILURE_CASE_IDENTITY_ASYMMETRY]:
- The result carrier and the accumulating/general carriers disagree on whether the failure payload participates in identity, and the disagreement is exact. The result carrier's failure case implements equality as a bare case test — any failure equals any other failure regardless of the carried error — and hashes to a fixed constant; its ordering likewise returns equal for any failure pair without consulting the error. The accumulating and general carriers hash and compare the failure payload through the payload's own trait, so two failures are equal only when their carried values are.
- This decides container behavior. A set or dictionary keyed on the result carrier coalesces all failures into a single key — distinct errors overwrite — which is correct for "did it fail" deduplication and wrong for "which failures occurred". The accumulating carrier preserves each distinct failure as a distinct key. Choosing the carrier for a keyed collection is choosing whether failure detail survives the hash, and the choice is irreversible inside the container.

[TOTAL_ORDER_PLACES_FAILURE_FIRST]:
- Every two-case carrier defines a total order in which the failure side sorts strictly before the success side: the failure/left/absence case compares negative against any success value and the success side positive against any failure; within a side, ordering delegates to the payload's order witness — except the result carrier's failure side, which orders all failures as mutually equal, consistent with its identity-transparent failure equality. Sorting a sequence of rail values groups all failures ahead of all successes with no predicate; a min over the sequence is "the first failure if any, else the least success".
- The absence carrier's order is the degenerate case of the same law: `None` sorts before any `Some`, compares equal to `None`, and hashes to zero. A sorted structure of optionals front-loads every absence; a min-priority queue of optionals dequeues absences first.

[MIXED_OPERAND_COMPARISON_ELIDES_LIFT]:
- The two-case carriers carry comparison and equality operators against the bare payload and against the lift literals, not only against another carrier: the result carrier compares directly against its success-side payload, and the two-sided carriers compare and equate against the payload, the success lift literal, and the failure lift literal. A predicate over a rail value against a constant needs no lift wrap on the constant and no unwrap on the carrier — the operator absorbs the lift on the literal side and the discriminant on the carrier side, and the mixed-operand comparison silently inherits the failure-first total order.

[EFFECT_CARRIERS_ARE_OPAQUE]:
- The effect carriers expose no meaningful identity: equality and hashing compare the captured delegate reference, never the eventual result — two effects computing the same value are unequal, and the same effect value is equal only to itself. There is no order, no structural projection, and no serialization. Using an effect as a dictionary key, in a set, or in a distinct-pass keys on closure identity, never on behavior; the correct comparison is over the run result inside the rail, never over the effect value. The record machinery generates this equality unconditionally, so the type system permits the category error.

[SERIALIZATION_SURFACE_IS_NON_UNIFORM]:
- Only the absence carrier carries a hand-written binary serialization contract: it writes a presence flag plus the bound value and reconstructs from the flag. The result, accumulating, and general carriers implement no such contract; the effect carriers cannot be serialized at all. A wire or persistence layer assuming uniform carrier serialization is wrong for three of the families — discriminated reference carriers must be projected to a wire-shaped union before crossing a boundary, effect carriers must be run to a value first, and the absence carrier travels only through the legacy binary contract, not through a modern codec it does not advertise.

[LIFT_LITERALS_ARE_CARRIER_AGNOSTIC_HUBS]:
- The success and failure lift literals are `readonly record struct` single-field values whose entire purpose is deferred carrier selection. The success literal exposes `Bind` overloads across the full carrier set — result, either, the effect families, reader, state, and the kind-generic monadic form — plus direct projections to the absence, result, and either carriers; the failure literal projects to the two-sided carriers and maps its payload. A value or error written once as a lift literal commits to no carrier until the surrounding bind or the destination type fixes one; the operator overloads and implicit conversions on each carrier accept the literal without an explicit constructor, making the lift literal the dispatch hub that replaces per-carrier construction at the head of a pipeline.
