## [05]-[NUMERICAL_QUALITY_SIGNALS]

- Arc-length machinery is the engine's strongest lane: Bezier decomposition + Gauss-Legendre (`gaussDegIncrease=17`) + monotone per-segment inversion at 1e-6 — the station family the Generation SpineRef consumes rides the BEST-verified path.
- RMF is the correct Wang-2008 double-reflection algorithm (not frame transport by finite difference) — award-grade choice, two repairable defects (NaN on coincident samples; #373 closure).
- Closest-point (curve+surface) is the WEAKEST verified lane: 5 Newton iterations at 1e-3 Euclidean acceptance, non-configurable.
- The closed/periodic axis is the engine's systemic soft spot: three closed bugs + two open maintainer checklists concentrate there.
- `FromSweep`/`FromLoft` construction is approximation-grade (rail sampled at 1e-3); factory surfaces are not exact sweeps.
- Closed-bug history (9 total, all pre-2.3.1) shows real fixes landed for `Point.InPolygon` #411, `Plane` ctor #377, `PlaneToPlane` #372, `Knot.Multiplicity` #232, `Extrema` stability #144 — the residue at 2.3.1 is the open set above, frozen by dormancy: NOTHING will be fixed upstream; every open defect is permanent until vendored.
- Perf: #391 open (no caching for dense `PointAt`/`PointAtLength` loops); `EvaluateAt` calls `RationalDerivatives` twice per U/V query (`NurbsSurface.cs:474-476`); surface `ClosestParameter` seeds by 5-deep full-surface `SplitAt` recursion — the vendored lane gains obvious span/caching wins under the kernel's SoA law.
