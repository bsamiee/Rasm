# Entrypoint Law — Distilled

[ENTRYPOINT_TOTALITY]:
- One concern exposes one entrypoint: a verb family (`Create`, `Update`, `Delete`, `Patch`) collapses into a `[Union]` with one case per verb and one total dispatch method; each sibling's preamble becomes its case constructor, shared validation becomes the dispatch prologue. Full-coverage generated `Switch`/`Map` is the totality proof — adding a case turns every full-coverage dispatch site into a compile error, so exhaustiveness is enforced at the type boundary, never asserted by a default arm. A request-union entrypoint cannot silently drop a new verb; the build break at the dispatch body is the intended pressure.
- Totality lives at the core, relaxation at the rim: `SwitchMapMethodsGeneration.DefaultWithPartialOverloads` emits separately named `SwitchPartially`/`MapPartially` methods — not extra overloads — each taking a leading nullable `@default` fallback plus one nullable per-case parameter. Distinct names mean the total and partial surfaces never enter one overload group and need no priority arbitration: dispatch internals keep the full-coverage form and break on new cases; peripheral subset call sites bind the partial method and absorb new cases through the fallback.
- `[UnionSwitchMapOverload(StopAt = [typeof(AbstractCase)])]` (`AllowMultiple = true`) generates additional `Switch`/`Map` overloads that treat the named case as atomic — exhaustive at the stop boundary, no fallback arm; multiple attributes yield multiple overloads with distinct stop boundaries. This is the deliberate partial-dispatch boundary, distinct from rim relaxation: the owner stops dispatch at a named case, callers stay exhaustive above it.
- The terminal verb-family collapse is the instruction set: verbs reified as a closed instruction functor inside a two-case recursive program union — a pure terminal carrying a result and a suspension carrying the functor-wrapped continuation. Each verb is a functor case, a program is stacked verbs under bind, and the interpreter is one natural transformation from the functor to a target carrier — run, log, mock, and optimize are four transformations of one shape, never four method families. Reach for it only when programs must be values (inspected, rewritten, re-interpreted); the request union with total dispatch is the form when they need only run.

[SEALED_ADMISSION_SURFACE]:
- The maximally sealed entrypoint composes three generator suppressions on the request union: `ConversionFromValue = ConversionOperatorsGeneration.None` (no implicit value-to-union conversion), `ConstructorAccessModifier = Private` (no public case construction), `FactoryMethodGeneration = None` (no generated factories). The only admission path is then one hand-written `public static` factory inside the partial; the only egress is generated dispatch. Input is validated exactly once at the factory, behavior is reached exactly through dispatch, and no type-system backdoor exists. `ConversionToValue = None` additionally removes downcast operators, eliminating premature-downcast `InvalidCastException` risk.
- `ConstructorAccessModifier.Internal` opens case construction to the whole assembly, not an enclosing type; when construction must scope to one owner, declare the union as a `private partial class` nested inside the owning service and keep `Private` constructors with explicit factories on the service.
- `FactoryMethodGeneration.Default` emits factories only when a member forces it (type-parameter members, `object`, interface members, duplicate member types); `None` suppresses unconditionally; `Always` emits for every member. Suppress-and-hand-write is the canonical controlled-entrypoint form over a union whose cases callers must not construct directly.

[MODAL_ARITY]:
- One entrypoint owns four modalities, and the discriminant is the value's shape, never a knob: singular is `Map`/`Bind` over the carrier; plural-preserving is `Traverse`/`Sequence` (container shape intact, effect lifted out); plural-reducing is `Fold`/`FoldMap` — monoid-keyed, not loop-keyed: a "sum these / merge these" endpoint is a `Monoid` constraint plus a projection, the monoid IS the policy value selecting the reduction, and the effectful folds add per-step state and early-stop predicates without a guarded loop. First-success is the choice fold (`oneOf`), total over an empty spread because `Alternative` supplies `Empty` — the zero-arity call is a legal value, not a guarded edge.
- The accumulating fork carries a runtime hazard the types do not catch: the applicative path merges failures through the fail type's semigroup resolved at runtime — a carrier parameterized over a non-semigroup fail type compiles cleanly and throws at the first applicative apply or traverse. Safe fail slots are an error monoid or a sequence; on a single-error rail the applicative and monadic paths coincide and the choice is invisible.
- Batch admission policy is the traversal choice, never a factory flag: one generated per-element factory paired with an accumulating carrier admits a whole batch and reports every rejection in one pass; paired with a short-circuiting carrier it stops at the first — the factory is written once, and its typed error becomes the carrier's fail slot, so accumulating traversal merges domain-typed errors through their own semigroup rather than concatenating strings. After the traversal the container is uniformly in the carrier — never a mixed container of admitted and raw elements — which is what keeps the next entrypoint's discriminant recoverable from shape alone.
- Collection-expression literals are the arity surface: the builder attribute on the carrier routes `[a, b, c]` through a type-level span-fed builder, so one entrypoint typed against the carrier absorbs zero, one, and many through identical literal syntax — arity is a property of the literal, and the builder, comparer, and conversion policy form one coherent definition-time surface on the carrier.
- `params ReadOnlySpan<T>` is the arity absorber: singular, multi-item, and empty call sites collapse into one signature; the compiler lowers a collection-expression argument to a stack-allocated span at dynamic sites and promotes constant primitive elements to the data segment with zero traffic — the allocation-free claim is a property of the target type, not the call. A `params` `ref struct` parameter is implicitly `scoped`: the buffer cannot escape without `[UnscopedRef]`, and an override must restate `params` or `scoped` to preserve the constraint, so the absorbed arity is stack-bound by construction with no caller-side lifetime burden.
- Plural and accumulating modalities are call shapes over one arm, never second bodies: `traverseM` threads the carrier's short-circuit through a batch (first failure aborts) reusing the singular arm verbatim; applicative `traverse` is the accumulating sibling that gathers every failure over a `Validation`-shaped carrier. Write-only command streams are `Applicative.actions(requests.Map(run))` — the discard is the combinator's semantics, not `_ =` at every site; `Replicate` is bounded repeat; `Between` brackets a dispatch with paired enter/exit effects.
- A stream reducing to one summary value is `foldM` with a carrier-threaded accumulator, never an external accumulator in a loop; `foldWhileM`/`foldUntilM` add an early-stop predicate over `(State, Value)`, giving the bounded-batch entrypoint with no count parameter. Fallback dispatch is the carrier's choice operator — `primary | secondary` over an `Alternative` carrier — not a boolean branch; `some`/`many` collect until the carrier's own failure is the stop condition (the carrier's `Apply` must override the lazy form or the recursion stack-overflows).

[KNOB_TEST]:
- Discrimination must be recoverable from the value — type, case, pattern, or arity — never from a parallel parameter. The rejected forms: a batch flag beside a sequence, a mode flag beside a value, a count beside a span whose `Length` already answers it. The test: if removing the parameter loses information the value cannot reconstruct, it is a capability; if not, it is redescription and dies. Capabilities orthogonal to the discriminant — a cancellation token, an environment record — describe how the work runs, not which case it is, and are the legitimate companions.
- Counts and modes needed internally derive from `span.Length` or from pattern-matching the union case at the dispatch site; they never surface in the signature.

[OVERLOAD_ECONOMICS]:
- Resolution runs three filters in fixed order: applicability, priority prune, betterness. The prune is not a betterness rule — it groups applicable candidates by declaring type, deletes every member below its group's maximum `[OverloadResolutionPriority]`, then recombines groups for ordinary betterness. A higher-priority overload wins by deleting same-type siblings from the candidate set before betterness runs; it therefore makes those siblings effectively uncallable from source even under an exact identity conversion — reachable only via delegate conversion, reflection, or unrecompiled callers. This collapses a width-fragmented surface onto one preferred entrypoint while preserving binary shape.
- Priority never crosses declaring-type groups: an applicable derived-type member excludes base members before priority is read, and a demoted (`-1`) base overload is not suppressed by a higher-priority derived one. Cross-type steering must co-locate the preferred and demoted overloads on one declaring type. Priority is read from the least-derived declaration, is never inherited across an interface seam, and applying the attribute to an override or implicit interface implementation is an error — steer on the concrete least-derived method, never the contract.
- The attribute is legal only where a method group is actually selected: methods, constructors, indexers. Properties, accessors, conversion operators, lambdas, and local functions never participate — an entrypoint family that wants priority steering cannot live behind a property getter.
- Two overloads differing only in reference-type nullability (`T` vs `T?`) are duplicate signatures (CS0111) — priority cannot arbitrate what the compiler rejects; `T` vs `Nullable<T>` are distinct and ordinary betterness already prefers the non-nullable for non-null arguments. Nullability-distinct dispatch that is structurally required uses separate names or a union input.

[COLLECTION_EXPRESSION_BINDING]:
- A collection expression has no natural type; the parameter target-types it, and betterness follows a dedicated lattice: `ReadOnlySpan<E1>` beats `Span<E2>` given implicit `E1`→`E2`; any span beats an array-or-interface target with convertible elements; between non-span targets the one with an implicit conversion to the other wins. The span-over-array preference is structural, which is why one `params ReadOnlySpan<T>` entrypoint silently absorbs every `[a, b]` call site even when array and `IEnumerable<T>` overloads are applicable.
- The lattice has an ambiguity hole: a covariant span beside a covariant array whose element relations do not compose (`Run(Span<object>)` beside `Run(string[])` called with `["<value>"]`) is a hard ambiguity. Never expose two collection-shaped entrypoints whose element types differ by a reference conversion; one span entrypoint at the most-derived element type is the only ambiguity-free shape.
- Expanded `params` form is last-resort applicability: normal form wins when a pre-built collection is passed, so the collection-typed call binds directly and never expands. Among expanded-only candidates, fewer constructed elements wins, then the span lattice applies gated on an identity element conversion — when elements differ by a non-identity conversion the tie-break declines and ordinary betterness decides, which can diverge from the bracketed-literal result. Two `params` entrypoints differing only in element type are mutually ambiguous on empty calls (`Run()`, `Run([])`): a zero-argument-capable entrypoint owns exactly one `params` element type.
- Non-array `params` collections are constructed at the lexical position of the params arguments, interleaved with surrounding argument evaluation; array `params` builds after all arguments evaluate. The evaluation order is part of the entrypoint's contract when element construction has observable effects.
- A bracketed literal cannot be an extension receiver (no natural type to bind `this`): a surface meant to be reached fluently from `[a, b]` must be a static method whose first parameter is the collection target.

[REQUEST_SHAPE_SELECTION]:
- Regular `[Union]` and ad-hoc `[Union<T1,...>]`/`[AdHocUnion(...)]` (both capped at five members) disagree on extraction by design: regular cases are concrete subtypes, so `value is CaseName c` works and no `Is`/`As` properties are generated; ad-hoc unions hold a value-index plus backing slots, so `value is T1` does not extract — extraction routes through generated named `IsTi`/`AsTi` properties. Choose regular when cases carry distinct named fields (the type hierarchy makes pattern extraction work); choose ad-hoc when the union is a positional sum of already-defined payload types.
- `T1IsStateless = true` stores only the discriminator for presence-only members (the `AsTi` accessor returns `default`), eliminating that member's allocation — use struct members there to avoid null complexity. `UseSingleBackingField = true` boxes struct members through one `object` field and is rejected for dispatch-hot paths.
- Span admission composes end-to-end for string-keyed vocabularies: every `[SmartEnum<string>]` automatically implements span-based `Validate(ReadOnlySpan<char>, ...)`; `[ObjectFactory<ReadOnlySpan<char>>]` on a `[ValueObject<string>]` adds the same span-accepting admission (`where T : allows ref struct`); a frozen lookup over `Items` with an ordinal-ignore-case comparer then routes span keys to case behavior without allocating a string anywhere on the admit-and-dispatch spine. Keyed versus keyless is the admission discriminator: the keyed form is an admission surface because the key is the factory's input — declaring it buys the whole factory, parsing, and serialization surface keyed on that primitive; the keyless form dispatches and sweeps its items but admits nothing (no key, no lookup, no factory interfaces) — the module owns all construction, callers hold items by reference, and a vocabulary whose cases need no external identity selects it losing no dispatch power.

[SURFACE_ATTACHMENT]:
- The module's public surface is the request union plus one dispatch entrypoint; the entrypoint attaches to the union via an extension block without touching the generated partial, routing entirely through the public generated dispatch surface. `SwitchMapMethodsGeneration.None` suppresses generated `Switch`/`Map` (independently for `MapMethods`) when an extension-block dispatch algebra owns the only dispatch policy — the generated methods would otherwise be a second, less-restrictive surface bypassing it.
- `[OverloadResolutionPriority]` works on extension members with the same same-declaring-class grouping: a span-preferred extension entrypoint declares both the preferred and demoted members in one static class or the prune never fires; two static classes offering same-named extensions compare by ordinary betterness regardless of priority.
