# [PY_GEOMETRY_IDEAS]

The forward pool of higher-order concepts for `geometry`, grounded in the host-free companion role. Each idea is a card — slug leader plus the capability, what it unlocks, and the gap or modern technique it draws on — and spawns one or more tasks in `TASKLOG.md`. `[1]-[OPEN]` holds live concepts; `[2]-[CLOSED]` records dispositions so an idea is never re-litigated.

## [1]-[OPEN]

[IFC_LIFECYCLE_DEPTH]: the 5D/4D model lifecycle the analysis owner is thin on — real costing/scheduling/transformation/diff vocabularies, not a single hand-rolled `NetFloorArea` fold.
- The `analysis.py` quantity arm derives one `NetFloorArea` through a bespoke `get_psets(qtos_only=True)` loop, exactly the thin single-product pattern the folder intent forbids. The `costing.py` sub-domain owns the real depth: `ifc5d` rule-driven quantity take-off across the full base-quantity schema (length/area/volume/weight per IFC class) plus `IfcCostSchedule`/`IfcCostItem` cost-tree rollup and structured cost-report export; `ifc4d` construction sequencing; `ifcpatch` recipe-driven model transformation (schema migration, georeference offset, attribute purge, sub-model extraction, merge); `ifcdiff` two-model revision comparison over `deepdiff`. One `LifecyclePhase`-discriminated owner folds the four ecosystem siblings, the quantity arm leaving `analysis.py` for the rule-driven `ifc5d` kernel.
- Unlocks the 5D-cost / 4D-schedule / model-revision dimension the folder intent names ("IFC validation/5D-quantity depth") but no owner reaches: a take-off answers the full quantity schema, a cost schedule rolls up to a total, a revision diff answers what changed between design iterations, and a patch recipe migrates or extracts a model — the construction-economics and model-management verbs a host-free BIM companion owes, all graduating through the geometry case.
- Draws on the gap that the IfcOpenShell ecosystem ships `ifc5d`/`ifc4d`/`ifcpatch`/`ifcdiff` as native 5D/4D/transformation/diff owners (all `0.8.5` companion-lane siblings over the `ifcopenshell` core, catalogued in the folder `.api`) yet none is admitted, so the hand-rolled quantity fold re-derives — badly, one key — what `ifc5d.qto.SCHEMA` owns as a rule table.

[VALIDATED_SELECTOR_GRAMMAR]: the IFC element-selection query is a validated grammar, not an unchecked string passed straight to `filter_elements`.
- `analysis.py` threads a free-form `query` string directly into `util.selector.filter_elements` for the `QUANTITY`/`PSET` arms — an unvalidated boundary where a malformed selector silently returns an empty match rather than a typed rejection. The `selector.py` owner authors a `lark` EBNF grammar over the IFC selection vocabulary (entity class, attribute predicate, pset/property predicate, classification, spatial containment), folds the parse `Tree` through a `Transformer` into a validated structured query, and lifts a parse failure into the runtime fault rail once — so an invalid selector is rejected at admission, never a silent empty result downstream. The validated query then drives `filter_elements`.
- Unlocks a typed, composable element-selection surface every analysis verb shares: a selector is parsed once, validated against the grammar, and reused across quantity/pset/IDS/clash subject derivation, so a query error surfaces as a structured fault at the boundary rather than an empty result set three arms deep.
- Draws on the gap that `lark` (the very grammar engine the IfcOpenShell selector and IDS parsers are built on) is unadmitted while the analysis owner passes raw query strings, so a selection grammar is re-derivable cleanly from `lark` rather than trusting an opaque provider-string contract — the `BOUNDARY_ADMISSION` law applied to the query that no owner currently admits.

[SCAN_INGESTION_PIPELINE]: the raw-scan preprocessing graph registration assumes but no owner produces — clean clouds are not free.
- `registration.py` admits a pristine `o3d.t.geometry.PointCloud` from nowhere; a raw terrestrial-laser-scanner or photogrammetry cloud carries ground clutter, statistical outliers, redundant density, and missing normals that wreck a registration fit. The `ingestion.py` owner is the `IngestStage`-discriminated preprocessing pipeline: `pdal`'s filter graph (SMRF/PMF ground classification, statistical/radius outlier removal, voxel/decimation downsampling, range cropping) over a chained `Reader | Filter | Writer` stage sequence, plus `laspy` COPC octree streaming and `pye57` E57 structured-scan ingestion, all folding into one registration-ready `o3d.t.geometry.PointCloud` the registration owner consumes — composing the data-branch columnar point-record bridge rather than re-reading the file.
- Unlocks the front half of the as-built pipeline the companion only owned the back half of: a raw scan becomes a clean, ground-classified, decimated, normal-estimated cloud ready for `kiss_matcher` global registration, so the scan-to-BIM deviation chain starts from real field data instead of a synthetic ideal cloud.
- Draws on the gap that `pdal` carries a full pipeline-engine catalogue (filters, streaming, mesh egress) and `pye57` carries an E57-reader catalogue, both nominally data-branch-owned for file exchange yet neither runs the processing graph, so the standard scan-preprocessing pipeline (the dominant cost of any real registration job) is owned nowhere — `pdal`'s filter stages are the unexploited admitted capability the registration owner needs upstream.

[SCAN_TO_MESH_RECONSTRUCTION]: the registered cloud becomes a watertight surface — the `reconstructed-mesh` graduation subject no owner currently produces.
- The compute graduation union carries a `reconstructed-mesh` `GeometrySubject` and `mesh-utility` repairs a GLB, but nothing reconstructs a surface from a registered point cloud — the producer of that subject is missing. The `reconstruction.py` owner is the `ReconstructionMethod`-discriminated surface over open3d: normal estimation (`estimate_normals` + `orient_normals_consistent_tangent_plane`) as the shared pre-step, then `create_from_point_cloud_poisson` (the watertight default), `create_from_point_cloud_ball_pivoting` (the detail-preserving alternative), and `create_from_point_cloud_alpha_shape` (the concave-hull variant), with `cluster_dbscan` segmenting a multi-object scan before per-cluster reconstruction. The reconstructed `TriangleMesh` exports to GLB and routes the `mesh-utility` `Repair` arm for watertight conditioning before graduating as `reconstructed-mesh`.
- Unlocks scan-to-mesh as a first-class companion verb: an as-built point cloud becomes a watertight solid the C# owner system imports, the clash/boolean engine consumes, and the deviation reference can compare against — closing the loop from raw field scan to usable BIM-ready geometry.
- Draws on the gap that open3d's full reconstruction surface (`create_from_point_cloud_poisson`/`ball_pivoting`/`alpha_shape`, `estimate_normals`, `cluster_dbscan`) is catalogued and unexploited while the `reconstructed-mesh` graduation subject has no producer — the reconstruction algorithms are the admitted capability that makes the graduation union's own subject reachable.

[SCAN_TO_BIM_DEVIATION]: scan-vs-model deviation and primitive-extraction on top of registration. (Page authored: `scan-registration/deviation.md`.)
- RANSAC plane/primitive segmentation (`segment_plane` plus iterative outlier-peel oversegmentation) extracts walls/slabs/columns from a registered cloud, then nearest-surface deviation (max/mean/std distance) between the cloud and the IFC-tessellated GLB over `trimesh.proximity.closest_point`/`signed_distance`, emitting a per-element deviation receipt with the over-build/under-build band. The deviation reference is the `tessellation/daemon.md` GLB; the registered pose is the `registration.md`/`ingestion.md` precondition.
- Unlocks construction-verification and as-built QA: the companion answers whether the built geometry matches the IFC design within tolerance, feeding deviation evidence back through graduation to the C# owner system and the TS viewer as a colored overlay.
- Draws on the gap that the package tessellates IFC and registers scans but compares them only here; the `scan-deviation` `GeometrySubject` literal admits to the compute graduation owner (cross-folder seam). Open residual: the `signed_distance` over/under band reduction choice and the cross-folder graduation-subject admission.

[COMPANION_CPU_OFFLOAD]: the heavy geometry kernels run on the runtime subinterpreter offload lane, not the companion event loop. (Trickle-down from `python:GEOMETRY_KERNEL_OFFLOAD_LANE`.)
- The registration ICP loops, the OCCT/IFC tessellation iteration, the `pdal` filter graph, the open3d reconstruction, and the `trimesh`/`manifold3d` boolean are all CPU-bound kernels that today block the daemon's `anyio` event loop or serialize through `to_process` pickle. The branch `runtime/concurrency/lanes` gains the `anyio.to_interpreter.run_sync` CPU-offload `LanePolicy` variant; every geometry owner that runs a heavy kernel hands it to that lane (the lane never imports the kernel) under one `CapacityLimiter` and one `DrainReceipt`, falling back to `to_process` where subinterpreters are unavailable.
- Unlocks true-parallel CPU geometry work without the companion stalling: a tessellation request, a registration fit, and a reconstruction run concurrently on subinterpreter-capable runtimes, and the daemon stays responsive during the 200-400 ms OCCT cold start and the multi-second registration loops.
- Draws on the cross-package `GEOMETRY_KERNEL_OFFLOAD_LANE` idea naming the geometry registration/tessellation loops as the exact heavy CPU kernels the lane must absorb; the geometry side is the consumer that hands its kernels to the lane, the lane owner staying branch-side — never a second concurrency surface minted in geometry.

## [2]-[CLOSED]

[STEP_AP242_COMPANION_HOP]: page authored (`step-bridge/cad.md`). The `step-bridge` sub-domain owns the ISO 10303 AP242/AP203/AP214 + IGES CAD-STEP tessellation hop over `cadquery-ocp` — `STEPCAFControl_Reader`/`IGESCAFControl_Reader` into an XCAF `TDocStd_Document`, `BRepMesh_IncrementalMesh` triangulation, and the native `RWGltf_CafWriter` GLB write — returning raw GLB the daemon keys, so one daemon serves IFC and CAD-STEP through one content-addressed hop. The retired `pythonocc-core` conda-only blocker is resolved by `cadquery-ocp` (sole PyPI OCP path, cp310-cp314 wheels, `<'3.15'` companion band); the C# `StepIso10303` codec gap closes end-to-end at the wire.

[TENSOR_MULTIWAY_REGISTRATION]: realized in `scan-registration/registration.md`. The one `RegistrationMode`-discriminated owner carries `GLOBAL` (the `kiss_matcher` initialization-free Faster-PFH/ROBIN/GNC bootstrap, no initial pose), `MULTISCALE` (tensor `multi_scale_icp` with robust kernels), `COLORED_ICP`, `VGICP` (`small_gicp` speed path), and `MULTIWAY` (pose-graph `global_optimization` folding `kiss_matcher` pairwise edges). The `kiss_matcher` global path supersedes the open3d RANSAC+FPFH bootstrap; a fitness/RMSE receipt graduates through the geometry case.

[ROBUST_MESH_ALGEBRA]: page authored (`mesh-utility/repair.md`). The `mesh-utility` sub-domain owns robust mesh repair and boolean as one `MeshOp` tagged union — watertight/winding/normal/hole-fill repair over `trimesh.repair`, exact union/difference/intersection over the `trimesh.boolean` `manifold3d` backend, and mesh-file decode/encode across `trimesh`/`rhino3dm`/`meshio` keyed by `MeshFormat` — the guaranteed-watertight, boolean-capable substrate the tessellation, scan-reconstruction, and step hops compose, and the clash-volume engine `ifc-analysis` composes rather than re-implements.

[IDS_BCF_MODEL_CHECK]: realized in `ifc-analysis/analysis.md`. The `AnalysisKind`-discriminated owner carries IDS model-checking through `ifctester` (`ids.open`/`Ids.validate`/`Specification.status`, BCF export via `ifctester.reporter.Bcf`), clash detection through `ifcclash` (`Clasher(ClashSettings())` over `ClashSet`/`ClashSource` TypedDicts, `ClashResult.a_global_id`/`b_global_id`), and BCF issue round-trip through `bcf-client` (`bcf.v3.bcfxml.BcfXml.create_new`/`add_topic`/`get_topics`). The buildingSMART triad is three separate PyPI distributions catalogued in the folder `.api`; `bcf-client` ships a pure-Python wheel, `ifctester`/`ifcclash` ride the companion lane. The `QUANTITY` quantity arm is superseded by the `IFC_LIFECYCLE_DEPTH` `ifc5d` rule-driven take-off (moved to `costing.py`).
