## [05]-[ADHERENCE REALITY]

The panel's premise holds here with force. Ranking the engine's demands by real vs wishful compliance:

- **Structural / high-compliance (keep, extend):** "ENUMERATE BOTH .api tiers IN FULL with a real `ls`" (`:585`) and py's STEP-0 "enumerate `docs/stacks/python/` with a real `ls`/`find`" (`:196`) force a verifiable tool action — an agent cannot fake having `ls`-ed. The discover→implement→critique→redteam succession is real structural verification: each stage re-attacks and redteam explicitly "VERIFY the critique's cross-file repairs landed on BOTH ends of every touched seam" (`:678`). The reading-map handed as JSON gives downstream stages concrete anchors, not "read more."
- **Wishful / low-compliance (the risk):** "READ … in FULL — never a partial, skim, grep-jump, or section-sample" (`:196`,`:548`) for a fable agent holding a 4-page batch under a multi-thousand-token PRE preamble is aspirational; agents trim it. "the map POINTS; you VERIFY and EXCEED it" (`:551`) is the classic agreeable-trim seam — nothing in `FIXLOG_SCHEMA`/`REVIEW_SCHEMA` forces the agent to enumerate members added BEYOND the map, so treating the map as a ceiling passes schema.
- **The weakest link — the terminal stage has no successor.** The engine deliberately deleted reconcile/verify ("cross-file ripples repaired in-pass, no reconcile stage," `:731`); redteam is last. So redteam's OWN cross-file ripple repairs are verified by nobody, and `REVIEW_SCHEMA` (`files`/`verdict`/`summary`) forces neither a per-seam count nor a both-ends anchor — a redteam agent can return `verdict: "fixed"` with a prose summary and no structural proof the counterpart landed. Given "agents trim work," the last-stage repairs are the softest guarantee in the pipeline.
- **Fix that matches the premise (structural verification over trust):** make the schemas count. Add to `FIXLOG_SCHEMA`/`REVIEW_SCHEMA` a required `seamsTouched: [{file, counterpartFile, bothEndsEdited: bool}]` and `beyondMap: [{catalog, member}]` (members stacked past the reading map). A schema-forced count/anchor is unfakeable in a way a prose "summary" is not, and it converts the two wishful demands (read-in-full, exceed-the-map) into structurally checkable outputs — and it re-creates a minimal successor guarantee for the terminal stage without re-adding a reconcile agent.
