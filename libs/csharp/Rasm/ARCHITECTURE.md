# [RASM_ARCHITECTURE]

The professional domain folder-map of the geometry/numeric kernel: the three mature co-located sub-domains (`Vectors`, `Analysis`, `Domain`) and the greenfield robust-core `Geometry` sub-domain, mirroring the eventual `Rasm.Geometry.*` source tree. Each leaf carries a one-line capability charter naming its eventual source files. The mature sub-domains are settled source; the robust-core leaves are the transcription units. Dependency direction is settled once in the branch architecture and is not restated here; the `.planning/` page router lives in `README.md` and is not duplicated into this map.

The mature siblings carry their realized capability in their own source and `Vectors/_ARCHITECTURE.md`; the robust-core sub-domains are authored in the transcription pass (the numeric floor first, predicates before the index/topology/healing/constraint/tessellation consumers, the consolidated fault family last). `Topology` and `Healing` each hold two distinct concepts in one professional domain, so each splits into two source files. `Tessellation` grounds its constrained-Delaunay owner on the exact in-circle/in-sphere predicates the numeric floor already carries; its `Build`/`ToMesh` rail is the arrangement substrate the boolean gate and the AEC-domain fabrication/nesting consumers compose. `Arrangement`, `Intersection`, `Fitting`, `Parameterization`, `Projection`, `Simplification`, and `Encoding` are the frontier sub-domains: each composes the floors already authored — `Arrangement` the constrained tetrahedralization plus the implicit-point predicates plus the GWN inside/outside scalar (the managed exact boolean retiring the native CSG gate), `Intersection` the implicit-point exact crossing tests the healing/offsetting pages spell inline (collapsed onto one owner, including the `PlaneMesh` section arm), `Fitting` the BVH neighbourhood query plus the LM iterate plus the cloud PCA vocabulary (the as-built reconstruction primitive), `Parameterization` the `Vectors` DEC operator surface (LSCM/ARAP/BFF over the cotangent-Laplacian the spectral substrate assembles), `Projection` the exact `Orient3D` silhouette/plane-side sign plus the `Intersection` section cut plus the BVH front-to-back traversal (the predicate-exact hidden-line drawing producer), `Simplification` the `Vectors` mesh operators plus the `Orient3D` collapse-validity sign plus the `topology` crease classification (the error-bounded QEM decimation), and `Encoding` the live `Vectors` mesh/cloud/SDF/curvature/geodesic readers plus the `topology/reconciliation` content-hash round-trip witness (the canonical representation packer the plugin/tensor lanes read). Each reaches its consumers through a settled rail (`Apply`/`ToMesh`/`FitReceipt`/`ChartAtlas`/`DrawingProjection`/`DecimationReceipt`/`EncodedGeometry`), never by coupling into a flat store interior, and none re-mints a floor the kernel already carries — `Projection` and the AEC-domain Fabrication HLR meet at the `DrawingProjection` carrier (this kernel owns the predicate-exact visibility, Fabrication the sheet-side composition), and `Encoding` and the Compute tensor lane meet at the `EncodedGeometry` carrier and the shared `EncodingChannel` vocabulary.

## [2]-[NAMESPACE_LAW]

The mature `Domain/Geometry.cs` owner and the greenfield `Geometry/` robust-core sub-domain share the bare token `Geometry` only at the filesystem path level; the C# namespace axes are DISJOINT and never collide. `Domain/Geometry.cs` declares namespace `Rasm.Domain` and owns the Rhino-normalization vocabulary (`Topology`/`Kind`/`CurveForm` — the geometry-kind discriminant and coercion table, NOT a type named `Geometry`). The robust-core lives under the `Rasm.Geometry.*` namespace tree (`Rasm.Geometry.Numerics`/`Spatial`/`Topology`/`Healing`/`Constraints`/`Tessellation`/`Faults`), one namespace per leaf. `Rasm.Domain` and `Rasm.Geometry.*` are separate roots, so `Rasm.Domain.Topology` (the mature object-kind smart enum) and `Rasm.Geometry.Topology.EntityKind` (the robust-core naming modality) coexist without a using-collision; the `Domain/Geometry.cs` file keeps its name and namespace, and the robust-core never re-mints the Domain geometry-normalization owner. This is the SETTLED reconciliation: no rename, no namespace re-scope, no source move — the two `Geometry` tokens are a path coincidence the disjoint namespace roots already resolve, and the robust-core transcription lands `Rasm.Geometry.*` source freely beside the unchanged `Rasm.Domain` owner.
