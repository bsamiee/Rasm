Refinement brings each lane's `00-bedrock.md` in `<WORKSPACE>` to publication quality — one agent per lane folder.

[DOCTRINE]:
- Every agent — the orchestrator and every wave agent — fully reads `<DOCTRINE>` before any other work: the laws, the collapse scan, the page craft, and what `<TARGET>` owns. That single read is the whole alignment requirement, and every finding, edit, and grade answers to it.

[REFINE]:
- Strip every line that is not a finding — meta-commentary, "this is..." or "X is..." framing, sourcing, versions, dates, coupling, and any introduction, routing, summary, sources, or validation section.
- Fix formatting: one H1, then `[GROUP_LABEL]:` groups of contiguous bullets with no blank line between items, and a blank line only around a fenced code block. Confirm the groups fit the lane, are not arbitrary, and are named for real shared content; reorganize or rename where the structure is weak.
- Consolidate holistically: read the whole bedrock as one body and fold any duplication or near-duplication that survived the merges into single combined-value entries.

[PROSE]:
- Apply the style guide surgically: front-load the controlling rule, tighten every sentence, cut filler and non-load-bearing hedges, one term per concept — never changing a finding's meaning to shorten it.
- Trim 3-10% of the content where the trim costs no finding value. Careful and surgical; nothing major, nothing structural.

[VALIDATE_AND_DEEPEN]:
- Verify every claim against the newest source. Delete what is false or stale, correct what is wrong, and remove low-value noise — but dig a table-stakes finding deeper before cutting it when real depth exists.
- Depth the page may never print is still value — the bedrock is mined again at enrichment and snippet time; cut noise, never reach.

[GRADE]:
- Score the content one to ten on four axes — richness, veracity, density, advancement — and on relevance to the lane, against a bar of 9.2 on each. Grading is critical, harsh, but fair — not a 9.2 out of convenience, not low for nit-picking. Rework until it clears.
