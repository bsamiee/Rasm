### 2. Owner/Cases/Entry/Auto/Packages/Growth/Boundary schema drives 4-6x intra-page invariant restatement
The per-region block schema structurally invites stating one invariant in Owner AND Auto AND Growth AND Boundary AND RESEARCH. Evidence: clock's packed-key/rsplit point (7 sites) and Meta-gate point (7 sites); identity's single-mint law (5 sites); admission's clock-consumption/single-mint (multiple). Dedupe policy needed: each invariant stated ONCE in its owning block; `Boundary` lists the deleted FORM without re-arguing the rationale already in `Auto`/`Entry`.

### 3. Recital of sibling-owner vocabulary is a proven drift vector
admission.md recites resilience's `RetryClass` member set and "must-add SECRET" — both now stale/false on disk (see admission finding). Rule: a page names a sibling owner's SYMBOL it consumes, never enumerates the sibling's closed vocabulary or asserts what the sibling "does not yet have." lanes' `WORKER_BAND` comment enumerating sibling-package interior folders is the same class.

### 4. Duplicate-mechanism where one aspect belongs
identity's `key` vs `of` fork the `content.derive` span/attribute/status mechanism (should compose one `derived`/`_span_fold` core). This is the one place the corpus violates its own "one owner per mechanism" law that it enforces everywhere else (contrast lanes' single `drained` aspect feeding both metrics+receipts).

### 5. Dead / unwired carriers

### 6. Coverage-vs-charter: ApiCatalogue.reflect
