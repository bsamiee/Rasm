# Derivation Closure Under Projection

[THE_LATTICE_HAS_TWO_FLOORS_AND_ONE_DIRECTION]:
- The projection family forms a lattice with two floors and a one-way descent between them: the UPPER floor is the field-record owner (`Struct<Fields>`, carrying `.fields` and the method `.pick`/`.omit`), the LOWER floor is the triple owner (`SchemaClass<A, I, R>`, carrying only the codec), and every projection is classified by which floor its RESULT lands on — a result that re-states the field RECORD (`Struct<Simplify<Pick<Fields, Keys[number]>>>`) stays on the upper floor and admits a next projection; a result that projects only the type maps (`SchemaClass<Simplify<Pick<A, Keys[number]>>, ...>`) has descended and cannot climb back.
- The descent is irreversible without re-declaration: nothing in the surface lifts a `SchemaClass` back to a `Struct`, so the field map is capital the upper-floor form conserves and the lower-floor form spends — and the closure law is that a chain stays solvent only while every link conserves the record, the moment one link spends it the remaining links are forced to the free forms over a `keyof A` key set they can no longer derive from `.fields`.
- The same projection verb sits on BOTH floors under one name: `self.pick(...)` (method, upper floor, `Struct` result) and `Schema.pick(...)(self)` (free, lower floor, `SchemaClass` result) are not argument-order variants but two lattice positions — so the chainability of a `pick` is decided by call SYNTAX, not by the verb, and a chain that must continue past a `pick` is written method-form even where the free form reads terser, the syntax the load-bearing closure decision.

```typescript
import { Schema } from 'effect'
const Owner = Schema.Struct({ id: Schema.UUID, label: Schema.NonEmptyString, secret: Schema.Redacted(Schema.String) })
const Public = Owner.omit('secret').pick('id', 'label')   // Struct -> Struct -> Struct: field-carrying lattice, chain stays open
type Keys = keyof typeof Public.fields                     // 'id' | 'label' — .fields survives, the key set re-derives from the owner
const Lowered = Schema.pick('id')(Owner)                   // SchemaClass: terminal, .fields gone, no second projection reachable
```

[PARTIAL_REQUIRED_KEYOF_EXIT_THE_LATTICE_UNCONDITIONALLY]:
- `Schema.partial`, `Schema.partialWith`, `Schema.required`, and `Schema.keyof` have NO `Struct`-method form — every one returns `SchemaClass` (`partial` and `required` over the mapped `{ [K]?: A[K] | undefined }` / `{ [K]-?: A[K] }`, `keyof` over `keyof A`), so optionality-flipping and key-extraction are one-way lattice exits regardless of caller; a `partial` applied before a `pick` forces the `pick` to the free form because the field record is already gone.
- This asymmetry is the closure's load-bearing edge: presence-flipping (`partial`/`required`) operates on the DECODED-and-ENCODED type maps simultaneously (`{ [K in keyof A]?: ... }` and `{ [K in keyof I]?: ... }`), a transform the field-record substrate cannot represent without re-running the modifier-token computation per field, so the library refuses to carry the record forward and emits the lowered triple — the absence of a `Struct.partial` method is the type system encoding that presence-flip is not a field-set surgery but a token rewrite over the already-extracted map.
- The order law follows: every field-set surgery (`pick`/`omit`) that must chain is placed BEFORE every presence-flip (`partial`/`required`) and every key-extraction (`keyof`), because surgery preserves the lattice and flip exits it — `Owner.pick('a', 'b')` then `Schema.partial` composes, while `Schema.partial(Owner)` then `Schema.pick('a', 'b')(...)` works only via the free form and yields a terminal node.

[EXTEND_IS_TERMINAL_AND_NON_ASSOCIATIVE_ON_THE_RECORD]:
- `Schema.extend(that)(self)` returns the dedicated `extend<Self, That>` interface — `AnnotableClass<extend<Self, That>, Type<Self> & Type<That>, Encoded<Self> & Encoded<That>, Context<Self> | Context<That>>` — which carries NEITHER `.fields` NOR `.pick`/`.omit`, so the super-schema is terminal: a field added by `extend` is unreachable to a later `pick` except through the free form over `keyof (A & A')`, and the merged field record is NOT recoverable as a `Struct.Fields`.
- The `Type`/`Encoded` slots intersect (`&`) while `Context` unions (`|`) — the requirement covariance the bedrock states surfaces here as the reason a chain of extends accumulates requirements additively but the value shape multiplicatively; `Schema.asSchema` is the only widening that re-flattens an `extend` stack back to a plain inspectable triple, never back to a `Struct`.
- Extension stacks but does not RE-OPEN: `self.pipe(Schema.extend(a), Schema.extend(b))` is legal because each `extend` accepts any `Schema.Any` as `self`, and an `extend<Self, That>` is a `Schema.Any` — so a multi-field super-schema is a left-fold of `extend` over the addend list, every intermediate an `extend<>` node, the field record erased at the first extend and the whole chain reading as one intersected triple at the end.

[EXTEND_SUPPORTED_OVERLAP_IS_A_RECURSIVE_FIELD_PREDICATE]:
- `extend` is total at the TYPE level (`extends Schema.Any` accepts anything) but PARTIAL at construction: a struct-with-struct extend succeeds only where every overlapping field-name's pair of leaf schemas itself supports extension, the support relation recursing into the overlap — two structs sharing key `k` extend iff `self.fields[k]` and `that.fields[k]` extend (two string refinements, two number refinements, two structs whose own overlaps extend), and a leaf collision on incompatible primitives (a `String` field meeting a `Number` field at the same key) is rejected at the AST merge, surfacing as a thrown construction error the type does not pre-flag.
- The supported extension catalog is the closure boundary of the merge: struct-with-struct (overlap-compatible), struct-with-index-signature (a struct widened by a `Record` adds the `[x: string]: V` arm), struct-with-union-of-supported (extend distributes across each union arm), refinement-of-struct, suspend-of-struct, and struct-with-struct-TRANSFORM where the transform's `from` and `to` sides share NO field name with the target — every other shape pairing is outside the merge algebra.
- The transform-side no-overlap rule is the sharpest constraint: extending a struct with a `transform` between structs is admitted only when the transform's intermediate field names are disjoint from the target's, because the merge cannot align a renamed/defaulted field with a target field of the same name without ambiguous double-write — so a defaulted field whose key collides with an extend target is a construction rejection, the field-rename (`fromKey`) the escape that moves the wire key out of the collision.

```typescript
const Base = Schema.Struct({ tag: Schema.Literal('a', 'b'), size: Schema.Number.pipe(Schema.greaterThan(0)) })
const Refine = Schema.Struct({ size: Schema.Number.pipe(Schema.lessThan(100)) })   // overlap key `size`: two number refinements DO extend
const Bounded = Base.pipe(Schema.extend(Refine))                                   // size: >0 AND <100, the overlap merged
const Widened = Bounded.pipe(Schema.extend(Schema.Record({ key: Schema.String, value: Schema.Unknown })))   // struct + index sig
type Whole = Schema.Schema.Type<typeof Widened>   // { tag; size } & { [x: string]: unknown } — intersected, .fields gone
```

[HASFIELDS_IS_THE_BRIDGE_BACK_INTO_THE_CLASS_OWNER]:
- A `Schema.Class` does NOT accept a `SchemaClass` or an `extend<>` as its field source — its argument is `Fields | HasFields<Fields>` where `HasFields<Fields> = Struct<Fields> | { readonly [RefineSchemaId]: HasFields<Fields> }`, so the only schema-valued inputs that promote to a class are a `Struct` and a struct-rooted REFINEMENT (recursively unwrapped through `RefineSchemaId`); the lowered `pick`/`omit`/`partial`/`extend` results are none of these and cannot seed a class.
- The bridge is the `refine<A, From>` interface carrying `readonly [RefineSchemaId]: From` and `filter<From> extends refine<Schema.Type<From>, From>` — a `Schema.filter` over a struct keeps the underlying `Struct` reachable at the `RefineSchemaId` slot, so `Schema.Class(Owner.pipe(Schema.filter(p)))` promotes by `HasFields` peeling the refinement to find the `Struct<Fields>` beneath, while `Schema.Class(Schema.partial(Owner))` does not type-check because `partial`'s `SchemaClass` result has no `RefineSchemaId` and is not a `Struct`.
- This is the closure's re-entry rule: the path from a projected sub-shape back into the rich class-owner lattice runs ONLY through forms that conserve the field record (`Struct.pick`/`.omit`) or wrap it recoverably (`filter` over a struct) — a sub-shape needing to become a class is built by `Owner.pick(...)` (still a `Struct`) fed to `Schema.Class`, never by `Schema.pick(...)(Owner)` whose `SchemaClass` is a dead end for class promotion.

```typescript
const Owner = Schema.Struct({ id: Schema.UUID, qty: Schema.Number, note: Schema.String })
class Sub extends Schema.Class<Sub>('Sub')(Owner.pick('id', 'qty')) {}              // Struct.pick stays a Struct -> HasFields admits it
class Guarded extends Schema.Class<Guarded>('Guarded')(
    Owner.pipe(Schema.filter((o) => o.qty > 0)),                                    // filter carries RefineSchemaId -> HasFields peels to the Struct
) {}
// class Dead extends Schema.Class<Dead>('Dead')(Schema.pick('id')(Owner)) {}       // REJECT: SchemaClass is neither Struct nor RefineSchemaId-bearing
```

[CLASS_PROJECTS_ONLY_DOWNWARD_THROUGH_EXTEND]:
- A `Schema.Class` exposes `.fields` and `.extend(id)(newFields)` but NO `.pick`/`.omit` — the class lattice flows downward (extend adds fields, deriving `Class<Extended, Fields & NewFields, ...>` inheriting prototype and methods) and never narrows in place; a sub-projection of a class is taken off `Class.fields` fed back to a fresh `Schema.Struct`, the class's own decode transformation (`ast: AST.Transformation`) being the reason no method narrows it — a class IS a transform from field-record to instance, and `pick`/`omit` cannot slice a transformation's `to`-side instance shape.
- `Class.extend` is the in-family widener distinct from free `Schema.extend`: it threads `I & Struct.Encoded<NewFields>`, `R | Struct.Context<NewFields>`, `C & Struct.Constructor<NewFields>`, and `Self` (the prior class) as `Inherited` — so the extended class re-derives all four slots AND inherits the base instance, where free `Schema.extend` on two classes would intersect the instance types but lose the constructor and prototype; the `.extend` method is the form that keeps the class identity, the free form the one that flattens to a triple.
- The constructor input `C` and the Type slot `Fields` diverge under class extension exactly as they diverge under the bedrock's fourth-slot law: `extend` intersects `C & Struct.Constructor<NewFields>`, so a defaulted added field stays omittable at the extended class's `new`/`.make` while present in the widened instance — the projection of fields into the constructor argument is re-run on the merged record, never re-stated.

[TRANSFORM_INTERPOSITION_IS_THE_SILENT_SLICE_FAILURE]:
- `Schema.pick`/`Schema.omit` operate on the DECODED `A` and ENCODED `I` only (`Keys extends ReadonlyArray<keyof A & keyof I>`), so a key present on exactly one side of an interposed transform is OUTSIDE the legal key set — picking a key that the transform synthesizes on decode (absent from `I`) or strips on encode (absent from `A`) is a type error at the key argument, not a runtime surprise, the `keyof A & keyof I` intersection the structural fence.
- At the AST level `pick`/`omit` return `TypeLiteral | Transformation` — the `Transformation` branch is the case where the source AST is itself a transform, so the projection wraps a `TypeLiteralTransformation` rather than slicing a flat field record: the sub-shape inherits the transform legs filtered to the picked keys, the pick descending INTO the transform's `to`-side fields but carrying the `from`-side correspondence for those keys only, never flattening away the codec.
- The trap the bedrock names sharpens here: a `Schema.transform` whose `to` is a struct admits `pick`/`omit` over the decoded fields, but a transform whose intermediate reshapes the key SET (a `parseJson` whose inner struct renames via `fromKey`) projects against the post-decode field names — a wire key reachable only on the `Encoded` side is unreachable to `pick` because it is not in `keyof A`, so the projection silently sees a narrower key universe than the wire carries, the slice succeeding over the wrong key set rather than failing.

[PLUCK_IS_THE_LATTICE_LEAF]:
- `Schema.pluck(key)` returns `SchemaClass<A[K], Simplify<Pick<I, K>>, R>` whose Type is the bare `A[K]` and Encoded the single-key record — so it lands BELOW even the triple floor into a leaf, not a `Struct` and carrying no key set a further projection could read, doubly terminal: a `pluck` cannot be followed by a `pick`/`omit` (no field record) nor by a `keyof` (the Type is a scalar, `keyof A[K]` is meaningless) — it is the closure's absorbing state, every chain that reaches it ends.
- The closure law places `pluck` LAST: it is taken after every field-set surgery has narrowed the struct to the column-bearing owner, and `Schema.Array(Owner.pipe(Schema.pluck('id')))` is the canonical terminal lift, the per-row column extraction descending from the one owner where a post-decode `rows.map((r) => r.id)` would re-derive the access the schema already owns.
- `pluck` and `getNumberIndexedAccess` (`SchemaClass<A[number], I[number], R>`) are the two single-axis extractors that fall off the struct lattice into a leaf: pluck extracts one named column, `getNumberIndexedAccess` the element schema of an array — both absorbing, both the point past which no projection composes, so a pipeline that needs to keep projecting reaches them only at its end, never as an intermediate the next link must climb out of.

[KEYOF_CLOSES_THE_KEY_SET_AT_TWO_PHASES]:
- `Schema.keyof(owner)` derives `SchemaClass<keyof A>` — the runtime-validating literal-union schema of the DECODED key names — while the type-level `keyof typeof owner.fields` derives the same set at compile time; the two are one projection at two phases, and the closure rule is that `keyof` reads the decoded `A`, so a transform-interposed owner's `keyof` yields the post-decode key set, never the wire keys.
- The two phases compose: a `pick` whose key argument is constrained against `keyof typeof owner.fields` cannot name a non-existent key, and the runtime `Schema.keyof(owner)` validates an externally-supplied key string against the same set before it reaches a dynamic `pick` — so a config-driven projection (a runtime list of column names) decodes each name through `Schema.keyof` then narrows the owner, the key universe checked once at the boundary and the static `pick` proven total against `keyof A` thereafter.
- `keyof` over an extended owner reads `keyof (A & A')` — the intersected key set — so the key projection survives `extend` even though `.fields` does not, the `keyof` extractor reaching through the `extend<>` triple where the field-record methods cannot; the key SET is recoverable from the triple, the field RECORD only from the `Struct` interface.

[ATTACH_IS_THE_ONE_PROJECTION_THAT_WIDENS_ONLY_TYPE]:
- `Schema.attachPropertySignature(key, value)` derives `SchemaClass<A & { readonly [K]: V }, I, R>` — it adds the discriminant to the DECODED type only, absent from `Encoded`, so it is the inverse asymmetry of `pluck`: pluck narrows Type and keeps the encoded record, attach widens Type and keeps the encoded shape, both transforms, both terminal — and attaching a `kind` literal to each arm before a `Schema.Union` derives a discriminated union from tagless wire shapes, the discriminant materialized by the codec rather than carried on the payload.
- The closure law: `attachPropertySignature` is placed AFTER all field-set surgery (it is a transform, exiting the `Struct` lattice) and BEFORE the `Schema.Union` that consumes the now-discriminated arms — the arm's pick/omit narrowing happens on the `Struct`, then attach lifts it to a tagged transform, then the union distributes its triple over the tagged members, the three-stage pipeline reading as one declaration where the discriminant exists on the decoded side and never on the wire the producer sent.

[REACH_LIMIT_FENCE]:
- A projection earns its place only by deleting a hand-spelled sub-shape and conserving the field record where downstream chaining needs it: choosing `Schema.pick` (terminal `SchemaClass`) over `Struct.pick` (chainable `Struct`) is admitted only at the LAST projection in a chain or where the result feeds a non-struct consumer; reaching for the free form mid-chain and then re-listing the field record by hand to re-enter the lattice is the alias-to-the-derivation the closure deletes — the method form conserves the record, the free form spends it, and spending it before the chain ends is the defect.
- A `partial`/`required`/`keyof`/`pluck`/`attachPropertySignature` result is materialized standalone only at three consumers; below that the projection lives inline at its one use (`Schema.partial(Owner)` at the one decode site, `Schema.keyof(Owner)` at the one dynamic-pick boundary, `Owner.pipe(Schema.pluck('id'))` inside the one `Schema.Array`), the owner re-read at each site and the extractor the only spelling — a named alias for a one-shot lowered projection is the lattice-exit cached as a binding, the re-entry it forecloses paid for in a hand-listed field record later.
- An `extend` earns its place only by merging two field families whose overlap genuinely supports extension and whose result is consumed as the intersected whole; an `extend` over structs with a colliding incompatible leaf is a construction rejection the type does not pre-flag, so the merge is authored only where every shared key's leaf pair is co-extendable — reaching for `extend` to bolt one field onto a struct that a `Struct.pick`-narrowed sibling already carries is the field added beside the owner rather than inside it, the super-schema's lost `.fields` the price of a merge that a single wider `Struct` declaration would have avoided.
- A `Schema.Class` promotion of a sub-shape runs ONLY through a record-conserving form: `Owner.pick(...)` (a `Struct`) or `Owner.pipe(Schema.filter(p))` (a refinement carrying `RefineSchemaId`); feeding a `SchemaClass` from `Schema.pick`/`partial`/`extend` to `Schema.Class` is the rejected register — the re-entry into the rich-owner lattice demands the field record be recoverable, and the lowered forms have spent it, so the class-bound sub-shape is taken at the `Struct` phase before any lowering, never reconstructed after.
- A transform interposed before a `pick`/`omit` narrows the projectable key universe to `keyof A & keyof I` — the keys present on BOTH the decoded and encoded sides — so a projection that appears to slice the wire shape but runs after a key-renaming transform silently operates on the post-decode key set; the proof a slice is correct is the key argument being in `keyof typeof owner.fields` of the PRE-transform struct, the transform pushed after the surgery (`Owner.pick(...).pipe(Schema.transform(...))`) so the slice sees the full field record, never after it where the universe has already narrowed.
