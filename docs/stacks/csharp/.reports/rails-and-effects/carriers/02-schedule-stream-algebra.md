# Schedule As A Free Duration Stream

[STREAM_CORE]:
- A schedule is an `abstract record : Semigroup<Schedule>` whose single abstract member is `Iterable<Duration> Run()` ŌĆö a potentially infinite, lazily-realized stream of inter-attempt durations, not a loop and not a count. Every constructor and combinator returns a fresh `Sch*` case record that defers all work into the enumerator returned by `Run()`; nothing iterates, delays, or allocates the series until a driver pulls it. Policy is therefore a pure value: it composes, transforms, slices, and merges entirely in the duration domain before any effect touches it.
- The base type carries the entire functor/monad surface directly: `Map(Duration -> Duration)`, indexed `Map(Duration, int -> Duration)`, `Filter`/`Where(Duration -> bool)`, `Select`/`Select`(indexed), `Bind`/`SelectMany(Duration -> Schedule)`, and the two-arg `SelectMany(bind, project)` for query syntax. `exponential` is itself defined as `Forever.Map((_, i) => seed * factor^i)` and `spaced` as `Forever.Map(_ => space)` ŌĆö the named builders are projections of `Forever`, so any custom curve is one `Map` over the index without a new case.
- Slicing is value-level: `Take(n)`, `Skip(n)`, `Tail`, `Prepend(Duration)`, and `PrependZero`. `Once` is `Forever.Take(1)`; `recurs(n)` is the transformer `s -> s.Take(n)` ŌĆö "number of attempts" is a stream truncation, never a counter threaded through the effect. `PrependZero` (prepend `Duration.Zero`) is the load-bearing detail of every driver: it forces the first attempt to run with no delay, so a backoff curve describes the gaps *between* retries, not a delay before the first try.

[MERGE_ALGEBRA]:
- Two running schedules combine four structurally distinct ways, each a pure stream transform with its own truncation law. `Union` (`|`) yields the element-wise `min` and runs to the length of the *longest* ŌĆö exhausted operands drop out and the survivor continues; it is the "tighten and outlast" merge. `Intersect` (`&`) yields the element-wise `max` and runs to the length of the *shortest* because it is implemented as `Run().Zip(Run())` which truncates at the first exhaustion ŌĆö the "loosen but bounded" merge. The asymmetry is exact: union is min-to-max-length, intersect is max-to-min-length.
- `Combine` (`+`, the `Semigroup<Schedule>` op) is sequential append ŌĆö the left stream fully, then the right; it does not interleave or merge magnitudes. `Interleave` zips into pairs and flattens, alternating one element from each and truncating at the shorter. The four operators are not interchangeable sugar: `|`/`&` merge magnitudes at equal indices and decide endurance by extremum, `+` concatenates phases, `Interleave` alternates sources ŌĆö choosing the wrong one silently changes both the delay magnitudes and the total attempt count.

```csharp
// "five attempts, exponential gaps, never longer than 2s, never tighter than 300ms"
Schedule policy =
    Schedule.recurs(5)                       // truncate to five
  | Schedule.exponential(10 * ms)            // union: min vs the curve, outlasts
  | Schedule.spaced(2000 * ms)               // union: cap each gap at 2s
  & Schedule.spaced(300 * ms);               // intersect: floor each gap at 300ms
```

[TRANSFORMER_ENDOMORPHISM]:
- A `ScheduleTransformer` is a `readonly struct` wrapping `Func<Schedule, Schedule>` ŌĆö the endomorphism algebra over schedules. Its monoid op is composition: `f + g` is `x -> g.Apply(f.Apply(x))` (left-to-right), and `Identity` is `Transform(identity)`. `recurs`, `maxDelay`, `maxCumulativeDelay`, `jitter`, `decorrelate`, `resetAfter`, `repeat`, `intersperse`, `NoDelayOnFirst`, and `RepeatForever` are all transformers, not schedules ŌĆö they are *pending modifications* awaiting a base curve.
- A transformer applies via `|` or `&` against a schedule on either side (`Schedule | Transformer` and `Transformer | Schedule` both call `Apply`); composition of policy intent is `transformerA + transformerB`. A bare transformer coerces to a schedule through an implicit operator that supplies `Schedule.Forever` as the base ŌĆö so `Schedule s = Schedule.recurs(3);` silently means `Forever.Take(3)`. This implicit fill is the trap: a transformer used where a schedule is expected does not error, it materializes against `Forever`, which is rarely the intended base for `maxDelay` or `jitter`.
- The stateful transformers are themselves lazy stream rewrites. `maxCumulativeDelay(max)` threads a running total through the inner `Run()` and `yield break`s the instant the accumulated *applied* delay reaches `max` ŌĆö bounding wall-time, not attempt count. `resetAfter(max)` is `RepeatForever` wrapped around `maxCumulativeDelay(max)`, so the curve restarts from its seed every time the cumulative budget is spent ŌĆö the canonical "decaying-then-fresh" backoff. `decorrelate` emits *two* durations per input (a jittered-up then a jittered-down sample), doubling the series length while de-correlating parallel callers.

[BACKOFF_AS_UNFOLD]:
- The named curves are anamorphisms, not formulas evaluated per attempt. `linear(seed, factor)` carries one accumulator: yields `seed`, then repeatedly adds `seed * factor`. `fibonacci(seed)` carries two accumulators (`last`, `acc`): yields `acc`, then advances `acc = acc + last; last = oldAcc`. `exponential(seed, factor)` is the indexed map `seed * factor^i`. Because each is a stateful generator behind `Run()`, the magnitude at attempt *n* is produced by advancing the unfold *n* steps ŌĆö there is no closed-form lookup the driver must re-derive, and the seed/factor live with the curve as a policy value, never as scattered literals in the loop.
- `upto(max)` and the cron-shaped builders (`secondOfMinute`, `minuteOfHour`, `hourOfDay`, `dayOfWeek`, `fixedInterval`, `windowed`) are *time-driven* schedules: `upto` yields `Duration.Zero` repeatedly while wall-clock minus start is under `max`, so it controls *how long* a repeat runs by emitting zero-gap attempts until the window closes ŌĆö a duration of zero is a "go now" signal, the absence of a gap, distinct from the stream ending. These accept an injectable `Func<DateTime>?` clock so the same schedule is deterministic under a fake time source and live under the default.

[CARRIER_UNIFORM_DRIVE]:
- The entire retry/repeat/fold loop is implemented exactly once, on `IO<A>`, and every other carrier inherits it by unlift. `RetryUntil(schedule, pred)` materializes `schedule.PrependZero.Run().GetIterator()` into a free cons-list `Iterator<Duration>` and recurses: at each `Cons(head, tail)` it sequences `IO.yieldFor(head).Bind(_ => Catch(BracketFail(), e => pred(e) ? fail(e) : go(tail, e)))`; at `Nil` it returns `IO.fail(lastError)`. `RepeatUntil` is the same skeleton with the success/exhaustion polarity flipped ŌĆö at `Nil` it returns `IO.pure(lastValue)`. Schedule exhaustion is *failure* for retry and *success* for repeat; the stream's end is the loop's exit and the iterator's structure *is* the control flow.
- `yieldFor(head)` consumes the duration as a cancellable delay, so the schedule's magnitudes become real backoff and `Duration.Zero` becomes an immediate continuation. `BracketFail()`/`Bracket()` re-run per iteration, so resources acquired inside a retried computation are released on each failed attempt and never accumulate across retries ŌĆö the resource lifetime is scoped to the attempt, not the policy.
- Any `MonadUnliftIO<M>` carrier derives `RetryIO`/`RepeatIO`/`FoldIO` as `static virtual` defaults of the shape `MapIO(ma, io => io.Retry(schedule))`: it unlifts the carrier effect to its inner `IO`, applies the single IO-level schedule loop, and re-lifts. `Prelude.retry`/`repeat`/`fold` (and their `*While`/`*Until` forms) forward to these extensions, so an `Eff<RT,A>`, a reader-over-IO stack, and a bare `IO<A>` share identical schedule semantics with zero per-carrier retry code ŌĆö the policy value and the driving loop are both written once and instantiated across the carrier set. `Eff` retry is therefore `Prelude.retry(schedule, eff)`, never a method on the effect, precisely because the loop lives on `IO` and reaches the effect through the unlift extension, not through an instance member.
