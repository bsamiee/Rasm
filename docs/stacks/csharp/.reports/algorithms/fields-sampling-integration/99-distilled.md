# Fields, Sampling, and Integration — Distillate

[INTEGRATION_ROUTE_AND_SUBSTITUTION_LAW]:
- Route choice is the primary accuracy-versus-cost decision: four structurally distinct quadratures — double-exponential on a closed interval (absolute-error target), fixed-order Gauss-Legendre, adaptive Gauss-Kronrod with embedded error estimate and L1 channel, and tensor-product 2D/3D cubature — are not wrappers over one kernel; order is the secondary knob. Tensor-product cubature costs exactly N^d evaluations with no symmetry halving and no sparse-grid alternative, capping practical 3D order.
- Infinity substitution is an entry-path property, not an integrand property: facade routes accept infinite bounds and substitute, while the double-exponential kernel called directly with infinite bounds feeds infinity into abscissa evaluation and yields NaN weights. The facade Gauss-Legendre composes a cubic change of variable on top of the already-mapped nodes — two stacked substitutions — so raw nodes on a finite interval require the static rule entry, and the instance rule exposes only node and weight accessors with no integrate method. A caller bypassing the substitution policy silently integrates a different integrand; only a recorded substitution makes the discrepancy detectable afterwards.
- Defaults are per-entry, never shared: relative-error target 1e-8 at the facade versus 1e-10 on the direct adaptive rule. Composite Simpson hard-throws on odd partition counts with no rounding; bisection preserves evenness, so only the initial count can violate.
- Node tables are a hidden cache contract: precomputed Gauss-Legendre orders are {2–20, 32, 64, 96, 100, 128, 256, 512, 1024} and Kronrod orders {15, 21, 31, 41, 51, 61}; any other order computes nodes on the fly through a single thread-static one-slot cache, so alternating two non-precomputed orders — or parallel loops with per-task orders — recomputes on every call. Named rule instances constructed at setup each hold their own node set and bypass the slot entirely.

[INTEGRATION_EVIDENCE_FAMILY]:
- Every accuracy-bearing output is a bare scalar on an out channel or discarded — error estimate, L1 cancellation accumulator, depth and level exhaustion, slope-clamp activations, capability booleans — nine emission points for one concept, admissibility of a numeric result. The collapse is one closed evidence family whose cases carry exactly what the producing route owns: value, error estimate, L1 ratio, substitution applied, skipped-evaluation count, termination discriminant; a missing field is a type error, never a defaulted zero.
- Three exhaustion mechanisms — adaptive bisection depth (default 15, reaching ~260k evaluations at order 15), tanh-sinh refinement level (cap 10), slice-bracket expansion — all return best-so-far with no convergence flag, indistinguishable from convergence at the call site. The terminated-at-budget case carries the binding budget and the residual at termination, so truncated-versus-converged is recoverable from the value's own shape.
- The adaptive tolerance budget halves geometrically per recursion level, so a sharp local feature absorbs more than its proportional share, and the per-node clamp at numerical resolution does not restore the proportional bound; the receipt records requested tolerance beside achieved per-leaf split, exposing the gap as data instead of invisible accuracy debt.
- The L1 channel is the free conditioning diagnostic: the value-to-L1 ratio measures cancellation in the same pass, remains valid for complex-valued contour integrands because the accumulator sums absolute values, and is invisible through the short overload that discards it.
- The integrand is a partial function the surface treats as total: no route inspects returns for non-finiteness, so a signed-distance field evaluated across its zero set poisons the weighted sum silently. Admit the integrand as a guarded delegate lifting each evaluation into an absence carrier; a pole or discontinuity becomes a skipped evaluation the fold omits, counted in the receipt as coverage. Cancellation and domain-fault regimes compose in the one receipt a downstream gate pattern-matches for admissibility.
- Nonconvergence is a typed terminal case, never a best-effort fallback — binding equally on owned builds with no package surface: entropic transport is entirely absent, so log-space max-shifted iteration, debiasing, and the hard nonconvergence contract are built from scratch with typed failure evidence.

[TABLEAU_DERIVED_OWNER]:
- The fixed-step surface is the whole admitted ODE universe: second-order Heun and classical RK4 over scalar or vector state returning full uniform trajectories, and explicit multistep orders one through four with no higher-order startup phase — no adaptive stepping, dense output, event localization, predictor-corrector, or tableau injection anywhere. Adaptive integration is an owned build.
- Stage arithmetic ships as inlined literal coefficients with no structural object behind them — a transposed weight yields a lower-order method that still returns a plausible trajectory. The collapse is one tableau value validated at construction: row-sum consistency (each node equals its stage-row sum) and the polynomial order conditions are definition-time facts of the coefficients, so the factory returns an order-carrying tableau or a typed structural fault, and no integrator ever receives an unchecked table.
- Verified order is derived, never asserted: the largest integer for which every order condition holds, computed once and carried as evidence on every step. An embedded pair is two weight vectors over one shared stage matrix; their weighted-sum difference is the local error estimate at zero additional stage evaluations, and its order is the verified-order gap between the vectors — the estimate derives from the same owner that produced the step, never from a parallel code path.

```csharp
public sealed record StepTableau(double[,] Stages, double[] Weights, double[] Embedded, double[] Nodes, int VerifiedOrder)
{
    public static Fin<StepTableau> Create(double[,] a, double[] b, double[] bHat, double[] c) =>
        RowSumConsistent(a, c)
            .Bind(_ => WeightsSumToOne(b))
            .Bind(_ => OrderOf(a, b, c) is var p && p >= 1   // order is DERIVED, never asserted
                ? FinSucc(new StepTableau(a, b, bHat, c, p))
                : FinFail<StepTableau>(Error.New("no order condition holds")));
}
```

[ADAPTIVE_STEP_POLICY_AND_CARRIER_STEP]:
- The scalar and vector solver overloads are structurally identical stage recurrences differing only in increment carrier — exactly the transcription-error class one generic step eliminates, and the shipped vector second-order predictor already deviates from its scalar twin. One step function constrained to the minimal additive-module operations (addition, scalar scaling, step-scaled increment) admits scalar, complex, fixed-rank vector, and grid-slab carriers; a regular-grid field advances as fused multiply-add over its flat backing through the tensor-primitive span overloads.
- The step takes two type parameters — coefficient field and state module — bridged by checked numeric creation at the single point a coefficient scales a state increment; order-condition laws stay polynomial identities in the scalar coefficient field, independent of the state carrier they later drive.
- Adaptive control is one frozen policy row, never five loose knobs: safety factor, step-ratio clamps, error-norm choice, and reject budget travel together; the next step derives from achieved error and verified order by the standard error-controlled ratio, clamped and safety-scaled by the policy — the policy owns the formula, the loop reads it off the receipt. Event localization by sign-change bracketing belongs to the same owned surface.
- Each step emits one evidence record — attempted step, embedded error norm, accept-or-reject discriminant, clamped ratio, running reject count — so a stiff region surfaces as a run of rejects in the stream, and budget exhaustion is a terminal case with residual, distinct from clean arrival at the endpoint. The receipt names which norm produced the controlling estimate: the naive squared-sum-then-root norm overflows to infinity for components near the square root of the representable maximum over the length, so large-magnitude state demands the scaled two-pass form, and an infinity in the error channel is then attributable to norm policy rather than to the integrand.

```csharp
[Union]
public abstract partial record StepEvidence
{
    public sealed partial record Accepted(double Attempted, double ErrorNorm, double NextStep, int Order) : StepEvidence;
    public sealed partial record Rejected(double Attempted, double ErrorNorm, double ShrunkStep, int RejectsSoFar) : StepEvidence;
    public sealed partial record BudgetExhausted(double Attempted, double Residual, int Budget) : StepEvidence;
}

static StepEvidence Control(StepPolicy p, double attempted, double errNorm, int order, int rejects) =>
    (errNorm <= 1.0, rejects >= p.RejectBudget) switch
    {
        (_, true)  => new StepEvidence.BudgetExhausted(attempted, errNorm, p.RejectBudget),
        (true, _)  => new StepEvidence.Accepted(attempted, errNorm, attempted * p.Clamp(p.Safety * Math.Pow(1.0 / errNorm, 1.0 / (order + 1))), order),
        (false, _) => new StepEvidence.Rejected(attempted, errNorm, attempted * p.Clamp(p.Safety * Math.Pow(1.0 / errNorm, 1.0 / (order + 1))), rejects + 1),
    };
```

[INTERPOLANT_CAPABILITY_AND_SPLINE_ROUTE]:
- The interpolation contract advertises differentiation and integration through runtime booleans and throws on unsupported calls; the capability is data the type system knew at construction and discarded. The collapse is capability as a phantom type parameter or marker-case discriminant so the unsupported call is unrepresentable. The two capabilities are independently lazy on one value — definite integration owns a cached indefinite prefix (linear-time exact segment integrals on first call, one binary search per query thereafter; pre-warm with one call before tight loops), differentiation owns per-segment derivative forms — so a composition needing both rejects single-capability interpolants at the type level instead of at the first failing evaluation.
- Definedness is a third capability axis no flag captures: the step interpolant reports differentiation support yet returns NaN exactly at sample points (the distributional jump signal) and zero inside segments; the pole-handling rational interpolant returns NaN, never a throw, when its denominator rounds below ULP tolerance. A gradient-accumulating quadrature over either poisons its accumulator silently; wrap evaluation in an absence carrier, skip empty cases in the fold, and record the skipped count as coverage.
- Spline route law: Hermite construction takes values plus first derivatives with per-interval constant-cost local solves and exact derivative matching — the route when tangents come from the field's own gradient evaluator. The modified-Akima weight term (half the absolute slope sum) prevents the zero-weight artifacts classic Akima produces where opposing slopes cancel, at the cost of importing boundary slopes into interior nodes when all surrounding differences vanish; classic Akima's degenerate fallback is a width-weighted linear tangent, not zero — fields needing zero gradient at smooth inflections route to the monotone spline, which zeroes slopes at monotone sign changes, takes the weighted harmonic mean for matching signs, and clamps endpoint slopes to three times the first interval slope against overshoot.
- Enumerable surface: boundary conditions are exactly natural, parabolically-terminated, first-derivative, and second-derivative — no periodic mode, so periodic fields mirror or extend samples manually; the shape-preserving spline trio enters through the spline owner directly, not the facade; sorted variants throw on unsorted input while in-place variants mutate shared arrays; the quadratic spline is full-capability but direct-construction-only with piecewise-constant curvature; the log-space linear interpolant differentiates but throws on integration; the transform-wrapped interpolant hardcodes a linear base and supports neither; the barycentric rational route (window order defaulting to full degree) handles near-singular data with neither capability.

[FIELD_DERIVATIVE_AND_SCATTER_RECONSTRUCTION]:
- The finite-difference step setter rounds to the nearest power of two — a binary-representable step removes one cancellation source; the relative step base is the square root of machine epsilon for all stencils, and the x-relative variant multiplies by one plus the magnitude, mattering only at large coordinates where the absolute step must track field scale. Stencil weights are exact rationals from one cubic-cost construction-time solve and constant-cost lookups thereafter; mixed partials recurse depth-first at p^k evaluations with no symmetry reduction — exponential and unavoidable.
- Multi-dimensional scattered reconstruction has no library surface: density estimation is not interpolation, and the fit and interpolation surfaces are one-dimensional. The composition is a radial-basis or polynomial design matrix into the direct regression dispatch, taking the rank-revealing route for near-dependent bases; a matrix-valued response reconstructs vector fields — gradient, flux — in one solve; weighted least squares carries per-sample confidence, and the local variant windows around a query point with an injected kernel, deleting hand-rolled windowing. Acceptance gating on rank, conditioning, and orientation stays caller-owned.
- Kernel density estimation parallelizes internally with no degree-of-parallelism cap — its threads do not compose with an outer parallel loop, and the unordered reduction makes estimates non-reproducible across schedules even when seeded; serial evaluation is the reproducibility route. Every query is a linear all-pairs scan — no spatial index exists anywhere in the surface, so pairwise statistics at scale are a design-owned gap requiring an external structure. Any continuous distribution density adapts to a kernel as a one-line lambda; no subtype.
- Field-comparison hazards: squared-distance and Euclidean routes have no scaled accumulation and overflow silently past the square root of the representable maximum over the length; the Canberra route emits a NaN per doubly-zero coordinate; the cosine route never guards zero vectors; the correlation route is the safe choice for possibly-constant series because its two-pass form returns NaN explicitly. The naive span softmax omits max-subtraction and overflows above the exponent ceiling — subtract the span maximum first. Custom per-element kernels stride-walk flat backings calling primitive span overloads directly — the operator-injection interface is internal — while tensor spans with derived strides give zero-copy axis slices for plane-by-plane integration without copying.

[SAMPLING_DETERMINISM_AND_ACCEPTANCE_DIAGNOSTICS]:
- Determinism is opt-in at every layer: the default source is per-thread entropy-seeded and non-deterministic across runs; the bulk array fill switches at length 2048 to parallel blocks each freshly entropy-seeded — non-deterministic regardless of any seeding; deterministic sampling constructs distributions over an explicit seeded generator. The two state-serializable generators checkpoint mid-sequence and resume exactly — the route for resumable field sampling; the thread-safe instance flag costs 2.5x per draw; a seeded in-place shuffle yields a deterministic, serializable traversal order, and the lazy variant avoids materialization when only a prefix is consumed. Grid initialization fills a tensor span in place over a flat backing, avoiding per-element generation.
- Sampler contracts are caller-owned: rejection sampling throws at runtime on any point where the proposal fails to dominate, with no diagnostic of the violating point — re-verify the envelope after every target modification. The Hamiltonian sampler's default gradient is a centered difference with a hardcoded absolute floor of one thousandth that dominates whenever the coordinate magnitude is below a thousand — supplying the gradient delegate is mandatory for sharp-gradient or large-scale fields. Slice-sampler bracket expansion has no budget, so near-flat log-density grows the bracket unboundedly; scale is mutable between draws for adaptation, burn-in runs synchronously in the constructor, and the acceptance rate reads zero before the first draw. The uphill-accept branch — the minimum of zero and the log ratio equaling zero — is the primary acceptance path on log-concave targets, not dead code.
- Diagnostics have approximation regimes: the effective-size estimate uses lag-1 autocorrelation only, overestimating effective samples under the long-range positive autocorrelation typical of large-step Hamiltonian chains; the transform-based autocorrelation route delivers the full spectrum in log-linear time but zero-mean-centers and normalizes so lag zero is one by construction — multiply by the sample variance for unnormalized values — and zero-pads to twice the next power of two for acyclic convolution; the two surfaces accept different shapes and do not compose.
- Acceptance-gate statistics: the quantile default is the median-unbiased R-8 definition; the custom surface covers R1 through R9 with named aliases plus a four-parameter convention form, and the function-returning extension materializes one sorted array, amortizing the sort across all probes. Running statistics combine exactly across workers — parallel online moments through fourth order, serializable for distributed aggregation — but a single pushed NaN permanently poisons extrema and all moments with no reset; guard the stream. Random subset generation forks on density between a sparse retry path and a full-permutation path; index sampling from spaces beyond the int range must take the big-integer overload — the int overload overflows silently.

[QUASIRANDOM_FAMILY_AND_NET_RECURRENCE]:
- No low-discrepancy surface exists — only pseudorandom generators — so quasi-Monte Carlo is an owned build whose design pressure is the closed family, the policy vocabulary, and the evidence a deterministic point set owes. Independent and equidistributed draws are not two cases of one sampler: variance law, error bars, and convergence rate all fork on the discriminant, and the state shapes do not unify — serializable continuation versus index plus randomization seed — so the admitting boundary carries the discriminant as a type.
- The base-2 net interior is integer, not floating: each coordinate is a left-justified fixed-point word accumulated by exclusive-or against direction words, divided into the unit interval exactly once at the boundary — the precondition for every randomizer, since digital shifts and matrix scrambles are bit operations unrepresentable on the divided double. The fast advance is derived, never enumerated: in reflected-binary order the successor differs in exactly one direction word, indexed by the trailing-zero count of the complemented prior index — one intrinsic and one exclusive-or per dimension — and a full power-of-two block equals the unordered net exactly.
- Direction words derive from a primitive polynomial and odd initial numerators expanded by a fixed shift-and-xor recurrence; the oddness-and-bound constraint is a definition-time admissibility law whose violation silently destroys the net property, so the table admits through a validating factory returning the generator or a typed structural fault, never a raw coefficient array. Resolution bits are bounded by the word width and effectively by the floating mantissa — beyond it, low words fall off the representable grid and buy resolution only for the randomizers, never for the divided value.
- The rank-one lattice is the family's second case, selected by integrand regime and sharing the replicate return: its state is one generating vector plus a count, its randomization one uniform shift of the whole set; a periodic integrand of smoothness order α converges at the count to the negative α, beating the net's near-linear ceiling, but non-periodic integrands must periodize first — tent or baker transform — or the rate is lost. The generating vector is a greedy component-by-component construction output validated against a smoothness-weighted error functional, never a stored magic constant, and net-versus-lattice is one vocabulary key — bounded-variation versus periodic-smooth — not two entrypoints.

```csharp
static (ulong[] State, double[] Point) Next(ulong[] state, ulong[][] directions, int priorIndex, int bits)
{
    var c = BitOperations.TrailingZeroCount(~(uint)priorIndex);      // rightmost zero bit of the prior index
    var advanced = state.Select((s, d) => s ^ directions[d][c]).ToArray();
    var scale = 1.0 / (1UL << bits);
    return (advanced, advanced.Select(s => s * scale).ToArray());    // divide once, at the boundary
}
```

[SCRAMBLE_BALANCE_AND_CONSTRUCTION_POLICY]:
- Equidistribution holds only at power-of-base counts; non-power prefixes, dropped origin points, and strided thinning all degrade discrepancy with no diagnostic. The count is therefore not a free integer: the draw entrypoint accepts the block exponent, making an unbalanced request unspellable — a caller needing more points jumps to the next exponent, never tops up a remainder.
- The scramble is a policy row fixing a variance law, not a boolean: unscrambled is deterministic with no error estimate at near-inverse-count rate with a logarithmic dimension penalty; a digital shift restores an unbiased estimator at the same leading rate; a linear matrix scramble with shift, and the full nested permutation scramble, both lift smooth-integrand convergence to the inverse three-halves power and guarantee never-worse-than-independent sampling — the matrix scramble pays one nonsingular lower-triangular bit-matrix per dimension, the nested scramble a permutation tree geometric in resolution. Extensibility across exponents survives only the shift randomizer, so progressive growth with error bars selects the shift specifically — the row carries variance behavior and extensibility together so neither is silently lost.
- Path construction is orthogonal to the scramble and is the lever for high nominal dimension: the net's early coordinates are the best-equidistributed, so routing the integrand's dominant variance into them collapses effective dimension — incremental construction spreads variance evenly, bridge construction routes endpoint and recursive midpoints early, and the eigen-ordered construction sorts covariance factors by descending eigenvalue, variance-optimal for truncation among linear constructions and a derived ordering from one decomposition, never a hand-tuned permutation. The documented non-monotonicity — early-coordinate routing can increase effective dimension on adversarial integrands — forbids treating construction as a pure win: it is selected against the integrand's variance structure and recorded as evidence so poor convergence is attributable to the construction, not the net or the integrand.

[REPLICATE_RECEIPT_AND_EQUIDISTRIBUTION_EVIDENCE]:
- A single equidistributed estimate has no recoverable spread — the worst-case bound needs an unknown variation constant — so the only admissible return is a replicate family: a few independent randomizations of one block, the mean of per-replicate means as the estimate, cross-replicate variance over the replicate count as squared standard error, and a Student bound on the residual degrees of freedom holding nominal coverage empirically despite non-normal replicate means. Block exponent and replicate count are one allocation policy — variance reduction lives in the block, error visibility in the replicates — never two loose integers the loop multiplies.
- The replicate receipt is a distinct family from the deterministic-quadrature receipt and must not collapse into it: it carries per-replicate means, cross-replicate variance, interval half-width, and the realized randomization seeds that make the estimate reproducible. A quasirandom inner expectation nested in an adaptive outer quadrature stacks the two carriers — the inner spread becomes the outer integrand's evaluation uncertainty — and keeping them separate is what lets the outer gate see which dominates the total.
- Net quality is structural, never measured: one quality parameter of the direction table bounds discrepancy and the error constant, computable at construction and carried as evidence on every block — a poor table is constructible but carries its degraded parameter honestly, so gates reject on quality instead of discovering it as slow convergence. Two-dimensional projections are not uniformly good even under optimal tables: specific coordinate pairs carry poor projection quality with visible diagonal structure at small counts, so any surface exposing arbitrary coordinate-pair probes owes the per-pair projection figure, derivable from the table. The truncation figure — the variance fraction captured by leading coordinates under the chosen construction — travels beside the estimate, and failing the required fraction is a typed case naming the diagnosis, never a silently wide bar the caller reverse-engineers. The case family is replicated-with-interval, deterministic-with-no-error-bar, and truncation-exceeded-with-diagnosis — the unrandomized case structurally cannot carry a spread field.

[SPECTRAL_MULTIPLIER_AND_WAVENUMBER_OWNER]:
- Every constant-coefficient differential or convolutional operator on a periodic grid — gradient, divergence, Laplacian, screened inverse, fractional power, smoothing, free-space convolution — collapses to one form: inverse of the symbol applied pointwise to the forward transform. The operator's identity is its symbol, a pure function of the wavenumber vector; three sibling operators each carrying a forward-multiply-inverse triple is the collapse trigger, and a new operator is a new symbol row, never a new code path. Symbols are policy values — i·k, the negated squared magnitude, the screened reciprocal, the fractional power, the heat exponential — and composition is pointwise symbol multiplication before a single inverse, so derivative-then-smooth is one fused pass, never two bracketed round trips. Which symbol produced a field travels as evidence, since the same data under two symbols is two different operators.
- The inverse of a self-adjoint operator is the reciprocal symbol — partial exactly where the symbol vanishes, the constant mode for the bare Laplacian inverse. The screened variant is total because screening shifts the symbol off zero; the bare inverse owns a typed regularization policy — mean-free projection or pinned integral — with the receipt naming which, because the two results differ by a constant the caller cannot otherwise recover.
- The wavenumber vector is a derived owner, never the raw index: the split-spectrum ordering — ascending positives through the Nyquist mode, then descending negatives, scaled by two pi over the extent — is computed once at grid construction; hand-indexing the bin number as the wavenumber mis-drives every bin past the half length, silently applying an aliased symbol. Each axis derives from one length-and-extent pair; separable multi-dimensional symbols are outer combinations of per-axis owners, the multi-dimensional transform runs in place over a flat row-major buffer taking the extents as an integer vector, and the apply walk strides the flat spectrum computing each bin's separable symbol — fused multiply-add accumulating over contiguous spans without materializing a full symbol grid.
- The Nyquist mode is the structural hazard: at even length the extreme positive and negative wavenumbers collapse onto one real bin, so odd-order symbols zero it as a definition-time law — an odd multiplier at Nyquist is unrepresentable on the real-packed spectrum and leaks a checkerboard mode. The owner carries the Nyquist index as a known position; there is no runtime guard for the apply loop to forget.

```csharp
readonly record struct WaveAxis(int Length, double Extent)
{
    public double[] K()
    {
        var dk = 2.0 * Math.PI / Extent;
        var half = (Length >> 1) + 1;
        return Generate.LinearRangeMap(0.0, 1.0, Length - 1.0, i => (i < half ? i : i - Length) * dk);
    }
    public int Nyquist => (Length & 1) == 0 ? Length >> 1 : -1;      // -1 ⇒ no exact Nyquist mode
}

static Complex[] Apply(Complex[] field, WaveAxis ax, Func<double, Complex> symbol)
{
    var k = ax.K();
    Fourier.Forward(field, FourierOptions.AsymmetricScaling);
    var n = ax.Nyquist;                                               // odd-order symbol: zero it
    var driven = field.Select((c, i) => c * (i == n ? Complex.Zero : symbol(k[i]))).ToArray();
    Fourier.Inverse(driven, FourierOptions.AsymmetricScaling);
    return driven;
}
```

[SPECTRAL_GRID_OWNER_CONTRACT]:
- The scaling convention is fixed by the operator's definition, encoded once in the owner, never re-passed per call: the symmetric root-N-each-way default cancels only on round trips — a non-unit-modulus symbol under it scales output by the root of the length relative to the asymmetric convention (forward unscaled, inverse over N), the only option under which forward-symbol-inverse reproduces the continuous operator; defaulting it back is the most common silent error in the surface. The condition split for spectra: the convolution-theorem path — unscaled forward, then pointwise squared magnitude — is the autocorrelation and power route, while the symmetric default yields the length-normalized periodogram directly; one convention per pipeline, recorded. The real-packed path rejects the inverse-exponent option with a throw, so half-spectrum operators keep the canonical forward sign.
- A real field's conjugate-symmetric spectrum packs its non-redundant half in place: the buffer is the field length plus two at even length or plus one at odd — an admissibility law the grid owner satisfies at construction, since the transform throws on undersized buffers — with real and imaginary parts interleaved, the zero-frequency and Nyquist bins purely real in the first and last real slots; the symbol multiply walks two slots per bin over non-negative wavenumbers only. A full complex buffer for a real field doubles memory and applies the symbol to bins the inverse discards; multi-dimensional real fields mix carriers — the real transform along the contiguous axis, complex transforms along strided axes — and the apply walk must respect the mix.
- The zero-copy bridge is a real seam: column-major dense storage hands its backing array directly with swapped extents to the row-major two-dimensional kernel, while the row-major view of the same storage always copies — so the matrix-field operator consumes the column-major backing and the wavenumber owner swaps its axis order to match; a storage-order and axis-order mismatch applies the wrong axis's symbol per bin and yields a transposed operator with no error.
- Grid length is a power-of-two policy on the field owner, not a free dimension: any other length — every prime — routes silently through chirp convolution padding past twice the length and running three radix-2 transforms plus a chirp allocation, an order of magnitude per application; pad or resample at construction with the padding recorded, since the spectral derivative is then interpreted on the padded period, not the physical one. Above lengths near the square root of the signed-integer maximum, the chirp's phase accumulation switches to double-precision index products — a second arithmetic regime invisible at the call site that shifts last-bit residuals across the threshold; the fast path has one regime at every size.
- Windowing and dealiasing are spectral policy rows: the periodic window variant — phase divided by the length — is the only taper consistent with the transform's period assumption; the symmetric variant — divided by length minus one — is for finite measurements and biases the lowest bins by a one-sample asymmetry when applied before a spectral derivative, while seam leakage from a non-periodic field is amplified by the symbol's high-wavenumber weighting. Nonlinear grid products alias above-Nyquist modes back into the band: the two-thirds truncation or the smooth exponential cutoff composes into the operator's symbol as a real mask — one pass — with the cutoff fraction recorded, because truncation changes the represented bandwidth that downstream resolution claims depend on.

[SPECTRAL_ROUNDTRIP_EVIDENCE]:
- A real-symbol operator on a real field owes a machine-zero imaginary residual on the inverse; excess residual diagnoses broken Hermitian symmetry — a mis-zeroed Nyquist or an odd-symbol leak — and marks the result inadmissible. This is a correctness receipt the consumer pattern-matches, replacing a silent complex output whose imaginary part the caller must remember to inspect.
- The discarded high-wavenumber energy fraction — spectral power above the dealiasing cutoff — is the resolution receipt a downstream gate reads to decide whether the grid resolves the field; it travels in one operator-evidence carrier beside the scaling convention used and the Nyquist-handling discriminant, one value to match for admissibility.
- Energy conservation is a free cross-check: spectral power equals grid energy up to the convention and a known symbol-derived factor, so the receipt carries spatial energy, spectral energy, and their ratio against the expected factor — a wrong scaling option surfaces as a ratio off by the grid length rather than a field of the right shape at the wrong magnitude, and a mismatch beyond tolerance flags either the convention or an aliasing fold.
