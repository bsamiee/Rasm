# Dense Algebra — Distillate

[ROUTE_AS_ONE_CLOSED_FAMILY]:
- One shared arrow, five solver families: `ISolver<T>` declares exactly four `Solve` overloads — matrix/vector right-hand sides × allocating/in-place — and nothing else. Evidence is structurally non-uniform: `Determinant` on every handle, `IsFullRank` only on the orthonormal and spectral handles, `Rank`/`ConditionNumber`/`L2Norm`/`DeterminantLn` each on exactly one handle, `Inverse()` only on the pivoting handle, in-place re-factorization only on the definite handle. No shared evidence interface exists, so evidence cannot be reached through a base reference — the route's own dispatch is the single place that knows which case exposes which probe.
- Modified Gram-Schmidt derives from the Householder QR abstract base, inheriting `Q`/`R`/`Determinant`/`IsFullRank`/`Solve` verbatim and overriding only construction; it also never routes through the provider — managed-only regardless of provider state. The "six factories" are five families; a closed family that enumerates the orthonormal path as a sibling double-counts a subtype — orthogonalization method is a discriminant field of the QR case, not a parallel arm.
- The element carrier is closed-set runtime dispatch: the `struct, IEquatable<T>, IFormattable` constraint admits exactly four element types and excludes the IEEE-754 and number interfaces, so the family cannot lift to a generic-math typeclass arrow — a generic route signature is decorative; the route is written monomorphic in its real-double specialization.
- The six entrypoints collapse into one closed route union — definite, square-pivoting, orthonormal, spectral, rank-revealing — each case carrying its admission policy as data: mode, symmetricity, vectors, rank-tolerance scaling. A `bool computeVectors` or `QRMethod` parameter riding beside the matrix is arity smuggled back into a knob; the mode belongs inside the case so the route is recoverable by matching the value. The case constructor seats the threshold convention, converting five inconsistent built-in thresholds — absolute, magnitude-squared, scale-relative — into one declared convention per route.
[ADMISSION_GATES]:
- No factorization constructor performs finite checks — NaN and Inf propagate silently into corrupted factor state with no exception; the admission gate is one vectorized all-finite predicate over the flat `Values` span, with NaN-any/Inf-any early-exit variants when the rejection must name its cause.
- The definite factorization is the definiteness probe and its thrown rejection is the evidence, but the managed kernel reads only upper-triangle entries then zeroes the lower — an asymmetric input certifies only the definiteness of the upper-symmetrized matrix; symmetrize first or assert symmetry structurally, and never substitute a separate eigenvalue probe.
- `IsSymmetric()` compares entries with exact `!=` — no epsilon — so accumulation-built matrices (outer-product updates, normal-equation formation) routinely fail it, and `Symmetricity.Unknown` then silently falls to the slower asymmetric spectral path whose solve throws. Force symmetry in one in-place pass over `Values` averaging mirrored pairs under column-major layout — zero allocation; the transpose-add-scale expression chain allocates three dense matrices (~6MB at n = 500).
- The pivoting factorization does not assert non-singularity at construction: a singular input yields a handle with `Determinant == 0.0`, never an exception; the post-construction gate probes the factor diagonal for near-zero entries, not the determinant product alone.
- The Gram-Schmidt path throws only on a bit-exact-zero column norm; a near-zero norm divides through and silently fills Q and R with NaN/Inf while `IsFullRank` still reports true (|NaN| almost-equal zero is false) — the only detection is an explicit all-finite check on the factor buffers after construction.
[EVIDENCE_PROJECTION_AND_THRESHOLD_LAW]:
- One fold over the route union derives the full evidence record from five non-uniform surfaces; a case lacking a probe contributes absence for that field, and the projection is total because the family is closed — derived logic applied to a library that refused to derive it itself.
- The determinant's sign is recoverable from exactly one case: the pivoting handle computes the diagonal product negated per recorded pivot inversion; the spectral determinant is a complex-product magnitude (positive even for indefinite operators) and the rank-revealing determinant is a non-negative singular-value product. Reflection detection and definite-to-indefinite transitions must route the pivoting case for the signed slot even when another case is the primary solver — one route, two handles, one fold; the test is `det < 0.0`, never `det != 1.0`, because a rotation's determinant is only approximately ±1.
- Built-in rank thresholds diverge by construction and in opposite directions: the orthonormal full-rank test is absolute at ~10·2⁻⁵³ against R's diagonal and passes a legitimately rank-deficient system whose smallest diagonal is ~1e-8; the spectral rank squares eigenvalue magnitude against a fixed ~1.1e-15 accuracy and classifies ~1e-12 eigenvalues as zero; the singular-value rank scales tolerance to σ_max's own ULP times max(m,n) — the correct rank-revealing convention. The receipt carries the singular rank as the conditioning rank and treats any spectral rank purely as definiteness signature — the two never share a slot.
- The truncated-reconstruction threshold (max(m,n)·σ_max·2⁻⁵³) differs from the rank threshold (σ_max ULP-scaled, ≈2⁻⁵² at unit scale) by a factor of two — a boundary-singular value is retained by reconstruction yet counted zero by rank on the same matrix; the route declares one relative criterion seated in the case, e.g. relative pivot `|R[i,i]| / |R[0,0]| < sqrt(eps)`.
[RANK_REVEALING_DUAL_MODALITY]:
- With vectors uncomputed the rank-revealing handle still allocates both full factor buffers (m×m and n×n) but skips computing them: `Rank`/`L2Norm`/`ConditionNumber` evidence is live while any `Solve` throws on the absent vectors. The case's vectors field selects evidence-only versus solvable; the solve route ignores the field and forces vectors true so failure surfaces at admission, never at the arrow — the modality is recoverable from which route consumes the case, not from a second flag.
- The factored solve divides by every singular value with no zero guard and no truncation — `Solve` and `Rank` on the same handle are computed under different conventions and can disagree: rank reports deficiency while the solve divides through and explodes numerically. Truncated reconstruction is the pseudo-inverse, which builds a fresh method-local handle per call and never touches a caller-retained handle — repeated calls are safe.
- No thin solve path exists in the managed provider: the factored solve iterates the full m×m U column-by-column, so a 5000×20 design matrix allocates ~200MB of working storage.
- Singular values arrive descending by construction (insertion sort inside the convergence loop): `L2Norm = |S[0]|` and `ConditionNumber = |S[0]| / |S[last]|` hold without caller sorting; the condition number is +Inf for rank-deficient operators — guard before gating on it.
[SOLVE_AS_ONE_BIND_OVER_THE_SHARED_ARROW]:
- `Solve` is the only member all five handles share, so the solve composition is one bind chain written once over the shared arrow: admission lifts the matrix into the finite-checked owner, the route fold lifts factorization with the case's conditioning gate firing as the typed rejection, the solve binds against the arrow into a pre-allocated result, and the residual cap is the next bind demoting success when the relative residual exceeds the cap.
- No dense solve path computes a residual — the cap is always caller-built against the original operator, never the factors; column-major layout makes each column a contiguous slice, so the A·x accumulation is one fused multiply-add per column over spans.
- The in-place overload is destructive by contract: the definite and pivoting solves block-copy the input over the result buffer then overwrite in place; the orthonormal solve writes the result directly without copying — a result buffer pre-loaded with prior content is silently destroyed. The in-place overload is the rail's allocation contract for hot routes with buffers reused across right-hand sides; the allocating overload appears only at the outermost boundary where a value, not a buffer, leaves.

```csharp
[Union]
public abstract partial record FactorRoute
{
    public sealed partial record DefinitePsd(Matrix<double> A) : FactorRoute;
    public sealed partial record SquarePivoting(Matrix<double> A) : FactorRoute;
    public sealed partial record Orthonormal(Matrix<double> A, QRMethod Mode, bool Modified) : FactorRoute;
    public sealed partial record Spectral(Matrix<double> A, Symmetricity Sym) : FactorRoute;
    public sealed partial record RankRevealing(Matrix<double> A, bool Vectors) : FactorRoute;
}

static Fin<Vector<double>> SolveRoute(FactorRoute route, Vector<double> b, double cap) =>
    route.Switch(                                                                           // conditioning gate fires inside Admit
        definitePsd:    c => Admit(c.A).Map(a => (A: a, S: (ISolver<double>)a.Cholesky())),
        squarePivoting: c => Admit(c.A).Map(a => (A: a, S: (ISolver<double>)a.LU())),
        orthonormal:    c => Admit(c.A).Map(a => (A: a, S: (ISolver<double>)a.QR(c.Mode))),
        spectral:       c => Admit(c.A).Map(a => (A: a, S: (ISolver<double>)a.Evd(c.Sym))),
        rankRevealing:  c => Admit(c.A).Map(a => (A: a, S: (ISolver<double>)a.Svd(true))))  // solve route forces vectors
    .Bind(t => Capped(t.A, t.S.Solve(b), b, cap));                                          // one bind serves every case
```

[HELD_HANDLE_ECONOMICS_AND_REFACTOR_PRIVILEGE]:
- A retained handle yields unlimited right-hand-side solves at zero refactorization cost, and exactly one handle re-factors in place: the definite handle block-copies a successor operand over its held factor buffer before re-invoking the provider; every other handle demands a fresh construction per operand — choosing the case is choosing the allocation budget for any loop whose operator mutates per step, and at small n the per-construction clone is the controlling cost.
- A stream of near-identical definite operators amortizes to one factor-buffer lifetime; a step that loses definiteness throws at re-factorization, and that rejection is the regime boundary, not an error to retry. A streaming route tracking log-likelihood reads the log-determinant per refreshed factor — `2·Σ log L[i,i]` over the factor diagonal — because the plain determinant flushes to zero under underflow with no indication and its logarithm is −Inf; projecting the strided diagonal into a contiguous span turns n virtual scalar reads into one vectorized log-sum pass.
- Matrix inversion in a hot loop never calls `Inverse()`: it clones the factors and allocates a second n² identity buffer — both cross the large-object threshold at n ≥ 104; the idiom is the retained pivoting handle solving against an identity with result and right-hand-side buffers reused across calls. On the orthonormal handle the lazy `R` property allocates an upper-triangle extraction on every access while solves consume `FullR` and the eagerly materialized Q — hot-path evidence reads use `FullR`, never `R`.
- Backtracking line search over a factorized step costs one forward-model evaluation per step length and zero refactorizations — the held handle serves every backtrack. Conversely, residual-dependent weights (robust reweighting on a Gram system) change the operator each iteration: the factorization must never be cached across weight updates; re-factor per iteration, through the definite handle's in-place privilege while the weighted system stays definite — which it is whenever the design matrix is full rank and all weights positive, so the definiteness probe gates each iteration.
[ITERATIVE_REFINEMENT_FOLD]:
- Refinement earns full precision from one factorization: factor once, solve once, then loop residual → held-handle correction solve → accumulate; each step is one matrix-vector product plus back-substitution — sub-cubic against a cubic decomposition paid exactly once. The residual is formed against the original operator in working precision, never against reconstructed factors — the factors carry exactly the rounding error the correction exists to cancel.
- The loop is a state-threaded fold over an immutable record (estimate, previous relative residual, status); termination partitions into status cases — converged below the working-precision floor, diverged when the residual grows, stagnated when it fails the contraction ratio, cap-reached as its own terminal status — recoverable from the final state, never from an exception or sentinel.
- The whole capped refinement runs on four persistent buffers regardless of iteration count — estimate, action scratch, residual scratch, correction scratch — with the estimate absorbing corrections by in-place add; the right-hand-side norm is a loop invariant hoisted into the initial fold state.
- Floor, cap, and contraction ratio are three rows of one policy record, never magic constants in the loop body — lifted, the body is a pure transition over operand, factorization, and policy, and one body serves precision-promoted and precision-tightened refinements by swapping the row. The cap is a recurrence schedule; temporal backoff combinators are inert for a compute-bound loop — a delay is policy that decides nothing, the rejected form.

```csharp
static (Vector<double> X, IterationStatus St) Refine(Matrix<double> a, ISolver<double> fac, Vector<double> b, RefinePolicy p)
{
    var ax = Vector<double>.Build.Dense(b.Count);
    var bn = b.L2Norm();                                            // invariant: hoisted once
    var end = (Schedule.recurs(p.Cap) | Schedule.Forever).Run().FoldWhile(
        (x: fac.Solve(b), prev: double.PositiveInfinity, st: IterationStatus.Continue),
        (s, _) =>
        {
            a.Multiply(s.x, ax);                                    // in-place A·x, reused buffer
            var rel = b.Subtract(ax).L2Norm() / bn;
            return rel <= p.Floor ? (s.x, rel, IterationStatus.Converged)
                 : rel >= s.prev  ? (s.x, rel, IterationStatus.Diverged)
                 : (s.x.Add(fac.Solve(b.Subtract(ax))), rel, IterationStatus.Continue);
        },
        s => s.st == IterationStatus.Continue);
    return (end.x, end.st == IterationStatus.Continue ? IterationStatus.StoppedWithoutConvergence : end.st);
}
```

[CONDITION_EVIDENCE_COSTS]:
- The rank, spectral-norm, and condition queries on the base matrix surface each construct a fresh vectors-off decomposition — three independent cubic factorizations for three reads, no shared handle, no memoization; one retained handle answers all three from its singular-value vector. The cheap induced norms — one-norm, infinity-norm, Frobenius — are quadratic flat-array provider reductions that touch no decomposition; a gate that mistakes the spectral two-norm for a free aggregate pays a decomposition it never reads the vectors of.
- The square-operator condition estimate avoids spectra entirely: κ₁ ≈ ‖A‖₁ × a power-iteration estimate of ‖A⁻¹‖₁ probing sign-vector right-hand sides through the held factorization a fixed few times — one cheap norm plus a handful of triangular solves, on the same handle the production solve uses, so conditioning evidence and solution share one factorization.
- The overflow-safe vector two-norm driving every convergence test is a scalar rescaling reduction — one managed call per element, no vectorization; a vectorized sum-of-squares pass over the contiguous residual buffer replaces it only when the residual magnitude is known bounded, forfeiting the overflow rescaling. The relative-residual denominator and any tracked operator norm are invariants computed once and threaded, never recomputed per iteration.
[ZEROS_POLICY_ALGEBRA]:
- One two-state `Zeros` value derives three structural decisions — result mutability (a structure-destroying map on diagonal or triangular storage must spill into off-structure cells, so include-mode promotes the result storage to hold the fill), target-clearing convention, and traversal completeness — never three parallel knobs; the three are never set independently, and a skipping map over structured storage without promotion silently drops every off-structure result it just computed.
- The kernel overrides the hint by probing the function at the additive identity — full traversal is forced when the caller asked to include or when f(0) ≠ 0 — so a structure-destroying lambda is upgraded regardless of the declared skip: the caller declares intent, the kernel derives necessity. The one blind spot is a lambda returning zero at literal zero while non-zero-preserving elsewhere — that case must pass include explicitly; the probe handles the common case and the flag remains the escape for the pathological one.
- Clearing is load-bearing only against a reused target: a hot loop reusing a result buffer across a skipping map must route the clearing convention or prior-iteration values survive at skipped structural-zero positions.
- The scalar arithmetic family is a (lambda, policy) table whose policy column is computed from the lambda's behavior at zero, not chosen by hand: the conditional divide row — skip for a nonzero divisor, include when dividing by zero so the resulting infinities populate the structural zeros — proves the policy is data evaluated once to seat the argument, never a per-cell test inside the loop.
- The rejected boundary shape is the flag triple — clear-target, force-full, result-mutable: eight combinations whose illegal members are expressible and silently wrong — or any second boolean restating what the probe at zero already reveals; a custom elementwise operation built on this spine exposes at most the policy enum and the function.

```csharp
void DoAddScalar(double s, Matrix<double> r)   => Map(x => x + s, r, Zeros.Include);                               // f(0) ≠ 0
void DoMulScalar(double s, Matrix<double> r)   => Map(x => x * s, r, Zeros.AllowSkip);                             // f(0) = 0
void DoDivByScalar(double d, Matrix<double> r) => Map(x => x / d, r, d == 0.0 ? Zeros.Include : Zeros.AllowSkip);  // policy is data
void DoScalarDivBy(double n, Matrix<double> r) => Map(x => n / x, r, Zeros.Include);                               // 1/0 ≠ 0
```

[MAP_FOLD_TRAVERSAL_CONTRACTS]:
- Binary arithmetic discriminates on the runtime storage pair by `is`-pattern, taking the flat-array provider path only when both operands and the target are contiguous column-major storage and falling to the shared elementwise base otherwise — acceleration is keyed on operand shape recovered from the value, never on a caller-supplied mode.
- The two-operand fold is storage-pair specialized: dense-dense walks both flat arrays in lockstep, dense-diagonal synthesizes off-diagonal zeros from the builder's zero, dense-sparse advances the compressed cursor supplying zero for absent columns — residual norms, elementwise comparisons, and weighted inner products run without densifying the structured operand; the structural skip survives the fold, not just the map.
- Fold direction is a performance contract under column-major storage: the column fold is stride-one over contiguous slices, the row fold strides at the row count — per-column reductions (norms, per-feature statistics, Gram diagonal) are the cache-aligned direction, and transposing the problem to align the fold beats folding against the grain. Both folds carry a typed accumulator plus a finalize-with-count projection — one-pass mean and variance with no second normalization sweep.
- In-place versus allocating is one kernel selected by buffer identity: the transform tests source-equals-result and routes to the in-place arm, so aliasing the buffer is how a pipeline requests in-place — never a separate method name; a chain reusing one buffer allocates only where a value must leave. The indexed variants cost only per-cell coordinate reconstruction from the flat offset; traversal order and partitioning are identical to the unindexed form.
[LAYOUT_ALIASING_AND_VECTORIZATION_GAPS]:
- The managed provider contains no SIMD anywhere; its GEMM kernel pre-extracts every column of the right operand into separate heap arrays before a scalar dot loop, doubling that operand's memory traffic, and the parallelization gate sums three dimensions linearly rather than multiplying them — small-n batches clear the threshold and pay parallel-dispatch overhead, so parallelism caps at one for small-matrix batches. Provider switching is process-global under a lock, never per-thread or per-operation.
- Elementwise transcendentals — exponential, logarithm, root, trigonometric, hyperbolic — have no provider entry and resolve to a scalar map, one managed call per element: the map-side vectorization gap; the factorization-side twin is the definite kernel's rank-1 column update, expressible as one fused multiply-add per column but written scalar. Reaching past the per-element map to a span-level vectorized call must reproduce the result-storage selection and shape guard the wrapper provides, or it skips the structural-mutability decision the policy layer makes for free.
- Column-major layout is the lever: data sits at col·rows + row, each column a contiguous slice — vectorized all-finite admission, residual accumulation at one fused multiply-add per column, and diagonal projections for the log-determinant all ride contiguous column spans; row access strides at the row count. The fused form executes with a single rounding, improving inner-product accuracy over separate multiply-then-add — an accuracy gain, not only a throughput one.
- Constructing a matrix over an external array binds without copy: the definite, pivoting, orthonormal, and rank-revealing constructors clone before factoring; the spectral constructor passes the raw buffer (its symmetric path copies internally). In-place maps and provider operations mutate the external array, and two handles over one array alias silently — clone before any in-place operation on externally sourced buffers; reserve direct array binding for single-owner hot paths.
- No packed symmetric, triangular, or band storage exists — symmetric algorithms allocate and traverse the full n² array, doubling memory over packed-format conventions; the upper triangle is read then overwritten, never packed.
[GRAM_FORMATION_AND_CONDITIONING_CLIFFS]:
- Forming the Gram matrix via the transpose-multiply densifies to n×n regardless of input structure and squares the condition number; the normal-equations route is the default regression method and the most dangerous near rank-deficiency — the route choice is explicit, never defaulted. The orthonormal route handles overdetermined systems without forming the Gram matrix at O(mn²); the rank-revealing route tolerates deficiency in evidence but its solve applies no truncation.
- The damped normal system and the augmented stack are the same mathematical solve with opposite conditioning: the Gram-plus-ridge form squares κ through the densifying transpose-multiply; stacking the design matrix over a √λ-scaled identity and applying thin orthonormal factorization to the (m+n)×n operator avoids the squaring at the cost of the larger dense allocation. They are two cases of one route discriminated by a conditioning-budget policy field — when the operator's condition exceeds the inverse cap the route selects the augmented case as a derived consequence of the bound threshold, never as a caller-named entrypoint.
- The diagonal-weight fast path exists only when the weight operand is the diagonal-matrix type — per-row scaling with no m×n intermediate; raw-array weights have no fast path, and the efficient form scales each row by the square root of its weight into a working matrix before forming its Gram. The weighted normal-equations route runs no singularity check before its definite solve; the thrown rejection is the conditioning-failure evidence at admission.
- Small dense systems (n ≤ 10: per-pair precision matrices, covariance probes, moment and Vandermonde fits with large point counts) are the factorization-as-probe regime — definiteness by probe, rank by decomposition, condition by evidence at O(n³) with small constants; at n ≤ 4 the managed path beats provider-switching overhead. Near-collinear bases reject the Gram route when the condition exceeds the inverse cap — factor the design matrix directly instead.
