# [PY_DATA_IDEAS]

The forward pool of higher-order concepts for `data`, grounded in the host-free interchange role. Each idea is a card — slug leader plus the capability, what it unlocks, and the gap or modern technique it draws on — and spawns one or more tasks in `TASKLOG.md`. `[1]-[OPEN]` holds live concepts; `[2]-[CLOSED]` records dispositions so an idea is never re-litigated.

## [1]-[OPEN]

[CF_FIELD_DATASET_OWNER]: the `field-dataset` sub-domain owning the CF-conventioned labelled N-D field dataset the branch admits but no fence consumes.
- One `FieldDataset` owner over `xarray` reads and writes CF-metadata field cubes (`netcdf4`/HDF5/Zarr engines), exposing CF-aware coordinate selection, label-indexed slicing, grouped and resampled reductions, and unit/coordinate-reference metadata, materializing to the same content-keyed `pyarrow`/Zarr egress the `columnar` and `tensor` owners already speak, never a second labelled-array store.
- Unlocks first-class environmental/simulation/scientific field interchange — climate, CFD, sensor-grid, and geophysical field cubes the AEC companion ingests and slices offline — closing the one admitted-but-unconsumed package (`netcdf4`, registered for "CFD and field IO") and lifting `xarray` from a function-local `tensor/store` virtual-cube helper to a named owner with its own catalogue, the labelled-field counterpart of the dense chunked-array owner.
- Draws on the gap that `tensor/store` owns the chunk grid and codec pipeline while only its `virtual` arm touches `xarray` (function-local, banned at module level), and `netcdf4`/`h5py` ride the README package list with zero design-page consumer; the CF-labelled field concern (coordinates/labels/CF-metadata semantics) is distinct from the dense chunk-grid store (chunk bytes), so it is one new owner composing the existing tensor egress and the runtime content key, the `<'3.15'`-banded `xarray`/`netcdf4` arms dispatching to the runtime subprocess seam exactly as the gated data rows do. The present `data/.api/xarray.md` already catalogues the CF IO surface (`open_dataset`/`open_zarr`/`to_netcdf`/`to_zarr`/`decode_cf`/`groupby`/`resample`/`interp`/`combine_by_coords`), so the owner binds a re-owner pass on the present catalogue from study-input framing to CF field-owner framing, not a new catalogue authored.

[VECTOR_SPATIAL_ALGEBRA]: the in-frame vector spatial-algebra operation axis the `geospatial` owner declares but never exposes.
- Promote `geospatial/claim.md#GEO` `VectorGeoClaim` from a two-method (`reproject`/`set_precision`) shape into one `VectorOp` tagged-union operation axis owning the full `geopandas` vector algebra the catalogue carries — `sjoin`/`sjoin_nearest` spatial joins, `overlay` set algebra (intersection/union/difference/symmetric-difference), `dissolve` spatial group-aggregate, `clip` mask, `buffer`/`simplify`/`convex_hull`/`concave_hull`/`voronoi_polygons` constructive operations, and the vectorized predicate family (`contains`/`within`/`intersects`/`dwithin`/`touches`/`crosses`) — folding one `QueryReceipt` keyed by `ContentIdentity`, the in-frame `GeoDataFrame` counterpart of the columnar DuckDB-SQL `[3]-[SPATIAL]` `SpatialEngine`.
- Unlocks first-class offline vector GIS — parcel-overlay set algebra, site-context spatial joins, footprint dissolve, isovist/buffer zoning, and nearest-feature attribution the AEC site/context companion runs without a GIS host — closing the catalogued-but-unexposed `geopandas` operation surface (`sjoin`/`overlay`/`dissolve`/`clip`/`buffer`/predicates all in `data/.api/geopandas.md`, none reachable through the owner) and making the spatial concern two settled axes: the `SpatialQuery` SQL-engine row for set-against-columnar joins and the `VectorOp` in-frame row for GeoDataFrame algebra, both on the one geospatial owner.
- Draws on the gap that `VectorGeoClaim` declares CRS/units/axis-order metadata and only `reproject`/`set_precision` operations while the `geopandas` catalogue (`sjoin_nearest`, `overlay(how=)`, `dissolve(by=, aggfunc=)`, `clip`, `union_all`, `concave_hull`, `voronoi_polygons`, `sindex`) is the deepest admitted vector-algebra surface no owner reaches, the spatial-index acceleration (`GeoDataFrame.sindex`) the operation rides rather than a Python STRtree loop; the operation axis is format-agnostic so a new vector operation is one `VectorOp` case dispatching to its `geopandas` surface, never a `spatial_join`/`do_overlay`/`run_dissolve` method family.

[RASTER_COVERAGE_ALGEBRA]: the raster-coverage operation axis the `geospatial` owner declares but never exposes.
- Promote `geospatial/claim.md#GEO` `RasterGeoClaim` from a single `resample` method into one `RasterOp` tagged-union coverage-operation axis owning the full `rasterio` algebra the catalogue carries — windowed/tiled `DatasetReader.read(window=, boundless=)` streaming over `block_windows`, `merge.merge` multi-dataset mosaicking, `mask.mask` geometry clipping, the bidirectional raster/vector bridge (`features.shapes` raster-to-vector, `features.rasterize` vector-to-raster), `warp.calculate_default_transform`/`warp.reproject` reprojection over the `Resampling` enum, and `WarpedVRT` virtual warping — folding one content-keyed coverage receipt over the rasterio NumPy-array egress, the raster counterpart of the `VECTOR_SPATIAL_ALGEBRA` vector axis on the same geospatial owner.
- Unlocks first-class offline raster coverage interchange — DEM/DSM mosaicking, site-mask clipping, terrain reprojection, footprint rasterization, and the raster-to-vector contour/footprint extraction the AEC terrain/site companion runs offline — closing the catalogued-but-unexposed `rasterio` surface (`merge`/`mask`/`features.shapes`/`features.rasterize`/`windows`/`WarpedVRT` all in `data/.api/rasterio.md`, only `warp.reproject` reachable) and lifting raster from a one-method claim to a full coverage axis.
- Draws on the gap that `RasterGeoClaim` declares coverage/band/resampling/nodata/transform/CRS metadata and only the `resample` operation while the `rasterio` catalogue (`merge.merge(method=, resampling=)`, `mask.mask(shapes, crop=, filled=)`, `features.shapes(source, transform=)`, `features.rasterize(shapes, out_shape=, merge_alg=)`, `windows.from_bounds`, `WarpedVRT`) is the deepest admitted raster surface no owner reaches; the windowed/boundless read is the large-coverage streaming path (avoiding full materialization) the operation rides, so a new raster operation is one `RasterOp` case dispatching to its `rasterio` submodule, never a `do_merge`/`clip_raster`/`rasterize_features` method family.

[STAC_CLOUD_NATIVE_CATALOG]: the cloud-native geospatial-asset discovery sub-domain the geospatial/cloud-egress lane lacks.
- Add the `stac-catalog` sub-domain owning one `StacCatalog` over `pystac-client` STAC API item/collection search (spatial/temporal/CQL2 filter), folding the discovered `pystac.Item` asset hrefs into the `cloud-egress` `ObjectEgress.GetRange` archival-byte path and the `field-dataset`/`tensor` virtual-reference cube, and materializing the catalog itself as a `stac-geoparquet` columnar Arrow table the `columnar` scan and `query` engine consume — one discovery-to-egress owner, never a second object-store transport or a second virtual-cube builder.
- Unlocks first-class cloud-native earth-observation and reality-capture asset interchange — querying STAC endpoints for the imagery/DEM/point-cloud assets covering a site bbox, lazily reading only the intersecting COG/COPC byte windows through the existing egress and virtual-cube owners, and folding the item catalog into the columnar query plane — the discovery layer the geospatial lane lacks above the raster/vector claims and the cloud-egress byte path.
- Draws on the gap that `geospatial` owns vector/raster claims and `cloud-egress` owns the `GetRange` archival byte-window read while nothing discovers WHICH cloud assets cover a query region; STAC is the cloud-native standard the geospatial domain converges on, `pystac-client` the search client and `stac-geoparquet` the columnar catalog encoding, both composing the existing egress/virtual-cube/columnar owners rather than re-minting them — the one admission the sub-domain needs (owner-to-extend-first: the catalog folds into `cloud-egress` and `columnar`, never a parallel store). `pystac`/`pystac-client` are pure-Python cp315-clean module-top; `stac-geoparquet` co-resides with the geospatial source-build stack via its `pyarrow`/`geopandas` egress, module-top since `geopandas` is source-build band but not banned-module-level.

[COLUMNAR_REMOTE_GLOB]: the remote-glob/partition-prune/window scan depth the columnar scan owner under-exploits.
- Extend `columnar/dataset.md#SCAN` `ScanPlan` with the catalogued-but-unexposed DuckDB depth: a remote-glob partition-pruned scan over `duckdb` `read_parquet(glob)` with the `httpfs`/`s3` filesystem registration (`register_filesystem`), Hive-partition predicate pushdown, and the analytical window-function projection family (`row_number`/`rank`/`lag`/`lead`/`first_value`/`percent_rank`) the `DuckDBPyRelation` catalogue carries, all terminating in the uniform `pyarrow.Table` the shared `QueryReceipt` folds.
- Unlocks first-class lazy cloud-dataset scanning — pulling only the partition files a predicate touches from a remote Parquet lake glob, and the windowed analytics (ranked dedup, lag/lead deltas, running aggregates) the offline analysis companion runs — without materializing the full lake or a second SQL surface, the cloud-scan counterpart of the local lazy reader.
- Draws on the gap that `ScanPlan` exposes `PolarsLazy`/`DuckDb`/`ArrowDataset` over local sources while the `duckdb` catalogue carries the `read_parquet(file_glob)`/`register_filesystem`/window-function surface (the `httpfs` extension the spatial engine already loads on a sibling path) no scan case reaches; the remote-glob scan is one `ScanPlan` case composing the `cloud-egress` transport credentials, never a second transport or a second SQL engine.
