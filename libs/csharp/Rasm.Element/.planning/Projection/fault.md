# [ELEMENT_FAULTS]

The seam fault rail: `ElementFault` a closed `[Union]` band (band 2500) for the structural-graph, value-admission, projection, and content-verification failures every `Rasm.Element` entrypoint produces — `NodeAbsent` (an edge endpoint, a `Bake` root, or a replayed `GraphDelta` reference naming a `NodeId` the graph never declares), `RelationshipInvalid` (a `Relationship` whose endpoint node-kinds violate the seam's structural edge law — a `Compose` whose part is a non-`Object`, an `Assign` whose target is not a `PropertySet`/`QuantitySet`/`Assessment` bag, a `Void` between two type-level objects — or a cyclic `Compose` ancestry `Bake` walks), `DeltaConflict` (a `GraphMutation` that puts an already-present `NodeId`, drops an absent one, or links a duplicate edge), `ValueRejected` (a `PropertyValue`/`MeasureValue`/`Acoustic`/`MaterialComposition`/`GeoReference` admission failure — a non-finite scalar, an out-of-`[0,1]` absorption, a band-arity mismatch, a non-positive layer thickness, an unresolvable measure unit, or an unresolvable CRS), `ProjectionFailed` (an `IElementProjection.Project` or the `Assemble` fold returning a delta the structural law rejects, or a captured projector exception lowered at the boundary), and `AddressUnstable` (the `Projection/address#CONTENT_ADDRESS` `ContentAddress.Verify` re-hash dual finding a persisted non-rooted node whose recomputed content id no longer equals its stored `NodeId` — the H7 tamper/corruption gate). The band is `Expected`-derived (`IValidationError<ElementFault>`) so band 2500 IS the `Expected` `Code` and a bare typed case lifts DIRECTLY onto the `Fin<T>`/`Validation<Error,T>` rail through the LanguageExt implicit `Error` conversion — no `.ToError()` lowering hop — each case carrying the kernel `Op` operation context and a `Category` telemetry projection, and a recovery reads `error.IsType<ElementFault.RelationshipInvalidCase>()` or `error.HasCode(2500)` rather than a message substring. The legacy per-case-code + manual `Code`/`Message`/`ToError()` shape (and the `base(detail, 2500, None)` ctor that targets the OTHER `LanguageExt.Common.Expected`) is the defect this owner closes: band 2500 is a `Code => 2500` override over the parameterless kernel `Rasm.Domain.Expected`, and the typed case IS the `Error`.

The band is one of several on the in-process `Fin<T>` federation and never re-cases its neighbors. The kernel `Rasm.Geometry/Numerics/faults#FAULT_BAND` `GeometryFault` (band 2400) carries the geometry-algorithm failures lowered through its OWN plain-union `.ToError()` (it is not `Expected`-derived); the `Rasm.Materials` `Profiles/profile#PROFILE_OWNER` `ProfileFault` (2300), `Construction/assembly#PLACEMENT_MODEL` `ConstructionFault` (2350), and `Appearance/bsdf#SHADING_FRAME` `MaterialFault` (2450) carry the construction and appearance failures; the `Rasm.Bim` `Model/faults#FAULT_BAND` `BimFault` (2600) carries the BIM-and-exchange failures. So a cross-folder `Fin<T>` reads its origin from the band code, and the structural-vs-semantic split is load-bearing: the seam's STRUCTURAL edge law (endpoint-kind legality, `NodeId` resolution) is the seam's total `Switch` → `ElementFault`, while the IFC-SEMANTIC legality (containment-relating-must-be-spatial, `Void` element→opening, a `Type` may not aggregate an `Occurrence`) is the consumer's `Projection/projection#GRAPH_CONSTRAINT` `IGraphConstraint.Validate` returning `Validation<Error,Unit>` (→ `BimFault.ModelRejected` accumulated) [M3] — never a sixth seam arm, because the seam's `Switch` carries no IFC vocabulary. The content-address MINT projection is TOTAL by design (the `Projection/address#CONTENT_ADDRESS` `CanonicalWriter` canonicalizes `-0.0`/`NaN` and quantizes to `Header.Tolerance` so identity never forks), so `ContentAddress.Of`/`OfGraph` mint no fault and a non-finite scalar is rejected at its VALUE-ADMISSION gate as `ValueRejected` before it can reach the byte projection; the `ContentAddress.Verify` re-hash DUAL is the one address operation that is NOT total — a recomputed content id that no longer equals the stored `NodeId` rails `AddressUnstable` (the H7 tamper/corruption gate), so `AddressUnstable`'s sole producer is a verification mismatch, never the byte projection and never a non-finite value.

Wire posture: HOST-NEUTRAL. `ElementFault` rides the `Fin<T>` rail every seam entrypoint returns and never sits between wire and rail — the `Graph/element#ELEMENT_GRAPH` `ElementGraph.Apply` (the validated delta-replay) and `Bake` fold, the authoring `Graph/delta#GRAPH_DELTA` `WorkingGraph.Apply`, the `Properties/quantity#MEASURE_VALUE` `MeasureValue.Of`/`OfCount`, the `Properties/property#PROPERTY_VALUE` `PropertyValue.Of`, the `Composition/acoustic#ACOUSTIC_FOLDS` `Acoustic.Of`, the `Composition/material#MATERIAL_COMPOSITION` `MaterialComposition.OfLayerSet`/`OfConstituentSet` and `Composition/material#MATERIAL_PROPERTY` `MaterialPropertySet.Of`, the `Geospatial/reference#GEO_REFERENCE` `GeoReference.Admit`, the `Geospatial/coverage#COVERAGE_NODE` coverage-band admission, the `Projection/projection#PROJECTION_CONTRACT` `Assemble` fold, and the `Projection/address#CONTENT_ADDRESS` `ContentAddress.Verify` re-hash gate (`Fin<Unit>` single-node, `Validation<Error,Unit>` snapshot sweep). The TOTAL surfaces carry no fault rail and never mint a case: `Projection/address#CONTENT_ADDRESS` `ContentAddress.Of`/`OfGraph` and `Graph/element#NODE_MODEL` `Node.ToCanonicalBytes` are total (canonicalizing rather than faulting), and `Graph/element#ELEMENT_GRAPH` `ElementGraph.Topology`/the `Element` read accessors are total. The seam carries no fault-band string-comparer accessor: `ElementFault` dispatches through its generated total `Switch`, keys no `FrozenDictionary`, and a `[KeyMemberComparer]` on the fault is the deleted form.

using LanguageExt;
using Thinktecture;
using Expected = Rasm.Domain.Expected;
using Op = Rasm.Domain.Op;
using static LanguageExt.Prelude;

namespace Rasm.Element;

// --- [ERRORS] -----------------------------------------------------------------------------
// The seam structural-graph/value-admission/projection/content-verification fault band: Expected-derived so band
// 2500 IS the Expected Code and the typed case lifts bare onto Fin<T>/Validation<Error,T> with no .ToError() hop
// (mirrors the production UiFault band + the kernel-Expected BimFault 2600). The kernel Rasm.Domain.Expected
// base ctor is PARAMETERLESS (Code a virtual Error member, Message abstract, Category virtual), so band 2500 IS the
// Code as an override and Message/Category project each case's Detail/label through the generated Switch — the
// base(detail, 2500, None) form targets the OTHER LanguageExt.Common.Expected (which carries no Category to override)
// and is the defect. [SkipUnionOps] skips the generated implicit-conversion ops while the generated Switch survives;
// every case carries an explicit kernel Op; arm-specificity is error.IsType<ElementFault.ValueRejectedCase>() (the
// Error.IsType<E> where E : Error probe — there is no Error.Is<E>()), telemetry error.Category(). Construction is the
// static factory ElementFault.Case(key, detail) over the nested sealed …Case record (the UiFault.InvalidInput/
// MutationRejected pattern — never new …Case(…) at a call site, never a runtime string code), the Expected derivation
// making the returned base an Error directly so it lifts bare; an IGraphConstraint.Validate accumulation combines
// ModelRejected over Validation via Error.Combine, which a .ToError() hop would erase. AddressUnstable is the
// ContentAddress.Verify re-hash dual (H7): the MINT projection is total (CanonicalWriter canonicalizes NaN/-0.0;
// non-finite is ValueRejected at admission, never a mint fault), but a recomputed content id != the stored NodeId
// rails AddressUnstable.
[SkipUnionOps]
[Union]
public abstract partial record ElementFault : Expected, IValidationError<ElementFault> {
    private ElementFault() : base() { }

    public sealed record NodeAbsentCase(Op Key, string Detail)          : ElementFault;
    public sealed record RelationshipInvalidCase(Op Key, string Detail) : ElementFault;
    public sealed record DeltaConflictCase(Op Key, string Detail)       : ElementFault;
    public sealed record ValueRejectedCase(Op Key, string Detail)       : ElementFault;
    public sealed record ProjectionFailedCase(Op Key, string Detail)    : ElementFault;
    // AddressUnstable is the Projection/address#CONTENT_ADDRESS ContentAddress.Verify re-hash dual (H7): a persisted
    // non-rooted node whose recomputed content id no longer equals its stored NodeId — the tamper/corruption gate,
    // disjoint from the TOTAL mint path (Of/OfGraph/ToCanonicalBytes never fault; non-finite is ValueRejected).
    public sealed record AddressUnstableCase(Op Key, string Detail)     : ElementFault;

    public override int Code => 2500;

    // Message/Category project through the generated Switch (survives [SkipUnionOps]) — the production UiFault shape,
    // not a per-case override, so adding a case forces a compile-time-exhaustive arm here.
    public override string Message => Switch(
        nodeAbsentCase:          static c => c.Detail,
        relationshipInvalidCase: static c => c.Detail,
        deltaConflictCase:       static c => c.Detail,
        valueRejectedCase:       static c => c.Detail,
        projectionFailedCase:    static c => c.Detail,
        addressUnstableCase:     static c => c.Detail);

    public override string Category => Switch(
        nodeAbsentCase:          static _ => "NodeAbsent",
        relationshipInvalidCase: static _ => "Relationship",
        deltaConflictCase:       static _ => "Delta",
        valueRejectedCase:       static _ => "Value",
        projectionFailedCase:    static _ => "Projection",
        addressUnstableCase:     static _ => "Address");

    // The per-case static factories — the ONE construction idiom the whole seam calls (ElementFault.ValueRejected(key,
    // detail)), returning the Expected-derived base so the case lifts bare onto Fin<T>/Validation<Error,T>.
    public static ElementFault NodeAbsent(Op key, string detail)          => new NodeAbsentCase(key, detail);
    public static ElementFault RelationshipInvalid(Op key, string detail) => new RelationshipInvalidCase(key, detail);
    public static ElementFault DeltaConflict(Op key, string detail)       => new DeltaConflictCase(key, detail);
    public static ElementFault ValueRejected(Op key, string detail)       => new ValueRejectedCase(key, detail);
    public static ElementFault ProjectionFailed(Op key, string detail)    => new ProjectionFailedCase(key, detail);
    public static ElementFault AddressUnstable(Op key, string detail)     => new AddressUnstableCase(key, detail);

    // IValidationError<ElementFault>.Create — the string-only admission the generated converter bridge calls on a
    // deserialization reject; routes the unspecific case under a boundary-admission Op (never a default Op) so a raw
    // message never escapes the typed family.
    static readonly Op Admission = Op.Of(name: nameof(Admission));
    public static ElementFault Create(string message) => ValueRejected(Admission, message);
}

## [03]-[RESEARCH]

- [BAND_ALIGNMENT]: the `Expected`-derivation grounds against the kernel `Rasm.Domain` `Expected` (`abstract record Expected : Error` with a parameterless `protected` ctor, a `virtual Category => "Fault"`, and the inherited `virtual Error.Code`/`abstract Error.Message`), the kernel `Op` `[ValueObject<string>]` (`Op.Of` caller-member-name), the Thinktecture `IValidationError<TValidationError>` contract the `[Union]` generator emits (`.api/api-thinktecture-json`), and the production `UiFault` band (`[SkipUnionOps]` + the `using Expected = Rasm.Domain.Expected;` alias). Band 2500 is a `Code => 2500` override (the kernel base carries no `Code`/`Message` ctor slot — the `Expected(string, int, Option<Error>)` ctor belongs to the OTHER `LanguageExt.Common.Expected`, which carries no `Category`, so the `base(detail, 2500, None)` form is the defect), `Message`/`Category` projecting each case's `Detail` and band label through the generated `Switch` (the production `UiFault` shape), and arm-specificity is `error.IsType<ElementFault.ValueRejectedCase>()` (the `Error.IsType<E> where E : Error` probe — there is no `Error.Is<E>()`), the `Category` string surfacing through the kernel `FaultExtensions.Category(this Error)` for telemetry. The band sits in the federation between the `Rasm.Materials` `MaterialFault` (2450) and the `Rasm.Bim` `BimFault` (2600), distinct from the kernel `GeometryFault` (2400) and the `ConstructionFault` (2350)/`ProfileFault` (2300) bands, so a telemetry reader banding by code never conflates a seam structural fault with a geometry or BIM fault. This is the C# pattern law "fault = a closed `[Union]` deriving from `Expected`" the rebuilt sibling bands already realize.
- [CONSTRAINT_SPLIT]: the structural edge law the seam's total `Switch` enforces (`Graph/delta#GRAPH_DELTA` `LegalLink`/`LegalAssign` endpoint-kind legality over the `Relations/relation#EDGE_ALGEBRA` `Endpoints`) rails `RelationshipInvalid`, while the IFC-semantic legality a consumer layers rides the `Projection/projection#GRAPH_CONSTRAINT` `IGraphConstraint.Validate` `Validation<Error,Unit>` [M3] — the two are disjoint by design so the seam carries no IFC vocabulary and the `Rasm.Bim`-implemented constraint carries no structural-graph mechanics. The `Expected` + `IValidationError<ElementFault>` shape keeps the typed arm recoverable through `Error.Combine`: the `IGraphConstraint` accumulation folds each independent violation into a `ManyErrors` whose `IsType`/`HasCode`/`Filter` overrides recurse over the inner collection, so `error.IsType<ElementFault.RelationshipInvalidCase>()` and `error.HasCode(2500)` survive the combine and `error.Filter<ElementFault.RelationshipInvalidCase>()` extracts the matching arms — where a bare `Error.New(2500, message)` from a `.ToError()` hop would erase the arm into an untyped message.
- [TOTAL_ADDRESS]: the content-address projection (`Projection/address#CONTENT_ADDRESS` `ContentAddress.Of`/`OfGraph`, `Graph/element#NODE_MODEL` `Node.ToCanonicalBytes`) is TOTAL by design — the `CanonicalWriter` canonicalizes `-0.0`→`0.0` and every `NaN`→one quiet-NaN pattern and quantizes every measure to `Header.Tolerance`, so a sign-of-zero or a payload-NaN never forks the hash and the projection is byte-stable across the C#/Python/TypeScript runtimes sharing the one `XxHash128` seed-zero rail; a faulting address would regress the H7 cross-runtime parity guarantee (the `ToCanonicalBytes` result is also the non-rooted `NodeId.Content` mint and the `Generator.Equals` content diff, neither of which can be a `Fin`). Every non-finite scalar is therefore rejected at its VALUE-ADMISSION gate (`MeasureValue.Of`/`OfCount`, `Acoustic.Of`, the material/property/geo admissions) as `ValueRejected` BEFORE it can reach the byte projection, so the MINT path (`ContentAddress.Of`/`OfGraph`/`ToCanonicalBytes`) never produces `AddressUnstable` — routing a non-finite through `AddressUnstable` is the deleted reading, the "reject a non-finite value" capability preserved by `ValueRejected` at admission; `AddressUnstable` rides the `ContentAddress.Verify` re-hash dual ALONE (a recomputed content id != the stored `NodeId`), the H7 corruption gate the total writer's mint can never hit but a tampered or corrupted persisted node can.
