# Owner Composition Algebra

[NESTING_CLOSURE]:
- The owner kinds are closed under nesting: a keyed owner is a structural owner's member, any owner is a union case's payload, a vocabulary item carries another owner as an instance column, and a frozen table keys on a tuple of owners. Every member of a generated owner is either a primitive the language closes or another owner that closes itself, so a domain graph assembled entirely from owners has one provable totality story rather than a patchwork of validated and unvalidated seams.
- The enclosing owner inherits its members' capabilities by structural composition, never re-declaration: the generated factory initializes through already-admitted member instances and generated equality delegates to each member's own equality, so admission, comparer policy, and default-safety arrive free at the outer layer. The outer factory validates only the cross-member relations no single member can see — re-validating a nested member reads as defensive but duplicates a proof the type already carries, widens the outer factory with checks that cannot fail, and couples the outer invariant to the inner rule so that tightening the inner rule silently changes outer factory behavior.
- Admission composes depth-first with a monotonic trust gradient: constructing the outer owner forces every inner owner through its own factory first, so an aggregate of N nested owners admits raw material at exactly N seams, each owning its slice of the invariant. Once a value is the outer owner it carries the conjunction of every inner proof; interior code that re-checks any layer is operating below the gradient.
- Flattening a nested aggregate into one wide structural owner with leaf primitives as direct members is the inverse of collapse: it discards every inner owner's admission, comparer, and default-safety and re-scatters those invariants as ad-hoc checks across the outer factory. The flattening pressure usually traces to a wire shape, and the correct move is one object factory projecting the aggregate to its wire grammar while the interior keeps the composed spine.

[GENERATOR_VISIBILITY_SEAM]:
- An owner keyed by another generated owner reaches its full derived surface only across an assembly boundary, because a generator cannot observe another generator's output in the same compilation. The composition-time alternative composes at the boundary instead of in the declaration: the outer owner carries the raw key type, a separate owner validates that key's vocabulary, and one admission expression runs the inner admission then the outer. Every derived surface stays intact intra-assembly at the price of one extra admission hop — a priced trade against an unpriced, undiagnosed capability downgrade.

[DEFAULT_SAFETY_PROPAGATION]:
- Default-hostility propagates as an interface fact: the enclosing struct's generation walks the full interface set of every member transitively, so one default-hostile member at any nesting depth poisons the entire product upward through the closure, and the computed state overrides any requested permission. There is no mixed lattice — an outer struct cannot allow a default an inner member forbids — so a nested aggregate either stays struct-and-default-safe down its whole spine or takes the class form at the first default-hostile member.
- The array breach inherits down the nesting: a struct owner hydrated from pooled or array storage materializes the zero pattern of every nested struct member at once, so the liveness gate is placed once at the outermost storage seam for the whole spine, never per layer — or every layer takes the class form and the question dissolves into the null channel.

[EQUALITY_REGIME_STACKING]:
- The three identity regimes do not blend across nesting — they stack, each owner owning exactly its own level: structural identity at an outer layer dispatches into key identity at an inner layer through the member's own equality, and case identity compares runtime case first, deferring to payload equality only within the matching case. No regime conflict is possible because no owner reaches through a member to its internals.
- The case-insensitive string default is the one regime fact that leaks silently across layers: it is declared per owner and never inherits, so an ordinal-sensitive outer layer over an inner string-keyed member that left the default yields an aggregate whose layers disagree on what equal means — unequal at the outer comparison yet colliding at the inner hash. The sensitivity stance must be declared at every string-bearing layer; the policy value itself should derive from one declared accessor type so the per-layer declarations cannot drift apart — repetition of the declaration is mandatory, divergence of the policy is the defect.
- The equality-and-ordering pairing obligation does not telescope: an under-specified inner member lets hashing and sorting disagree at that layer alone while every outer layer stays consistent, and the inconsistency is invisible until exactly that member participates in a sorted container. Identity-without-order is a legal stance only when declared at the layer that holds it; no outer owner can grant or repair it.

[CONVERSION_TRUST_GRADIENT]:
- Conversion direction composes into a typed gradient: projecting an aggregate down to its innermost primitive is a chain of implicit erasures, reconstructing it upward is a chain of explicit admissions — evidence leaves frictionlessly and re-enters only through validation at each layer. Ad-hoc members keep the same polarity (ingress implicit, egress explicit), so the gradient is uniform across structural and union layers of one aggregate and a reconstruction spelled without the explicit casts does not compile.
- The single accessibility knob composes under nesting into one funnel: the outer owner can hand-write the sole ingress while each member independently token-gates its own, so the aggregate's admission funnel is private at every layer except the deliberately exposed verb — no layer leaks a public constructor through which interior code could synthesize an inner owner past its invariant.

[DISPATCH_GROWTH_AXES]:
- Exhaustiveness composes multiplicatively under nested dispatch, and the compile-break sets of the layers are disjoint because arms bind by named argument at distinct dispatch surfaces: adding a root case breaks every root-granularity site, adding a branch case breaks every branch-granularity site, and neither sweep touches the other's consumers. Choosing a consumer's dispatch granularity is therefore choosing which growth axis it is coupled to — branch-granularity consumers are immune to sibling-branch growth, root-granularity consumers immune to within-branch growth.
- Generated dispatch admits stack-only state and results at every nesting level, so a span can thread an outer dispatch whose arm re-dispatches an inner union with the same span, capture-free the whole way down — a catamorphism over a nested owner graph with zero allocation. A recursive owner folds two non-interfering ways on one declaration: case-owned recursion through overridden members never changes a call site, while dispatch-route recursion breaks every call site on growth — the placement decision is which of those two change-propagation behaviors the operation's correctness wants.
