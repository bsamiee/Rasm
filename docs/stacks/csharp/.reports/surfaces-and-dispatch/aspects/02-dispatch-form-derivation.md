# Dispatch-Form Derivation And The Trait-Default Cascade

[ONE_PRIMITIVE_DERIVES_THE_FAMILY]:
- A carrier trait declares the smallest closed set of `static abstract` operations and exposes the entire operation family as `static virtual` defaults derived from them. The reducing-traversal trait declares exactly two abstract primitives — a forward state-threaded fold and a backward state-threaded fold, both predicate-gated — and derives roughly fifty receiver-syntax operations from them: emptiness, count, existence, universal predicate, find, partition, sum, product, min, max, average, indexed access, sequence projections, head, last. Implementing the two primitives is the whole carrier obligation; the rest is the one declared correspondence projected.
- The derivation is literal, not nominal. Count derives as a backward fold incrementing an integer; emptiness derives as a forward predicate-gated fold seeded `true` that halts on first element; indexed access from the end derives as a backward predicate-gated fold accumulating an index–result pair and stopping when the result slot is inhabited; partition derives as one fold threading a true/false sequence pair. The same two primitives, threaded with different accumulator shapes and halting predicates, are every secondary map — the doctrine's "declare one primary correspondence, derive every secondary" is the carrier trait's literal construction.
- The map trait declares one abstract operation and derives constant-map and the lazy-argument map from it; the applicative trait declares lift plus two apply overloads and derives the entire effectful-sequencing surface — forward action, backward action, replicate, between-delimiters, parser actions — across eight argument-laziness permutations; the alternative trait declares empty plus the choose binary and derives the full parser-combinator suite. The abstract surface of each trait is a handful of lines; the virtual surface is hundreds. Selecting a dispatch form is selecting which trait the carrier satisfies, and satisfying it costs only its abstract core.

[OVERRIDE_ONLY_WHERE_A_FASTER_PRIMITIVE_EXISTS]:
- A `static virtual` default is the correct dispatch for every carrier; a carrier overrides it only when it owns a structurally cheaper operation. The count default walks element by element; a carrier with an O(1) size field overrides count specifically and inherits the other forty-nine defaults unchanged. The override decision is per-operation and local: the carrier replaces exactly the methods where its representation beats the generic fold and leaves the rest derived. The shape inverts the abstract-base-class obligation where every method must be supplied — here every method is supplied for free and overriding is the exception that pays for itself.
- Overriding a derived default never alters the law the default encodes; it only changes the cost. A carrier that overrides emptiness with a representation flag must still return the same answer the seeded fold would. The trait-default is therefore the executable specification: a candidate override is verified by equivalence to the default it replaces, and a carrier with no faster primitive correctly ships zero overrides.

[TRAIT_INHERITANCE_STACKS_THE_DISPATCH_SURFACE]:
- Dispatch surfaces compose by interface inheritance, not by re-declaration. The traversal trait inherits from both the map trait and the fold trait, so a single traversal-satisfying carrier already carries the full mapped surface and the full folded surface in addition to its one abstract traverse operation; the alternative trait inherits from the choice trait, the applicative trait, and the map trait, so satisfying it yields choice-folding, applicative-sequencing, mapping, and the parser-combinator suite at once. The richest dispatch surface a carrier can expose is selected by the most-derived trait it satisfies, and every ancestor surface arrives with it.
- The traverse abstract is the seam where the preserving and reducing modalities cross: the sequence operation derives from traverse with the identity arrow; the monadic traverse derives by routing the same arrow through the monadic free function; the default traverse derives in the other direction from map-then-sequence. One abstract traverse fixes the whole cross-modality family, and the carrier that supplies it inherits accumulate-versus-short-circuit selection without writing either path.

[LAZY_SECOND_ARGUMENT_IS_LOAD_BEARING_INFRASTRUCTURE]:
- The choice and apply primitives each ship a paired overload whose second argument is a deferred thunk rather than an evaluated carrier. The deferred overload is the termination mechanism for the recursive combinators, not an optimization knob. The one-or-more and zero-or-more parser combinators are mutually recursive local functions where the recursive arm is passed as the deferred thunk; an eager-only carrier forces the recursion at construction and overflows before parsing begins. Any custom alternative or applicative carrier that omits the deferred overload silently loses the entire repetition and separation surface that derives from it.

```csharp
static virtual K<F, Seq<A>> Some<A>(K<F, A> fa)
{
    return some();
    K<F, Seq<A>> many() => F.Choose(some(), F.Pure(Seq<A>()));
    K<F, Seq<A>> some() => Cached<A>.cons * fa * memoK(many);
}
```

- The combinator body reads as applicative algebra: the cons constructor lifted, applied across the element carrier, applied across the lazily-deferred remainder. The multiply operator is apply and the deferred remainder is the thunk overload; the alternative bar is choose. The whole repetition family is two operators and one memoised self-reference, and it terminates only because the second apply argument is deferred.

[ONE_GENERATED_TYPE_IS_THREE_DISPATCH_FORMS]:
- The frozen-table dispatch form, the case-owned delegate-row form, and the boundary-admission factory are not three constructs to choose among — they are one generated keyed type viewed through three of its static-abstract members. The same declaration that resolves a key to a case through the generated lookup table also sweeps every case through the all-items surface and admits a raw key through the validate operation; the keyed family additionally implements the bare-value factory and the keyed-value factory simultaneously, so the type that dispatches on a key is definitionally the type that admits one. The selection question "table or smart-enum or admission" dissolves: declaring the family yields all three surfaces, and a call site reaches for whichever static-abstract member its problem names.
- Because the family satisfies the same factory contract the boundary uses, the dispatch surface and the admission surface share one error payload and one validate signature. A plural admission that maps raw keys through the validate member and a bounded-vocabulary sweep that traverses the all-items member thread the same carrier, so admitting a batch of keys and dispatching over the resolved cases is one carrier-polymorphic pass rather than an admit step feeding a separate dispatch step.
- The key-to-case resolver is a generated lookup whose equality and ordering policy is fixed at the type level by a static-abstract singleton accessor named in the declaration, so the table's discrimination law is part of the dispatch form's identity, not a per-call configuration. Two families over the same key primitive with different declared comparers are different dispatch surfaces; the comparer choice is therefore a definition-time selection between dispatch forms, not a runtime argument the caller supplies.

[STATE_THREADING_IS_THE_DISPATCH_NOT_A_PARAMETER]:
- The generated total dispatch over a closed family threads one caller-supplied context value through every arm as a parameter whose name is fixed at the declaration site, not by any call site. Renaming that thread-through parameter is a single declaration-level control that rewrites the parameter in every generated dispatch overload at once. The context is therefore part of the dispatch surface's shape, not an ambient capture: shared state enters the dispatch as a threaded argument and the closed family is the only place it flows, which is exactly the form that keeps the arms allocation-free and the dispatch total.
- Coarse and fine dispatch coexist on one family without duplicating the family. A definition-time control emits an additional dispatch overload that treats a named set of cases as opaque atomic arms rather than descending into their substructure, so a caller chooses resolution granularity per call site by overload selection while both overloads target the same generated type. Granularity is a property of the dispatch overload, never a separate flattened family or a boolean depth flag.

[COMPOSITION_TIME_POLICY_CANNOT_REACH_INSIDE_GENERATED_DISPATCH]:
- Generated case dispatch executes synchronously and atomically at the call site, so a composition-time effect transformer wrapped around the dispatch sees the dispatch and all case behavior as one indivisible unit. Per-case retry, per-case timeout, or per-case recovery is therefore not expressible by wrapping the dispatch — the dispatch already collapsed to a value before any transformer runs. Per-case policy requires each arm to first produce an effectful carrier wrapped with its own policy, after which a traversal threads the already-wrapped arms; the granularity of the composition-time aspect is fixed by where the carrier is introduced, inside the per-case arm or around the assembled dispatch.
- This fixes the layering law between the two aspect kinds. Definition-time dispatch is a property of the type and is fully materialized before composition begins; composition-time policy is a property of a specific call site and attaches outside the materialized dispatch. A carrier that needs both — case-owned behavior and per-case effect policy — must lift each arm into the effect carrier at definition-adjacent code so the effectful arm is the value the dispatch produces, never a synchronous value the policy can only wrap whole.
